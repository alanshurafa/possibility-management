Agreement on Strikingly

You walk out of the house and meet a neighbor on the street. You say "Good morning." He replies "Good morning."
When did you agree to do it that way? When did you make the agreement to do it exactly that way? Why do you always greet him first? Is it because he is older? Do you agree with this way of doing things?
I've noticed something strange: when I'm walking down the street and people come towards me, I always say hello first. Always.

Without thinking about it (until now!). Even if they are complete strangers. The crazy thing is that there is some kind of irritation between the perfect stranger and me if I don't say "hello" first. It's like he and I made this agreement centuries ago, "Okay, if we happen to meet, I'll say hello first, you keep quiet until I do, is that clear?" "Ok."
The experiment goes like this: for 3 days, observe these unconscious agreements. Branch off 20 percent of your attention and watch the game from above.

Consider things like this:
How do you say hello to other people?
Who greets first? Is there eye contact?

Are you touching each other?

How far apart are your feet?

Do you hug each other? If so, for how long?

Who gives the impulse to stop hugging first?

What do you talk about then?
Have you made an agreement about it? Do you agree with the approach?

Do you need something different? What do you want to change?
(Learning spiral)

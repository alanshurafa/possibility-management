Assumptions on Strikingly

To do this experiment, you must have done Experiment 1 and/or 2 before, because you need a set of assumptions to put under the microscope.
Take the first assumption and write it down again. This assumption is a written down thought from your head.

What you think with is called thoughtware. It is similar to the program in a computer.

By changing the program, you get different results.

Break the assumption sentence into its parts and put them one by one under your attention magnifying glass.

Where do these parts come from?

What opinions, interpretations, promises, beliefs, attitudes, and declarations are in each component? What do you feel when you explore this?
For example, one of my assumptions is, "I assume there are happy marriages."
I put the word "marriage" under the microscope as a meme.

Why do I use the word? Where did I first hear it?

What emotions rise up in me? And most importantly, what assumptions are in turn inherent in the word "marriage"? In my case, the assumption that an administration can certify that two people will stay together for life.
You can also do this experiment together with a spaceholder. It might be helpful to have someone navigate the depths with you.

Emotions may also surface that can lead to Emotional Healing Processes.

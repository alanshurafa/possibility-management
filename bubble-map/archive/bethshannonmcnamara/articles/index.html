Beth Shannon McNamara on Strikingly

I walked into the kitchen and there was a very still lizard with no tail. On closer look, the place where the tail had dropped from, likely having to do with the cat, was severed and bloody, so I knew this creature had been through something moments before we met.

As I continue to research some of the programs (survival strategies) I picked up in my insular family unit, in church, in school, I keep finding a plethora of unconscious, sometimes slightly conscious ways, I’ve been creating repetitive experiences in my life. All the while defaulting to the “idea” that they are simply happening TO me, as if I was put on the planet to be a passive participant.

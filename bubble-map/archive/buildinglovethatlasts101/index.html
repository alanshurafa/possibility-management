Building Love That Lasts Study Group Week #103

EXPERIMENTS:

1. WHEN SHAME IS PRESENT, ASK YOURSELF OR OTHERS TO TELL YOU ABOUT THE FEAR, SADNESS, & ANGER THAT IS UNDERNEATH SHAME. Shame will not change the behavior, feeling the remorse, (sadness) will. Say, I am going to walk through the door with you to distinguish conscious remorse and understand how you created this. Behavior does not change until we feel the remorse. Let remorse get full blown and say, “I created this”. Then grieve it and this will transform you.

2. PUT A SPOTLIGHT ON SHAME. Make a gesture with your arms in a V above your head, guiding the spotlight on shame. Speak what you are ashamed of and have fun with it. The habit and heaviness of going to shame is gone. Playfulness Interrupts everything.

3. GATHER WITH A FEW PEOPLE & TAKE TURNS SPEAKING ABOUT THE SHAME PRESENT IN YOUR BODY. Notice the feelings of fear, anger and sadness that are underneath. Share what you are feeling and where it comes from. Ask others if they have experienced this or know others who have. When you speak about shame, it often melts away. We think we are alone in our shame, when most often others have experienced something similar.

4. STUDY THE MAP OF THREE KINDS OF LOVE. Ordinary love is where love is scarce, and contains lots of gremlin food which is dependent on certain circumstances or feelings. Extraordinary love is respectful, playful, and alive in the present moment. You source love happening in each moment so love cannot be missing. Archetypal love is where love is the most abundant thing in the universe. It is impersonal and not about you. You are not in each others bubble. http://evolutionofrelating.mystrikingly.com

6. USE WORDS TO PAINT AN EXPERIENCE INTO ANOTHER PERSONS BODY. Stay there and know this is experiential reality.

7. NOTICE THE SHIFT OF SPACE & THE GAPS IN-BETWEEN. Minimize your now so you can go through the gap between spaces. Practice shifting out of spaces you don’t want to be in, and into spaces you want to be in.http://navigatespace.mystrikingly.com When there is nothing in the gap, everything is possible.

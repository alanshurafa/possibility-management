Build Matrix on Strikingly

Expanded matrix gives you the possibility to navigate new territory of awareness. Modern culture has an extremely limited view on the possibilities of what is possible for a human life. The unique menu offered by modern culture is the linear life plan. The linear life plan goes something like this: you are born in a nucleus family, starting at age five years old you should go to school, and then to a different school, and then to university. You must have good grades, so you can have a good job after school. After exiting university, you get a job as an employee. You find a life-partner at university or at work. You get married, have children, a dog and a house outside the city. You change jobs or start your own business. You get divorce and change your life partner. After a few rounds of that, you get old enough to retire. You are then put in an old people’s home. You die. This is the average linear life plan.

Building matrix enables you to navigate new territory of awareness, including the territory of the nonlinear life plan. If you were not aware that there was a linear life plan then you could not have considered that a nonlinear life plan was possible. Marking a difference between a linear life plan and a nonlinear life plan is making a distinction. Making that particular distinction gives you more options to choose from, the more possibilities for your life.

A human life is a treasure hunt. We dedicated most of our time and energy in finding a treasure. Everybody’s treasure is different: love, happiness, deep friendship, money, a community, a purpose, making an impact in the world, … Whatever it is, it is what we want to achieve.

Logan LaPlant left school (and made a Tedx Talk about it, watch it below) because he realised he was not learning what he was supposed to learn to get to his treasure. If most people are looking for Happiness, how many classes in ‘Happiness’ did we have in school? he asked. How are we supposed to know how to get there? Why on earth are we learning about algebra and greco-roman history when what we really want to know is how to be happy?
How are we going to figure out how to get there? We need an accurate map of how to get from here to there.

Before Christopher Columbus went to find India crossing the Pacific, he did not have an accurate map of the world, the maps stopped at the contours of Europe. Christopher Columbus ended up in the Americas thinking he was in India. He did not get to his treasure because his map was not accurate enough. How many times have you wanted to reach a treasure and ended up finding yourself in a totally different place than where you want to be? Probably your inner map of that territory was not accurate enough to navigate yourself to your goal.

Expanding your matrix makes your inner map more accurate. Each new distinction that reorders your energetic body sheds light on new parts of the map that you didn’t know you didn’t know about. StartOver.xyz provides new maps for the known and unknown territories.

The new thoughtmaps comes out of a context that holds more consciousness than modern culture. The matrix of next culture has expanded way beyond that of modern culture.

Distinctions influence our lives whether we are aware of them or not. It is like gravity, whether you are aware of the sensation of gravity or whether you believe in gravity or not, it affects you whether you want it or not. Consciousness works in a similar way. The more distinctions you collect, the more leverage you have about circumstances. A new map opens new rules, new purposes, new possibilities and therefore new possible gameworlds that couldn’t be thought of before. Building new distinctions about gameworld gives you new territories to explore and new experiments to try in the domain of work, play and service.

No one can build matrix for you. And more interestingly, no one can stop you from building matrix.

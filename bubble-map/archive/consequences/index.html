Consequences on Strikingly

Everything is cause and effect, Buddha has already said. He did not say: Everything is cause or effect. He said: Everything is cause and everything is effect. If this is true, every effect is at the same time again the cause of a new effect. Or to put it prosaically:

Cause is effect is cause is effect is cause is effect is cause is effect is cause is effect is cause is effect....

Take a large sheet of paper. For example, flipchart paper is good. Put an X somewhere on the paper. There you are now. That you are here now is an effect.

What is the cause of this effect? Draw a line from this X in one direction to the 1st past station (1st PS) and make a few cues for yourself. The fact that you ended up at this past station was the effect of something else, perhaps even further in the past.

Draw a line there and call this new station 2nd past station (2nd PS). I know it's not that simple. What if it is?

The challenge in this experiment is to stay simple and clear. Think of it as a game. How far into the past can you or will you track your life in this way? My advice is to record at least 12 past stations. When you're done, look at it all again from the top. Each past station, the beginning, the X on the now, everything is cause and effect.

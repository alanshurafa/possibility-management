Golden Bridge Collective

A seed of an idea about living as a village where the children were raised and the people lived and worked together.

A pilot project was born at Tui community when Annika moved into the sleepout behind Evelyn, Hannah & Elliot as an experiment to see what living closer together looked like. Already there was more connection, collaboration with Evelyn and cooking and eating together.

The seed took further root after Annika decided to leave Tui Community. This decision was closely followed by Hannah and Elliot who decided to combine forces with Annika to form a 3-cell and start meeting to talk about how we might live together elsewhere.

Sanne’s curiosity was piqued and she asked to join the group. At first it was a no because of fear to expand and become more complex too quickly. After another meeting, and another enquiry from Sanne (persistence can pay off), a yes was found and the 3-cell became a 4-cell.

Most of us in the 4-cell then had the impulse separately to invite a fifth member who was looking for somewhere to live and had just completed the kaimahi program at Tui. Adrian joined the group, fresh into Possibility Management and with an eagerness to attend his first Expand the Box training at the beginning of May.

We started meeting regularly and writing a codex, sometimes dining together too. The day after we put an advertisement out for accommodation, we received a response and organised a visit. All of us had a yes for the space, one of us without even seeing it.

We came together again and asked ourselves if we were crazy to jump into this so quickly without a full codex or clear purpose for the space. Energetically we all had a YES to this project and we decided to go for it and gave a yes to the first property we visited in Pōhara. A bit of a clean slate without much maintenance; perfect for focusing on the project and relating amongst ourselves.

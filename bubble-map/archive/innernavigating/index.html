Inner Navigating on Strikingly

Your physical body consists of parts. Physical parts. Head, chest, pelvis, hand. Parts are also the individual organs like the heart, the liver, the lungs.
Just as there is a navigation system in our car, there is one in our body. Everybody has it, it works complexly and precisely. It helps you to sit exactly on the right place of the chair, so that the chair does not tilt and you sit comfortably. This inner navigation device also helps you pick your nose at night. Even if it's pitch black in the room, your index finger will find the nostril. Without this navigator in our bodies, we would trip, injure ourselves, and could never learn such complex activities as swallowing, walking, or talking.
Here comes the experiment:
Lie on your back, a yoga mat or your bed works well. Close your eyes and let your attention wander to the inside of your chest. There is something like the exact center, navigate exactly to this point. Try to find this center to the millimeter.
How do you know you have found the center?
There is also an exact center point in the head. Navigate to this center point as well.
Finally, navigate to the exact center of your belly.

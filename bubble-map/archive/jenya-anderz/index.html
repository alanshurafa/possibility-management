Were you ever taught how to navigate your emotions in a healthy, conscious way? If you grew up in modern culture, the answer is probably NO. Schools don’t teach emotional empowerment, and many of us were raised with messages like 'boys don’t cry' or 'good girls don’t get angry'. As a result, we learn to suppress, ignore, or fear our feelings instead of using them as the powerful tools they are.

The Hidden Cost of Emotional Suppression

To cope with this lack of emotional empowerment, you might have developed different strategies:

🔹 Making yourself numb, shutting down emotions entirely to avoid 'discomfort'.

🔹 Going into 'overwhelm', swinging between emotional explosions (unconscious rage outburst, or panic attack) or implosions (self-blame, isolation, depression).

When emotions become too intense or confusing, you may unconsciously turn to distractions—social media, overworking, emotional eating, excessive meditation, alcohol, sex, or endless complaining—to avoid truly 'feeling'.

Ignoring your feelings comes at a cost. You may find yourself:

Isolating yourself when emotions become too intense.

Reacting impulsively, damaging your relationships and self-trust.

Feeling disconnected from your true self, stuck in patterns that don’t serve you.

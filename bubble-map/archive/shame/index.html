    CONTROVERSY ABOUT SHAME

    Overall, in modern culture, there is confusion about Shame.

    Various contexts view Shame in a different light. Whereas a religion might view Shame, and its related sensation 'guilt', as a way to control or subdue their congregation and force them into a particular penance, psychology might use Shame as a reason to prescribe brain drugs, yet still another context may view the experience of 'Shame' as a 'fifth feeling' in addition to anger, sadness, fear, and joy. In Japanese Bushido, if you 'lose face' and acquire enough Shame, it was expected that you should commit suicide through Harakiri, or Seppuku, so that the Shame ends with you and does not reflect on your 'superiors'.

    Possibility Management's empirical research since 1975 relates to Shame as a 'Mixed Emotion', the sensations that arise when you blend together certain proportions of the Emotion of anger plust the Emotion of sadness plus the Emotion of fear.

    There are several possibly unfamiliar Distinctions contained in the previous sentence.

    THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN FEELINGS AND EMOTIONS

    As explained in the upgraded thoughtmap of Feelings used in Possibility Management, there is a radical difference between a 'Feeling' and an 'Emotion'.

    A Feeling is not a thought.

    Thoughts mostly occur in your mind, your 'intellectual body', which - in the Possibility Management world - is one of your 5 Bodies: physical, intellectual, emotional, energetic, and archetypal.

    Feelings and Emotions occur in your Emotional Body, centered around your physical heart.

    Possibility Management distinguishes four Feelings (Conscious Anger (archived — not captured), Conscious Sadness, Conscious Fear, and Conscious Joy).

    Possibility Management distinguishes many Emotions (Emotional Anger, Emotional Sadness, Emotional Fear, and Emotional Joy, plus all the varieties of Mixed Emotions, where two, three, or all four of the Emotions are mixed together in various proportions to create an entire palette of Mixed Emotional sensations).

    The Feeling of anger, for example, feels the same as the Emotion of anger, at the beginning. How can you tell the difference if the anger (or any other feeling) is a Feeling or an Emotion?

    It is very simple to detect the difference between Feelings and Emotions:

    • If the sensations of anger (or sadness, or fear, or joy) come up, you apply its energy and intelligence, and the sensations of anger vanish from your body in less than 3 minutes, then it is a Feeling.
    • If the sensations of anger (or sadness, or fear, or joy, or any of the Mixed Emotions) come up and last longer than 3 minutes, or if you have felt the same emotion about the same thing before, then it is not a Feeling. It is an Emotion.

    Neither Feelings nor Emotions are good nor bad, right nor wrong, positive nor negative. Feelings and Emotions each server their own Purpose.

    • Feelings are for handling things.
    • Emotions are for healing things.

    SHAME IS A MIXED EMOTION

    Shame is a Mixed Emotion, created inside you by mixing together anger, sadness, and fear.

    If you ever want to experience Shame, then mix anger, sadness, and fear together as one experience in your heart.

    Shame includes anger, for example, anger that there is any kind of social pressure for not doing or being what you want to do or be, anger that you were caught doing something that has been defined as bad, or evil, or forbidden, or 'inappropriate', anger that anyone would care enough to be regulating you about this, anger that you could not protect yourself from being discovered.

    Shame includes sadness, for example, sadness that you do not get to be yourself, sadness that you might have to stop your secret pleasures, sadness that there is no room for your authentic self in this world, sadness that a future you once wished to have for yourself is lost.

    Shame includes fear, for example, that you might be punished, fear that others may find out about what you did or what you secretly want to do and your reputation or credibility will be lost, fear that what you have been hiding has been discovered and might be taken away from you, fear that you will not be able to stop and could be killed for this.

    If you ever DO NOT want to experience Shame, then Unmix your Emotions of anger, sadness, and fear.

    Full instructions and demonstrations of Unmixing Emotions are online at https://unmixemotions.mystrikingly.com. After Unmixing your Emotions of anger, sadness, and fear, your Shame will be rather instantaneously gone.

    But after Unmixing your Emotions you will still have your Emotions. You will have strong Emotions of Anger, strong Emotions of Sadness, and strong Emotions of Fear. The benefit is that now your Emotions are Unmixed. Now you can use them for what they were designed for. Each of these three Emotions is a precious Doorway to a new or deeper Emotional Healing Process (EHP).

    RESEARCHING SHAME

    By Joseph Goldin shared in the US Possibility Village Telegram group 20 March 2025.

    Dear Village, I share my research on Shame with you all.

    I am discovering my shame. I am discovering its characteristics. When I feel shame it is hard to look into the eye of another person. When I reveal my shame I feel as though I can break. That I am glass that could shatter. I become like a little boy looking for someone to hold me and love me and tell me it’s ok.

    In my shame is sadness and a sense that there is something wrong with me. There is anger to try and hide myself from the world and from others and fear to be seen. I want to be alone and yet I am lonely and I want to be seen. When I do look into someones eyes and reveal my shame there is some gladness.

    I don’t want people to see me and how wrong I am, that there is something wrong with me. That I am different. I feel shame to be a man. When I touch my face and hold myself I don’t know whose hair is on my face. When I look at my face I do not know who I am. I do not like what I see. Looking at myself I am scared.

    Many times this shame is with me under the surface. I try and hide it and yet it finds the cracks and seeps through. It manifests in me as self abuse or as a challenge to make eye contact and be present with others. It becomes a wall separating me.

    The more I push away my shame and sadness the more the panic grows. The core of the panic is there is something wrong. There is something wrong with me. There is something wrong with me and I cannot tell anyone, even myself. The more I push it away the stronger the panic gets and the more lonely I get. Then I want to hide in a cave and disappear, drown myself in fantasy books or even the bliss of nothingness yet the panic is there with me.

    I did not know I felt shame. I remember a few years ago saying that is not something I experience. Now I know, I know and I am glad to know. During the feelings Core Lap and FP Deepening lab we did a lot with shame. I learned shame is a mixed emotion and yet has different doorways for healing, different than unmixing. Another doorway for shame is to be seen and heard. To be in front of another person and to show my shame. To maybe even look into their eyes and speak it. To let myself cry, to say what it is I am ashamed about. To speak out how I feel fragile like I can break and even that I want someone to hold me and tell me it’s ok. This has been my unfolding with shame. I sense there is so much more to unfold. I am glad to be uncovering this in me. Im glad to share my research on shame with you.

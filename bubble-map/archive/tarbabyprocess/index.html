Tar Baby Process on Strikingly

The Tar-Baby is the second of the Uncle Remus stories published in 1881 by Joel Chandler Harris, and popularized in Song of the South, the 1946 Disney movie. Other versions of the story, however, have surfaced throughout the world, including Nigeria, Brazil, Corsica, Jamaica, India, and the Philippines. The folk-tale is about a decoy doll made of tar and turpentine used by the villainous Br'er Fox to entrap Br'er Rabbit. The more that Br'er Rabbit fights the Tar-Baby, the more entangled he becomes.

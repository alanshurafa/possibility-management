Trainer Path on Strikingly

(revised 12 November 2024)

NOTE: The following letter is the most recent in a multi-year series of invitations and boundaries, distinctions and possibilities exchanged amongst the group of people that used to be referred to as the 'Possibility Management Trainer Guild'.

Possibility Management is an evolutionary gameworld. This means that part of the nonmaterial value created and delivered by Possibility Management is personal and organizational evolution, also referred to as transformation. What we as a gameworld have been learning is that evolutionary gameworlds must also evolve, transformational gameworlds must also transform.

The Possibility Management gameworld is currently (October - December 2024) in an evolutionary Liquid State. The following letter is written by Vera L. Franco, a Possibility Management Expand The Box and Possibility Lab Trainer, Gremlin Transformation Coach, a Women Of Earth spaceholder, and a global gameworld Village Weaver. Vera has been a spaceholder of and actively cocreating the Trainer Path trainings and calls since before Trainer Path began. She has been on the Possibility Management Infinity Ring in various nodes since our Infinity Ring was formed. It is legitimate that she spontaneously wrote this letter.

Dear everyone,

I am speaking on my personal behalf, not as a representative of the Trainer Path. I am now starting a series of back to back ETBs and a Lab ending in the first week of December so I will be off communication for a while. And I have some time now as I am on the three hour drive up to the training space in New York.

I write to you with fear, anger and sadness. I have been crying, screaming, yelling about this change in the Trainer Path and Trainer Guild gameworld, and the heartbreaks that are contained in it. I want to tell you where I personally experience myself now and what I now see.

I feel fear that some of you who have been in the original Trainer Guild group do not comprehend how grave it is for the Possibility Management Trainers to NOT be operating in the same context.

This is literally one of the first maps given in an Expand The Box training (ETB): the Thoughtmap of Culture and Responsibility. The distinction is very clear: Different contexts (levels of responsibility) create different cultures.

This means that since perhaps 2012, so-called PM Trainers are not playing the same game. We are simply not. One team is playing soccer, another team is playing volleyball, and another team is playing polo. Yes, they all use balls, they all use two teams, they all use nets, but they do not play the same game.

The people now engaged in this PM gameworld conversation are not creating the same Possibility Management.

And if after our calls and emails, you do not feel anger or fear about calling volleyball, polo, and soccer the same name then I cannot call you a Possibility Manager at this point, nevermind a Trainer. Or you have some block in the way of your own Stand.

I say these exact words in every ETB while delivering the Thoughtmap of Culture and Responsibility. This is not a fantasy world. If anyone here has doubts about the relation between cultural context and its level of responsibility, think about how Possibility Management has been evolving in the last five years, building a vibrant global village with new Possibilitators, new gameworlds, new processes, new thoughtmaps, new distinctions, new teams, new traditions, and then think about in which ways you have been removing yourself from it.

And again, I am not blaming you for removing yourself from it. It is your choice. What you choose is not bad, or evil, or dumb. You simply made some steps in another direction along the way every day for the past five years.

Some people in this group did not. It was also their choice.

Personally if a group of Trainers came together and told me that there was a way for me to evolve my work and my practice and my being because they had discovered amazing new practices and processes, as a precessional I would be one of the first to say "What is my next step in this?" I did that with becoming a Trainer before there was a Trainer Path, and did it within the Trainer Path, and it is the same guidance that I take which led me to go to my first Feelings Practitioner training. It is the same desire that I have within me to book myself one day soon in the Peaceful Warrior training too.

Conversations and negotiations cannot proceed if there is no agreed upon distinction of Context (Levels of Responsibility). Even the thoughtmaps of Possibility Management can be interpreted completely differently in different levels of responsibility. I have seen it time and time again in communities, when there is a mixed context, the only possible playing field between all players is at the lowest common level of responsibility.

I am not willing to do that. I have recently been in conferences, gatherings, summits, and communities where I cannot play full out with others because people are committed to different levels of disempowerment. I have tried to 'soften my words', 'make a bridge', 'be more understanding', 'be more of a translator'. It kills me on the inside. I can actually experience the clarity of my Bright Principles become less sharp, my intuition becoming less impersonal, less Archetypal. I can sense an ever-so-slight-dullness in me when I suppress the clarity of my Bright Principles fit someone else’s 'emotional sensitivities'.

I have worked too hard and long to jack-in to my Archetypal Lineage for me to not let it speak loud and clear, and fiercely so.

I wish that every single person on each of the calls we made together would have enthusiastically said, "I want to join the Trainer Path!" I am recovering from my own shock and heartbreak that this did not happen. I want the Trainer Path to thrive in diversity, but within the same context.

Maybe I need an EHP, and I ask my team of Trainers to call me out and hold space for me if they see that, because I play in a Trainer culture game where I celebrate that I can take a risk and get feedback from my team so that I can become a clearer and simpler space through which my Bright Principles can speak in the world. But I will not stand for a pretend conversation that we are playing the same game.

I feel sad that this conversation did not start years ago. I feel even sadder that it was someone outside the Trainer Path who noticed our mixed context and made a move for clarity and integrity in our team.

I want to be very careful about what Possibility Management promotes in the world. If players of polo, volleyball, and football are all called 'Trainers', then there already exist hundreds of 'PM Trainers' delivering Rage Clubs, Fear Clubs, Rage Club Spaceholder Trainings, WorkTalks, Workshops, etc. Then we make no distinction between any spaceholder who uses PM Tools and thoughtmaps regardless of their daily-life context. This would be telling a lie to the world about who we are.

I have personally been and still am in a massive Liquid State about this evolutionary PM gameworld change. I have questions like:

  • "If it is not Radical Responsibility, can it even be called Possibility Management, seeing that PM comes from radically responsible thoughtware?"
  • "What about Archiarchy? I want to build Archiarchal cultures all over the world. But who would inhabit them if even in the Trainer Guild there isn't a common context?"
  • "Can Possibility Management also hold other contexts? Where is the line?"

I am still discovering this for myself and with other people that are caring for Possibility Management at a Gameworld Building level. This is why, in my view, these changes happen in steps. The Trainer Guild has been having this same conversation for several years now already. Right now it is time to be with the full impact of what it means that we are not playing the same game. I want to shout loud to each one of us that the Trainer Guild is dead! This is the impact of a mixed context. The Trainer Guild is dead.

Until very recently the Trainer Guild was assumed to be in the same context. Back then there was a distinction of calling someone a 'Trainer' and calling someone something else. Back then a Trainer was called a 'Trainer' because they would deliver ETBs and Labs contexted in Radical Responsibility. But if the Trainer Guild is truly defined as a group of Possibility Management Trainers who are contexted in Radical Responsibility, delivering Expand The Box Trainings and Possibility Labs, then we do not fit that definition anymore. Then there is no more Trainer Guild.

This is not what I wish. I don't wish the Trainer Guild to be over, generalized into contextual mush, accepting anyone and anything. But the Trainer Guild was over when different Trainers started contexting themselves differently from each other. Which means that the Trainer Guild has been dead a long time. I think deep down you sense that too. I feel sad about it. I feel angry about it. I feel fear about it. But by looking directly at this mess, also something else can be born instead, and I feel joy about that.

There has been the assumption that we are using a common website. Common to what? There is no common website anymore. Its commonality ceased to exist from the moment that we discovered that we do not play the same games.

A new commonality between different contexts needs to be discovered, not assumed. It would be a lie to the world to let things remain as they are, to give everything the same name, and even call ourselves the same names or titles. If there is a need to discover the commonality of context between each of us, let the people who want that start that, not because of a ‘right’ over a website, but because of a willingness to be in collaborative communication, collaboration, and co-empowerment. Because without it there is no relationship.

The intention of the next steps of the Trainer Path in regards to the website is to give enough time to gain more clarity about how to communicate the diversity within Possibility Management. Already now there are hundreds of Possibilitators all over the world offering spaces using the tools, distinctions, thoughtmaps and processes of PM, contexted in radical responsibility, not just the people in this email group.

But most importantly for this step is that there is a clear need for guardianship about what offers would go into the possibilitymanagement.org website, and what offers do not. Is it all the offers of anyone using the 4 Feelings Thoughtmap even if they context it in Tantra, Emotional Management, Improvisational Dance, or somatic experiencing? How do we make sure that there is quality in the offering and alignment with the context of Radical Responsibility and the building of Archiarchy? These questions will need clear communications and negotiation with the guardianship team, otherwise we will have a big mess on our hands.

How Possibility Management presents itself in the Possibility Management calendar website must be in integrity with its inside, and Possibility Management has evolved. It is not the same as it was even one year ago.

In heartbreak, and also in clarity.

I’m here.

Vera.

LEGENDS - Villaging Bridgehouse

Because the legends are so abundant, US Tour event #2 Rage Club
@dawaychouren, @hannahhirsh, and I held a Rage Club at a land based community called Earth Dance Thursday night. Joseph took a stand for this community to experience something different with their feelings and for sharing the transformational potential of people using their feelings as information for them, not to change others. His stand filled the room. He brought so many people together both from the community and people from different corners who’d never met before.
Right from the start, I noticed this space had a special magic. The community was ready for us and welcomed us fully. It seemed as if the trees surrounding us even knew Rage Club was about to happen.
While we set up people slowly emerged from the trees on foot, not their cars. A parent and two children came into the space as we set up and grabbed padded sticks from the supply closet. They told us, “we gonna have a Jr. Rage Club in the other room.”

I felt so much joy being in this space and I quickly discovered it was because this wasn’t an Introduction to Rage Club to inspire individuals, but for a community and the community-being that was present in the space. It was a villaging Rage Club and possibility for people to continue these practices together was palpable.

Coming from the Villaging Bridgehouse, I also got to step into this space from a new place.

One of my fears holding this Rage Club with @dawaychouren and @hannahhirsh was that I had no idea how to hold a space with 3 people. I was scared that I wouldn’t be able to be in contact with both Daway and Hannah and elegantly navigate the space with them. I spoke about my fears with @AnaMariaDiazTirado and she shared a possibility of having space for us 3 to radically relate before hand. She shared that we could set the space from our relating and then participants simply step into that space. I was a big yes for this.
So early in the day Hannah, Daway, and I set aside time to relate, not to plan or talk about the space, but to be vulnerable together. My reaction was really something like, how have I never done this before?! I loved it.

I got to go slow, be with their beings and open my heart to their archetypal lineages. I noticed what was in the way of me collaborating and seeing their archetypal bodies and I shared these things with each of them. When I looked into the space between us there was a tender vulnerability and the sense that the space between us could hold anything.

This space did expand to include the participants and the quality was new to me, as if the air had a different viscosity and I was connected to Daway and Hannah as a team. At one point in the beginning of Rage Club I let my emotional fear connected to getting my first impulses out that I narrowed my awareness in the space and completely missed that no one was activating anger in their lower bodies. I had let my emotional fear cloud my presence and experiential reality but even then the space held by our relating was still there. When I moved around the emotional fear, I was seamlessly able to return to the collaboration with them and back to that relating space, it was resilient!

I celebrate this new discovery of holding space first from a space of radical relating and how much fun it was to be in this dynamic play together. I want to keep experimenting with starting this way.
Love,
Meredith

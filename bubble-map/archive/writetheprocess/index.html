Write The Process on Strikingly

Your Vision is clear: write the next article, the same way anyone writes a transformational article. That is, die first. Think later.

What is an article not?

An article is not a Tweet. A Tweet is your ego's cleverness cutely compressed into 256 characters.

An article is not a Blog. A Blog is your mind farting opinions - people like smelling their own farts.

An article is not a FaceBook Comment. A FaceBook Comment is a political maneuver trying to attract more FaceBook Friends.

An article is not a Diary Entry. A Diary Entry is your private underwear. Showing your private underwear attracts abusers and perverts.

An article is not a Letter of Complaint. A Letter of Complaint is admitting your own powerlessness, thinking your persecutors will come save you.

An article is a memetic experience for the reader where you declare transformational war against unconsciousness through presenting designs for new kinds of flying machines the reader can test fly.

HERE ARE SOME FURTHER ARTICLE-WRITING HINTS:

Refocus away from trying to fight current beliefs, understandings, concepts and constructs. Why? Because if by some miracle you ever succeeded in destroying the status quo, your readers would have to hate you.

Readers would have to hate you because you caused their world to collapse into the void. Most readers are not equipped to enjoy groundlessness. If you kill their current concepts, you may as well kill them. Instead, give them a life boat even before they realize their own boat is sinking.

Your article is a safe-haven, a bridge over the bottomless pit, a sideways elevator, a secret escape passage through impenetrable walls into a transformed cultural context in which it is okay for the reader to be a bowl of jelly, utterly liquid and confused for the weeks it might take them to start over from zero and become a revised human Being, functional in the new worldview.

Avoid writing from the viewpoint that you are a victim, a small powerless creature trying to complain your way to power, trying to undermine or be a nuisance, trying to threaten or scare others to manipulate them to figure out how to shift into a regenerative future for life on Earth.

If you write towards your enemies like pointing a sniper rifle at their head they cannot be adaptive to your pressures. They are stuck in their hell-world and they must defend it with their life. They know nothing else. Have relentless compassion for their helpless mechanicality.

Buckminster Fuller's quote is no longer powerful. He said, "You never change things by fighting against the existing reality. To change something, build a new model that makes the old model obsolete."

Existing systems in modern culture are already obsolete.

Modern culture is exterminating life on planet Earth at the fastest possible rate. The sixth great extinction is massively underway. Stop being angry about how bad it is. Use your anger to build out new possibility space for people to occupy.

Build it and they will come.

If you don't build it, no one even has a chance of coming.

You have a part to play or you would not be reading this. You have a unique perspective or you would not have carved a feather into a pen to write with.

The rule of law of western civilization is over. Anyone following the rule of law of western civilization is criminally insane. Anyone enforcing the rule of law of western civilization has already forfeited their life.

What does this mean? It means that your writing can be bolder than you might possibly have heretofore dared to imagine.

Your writing needs to be so daring that you shit in your pants to write it.

Your writing delivers elements that build out new territory, that says things not understandable through commonly accepted ways of thinking, that drills new grooves into readers' thought patterns.

If you fail to unleash a torrent of wildness and delusion-crushing imperatives you leave people behind. You abandon them to the terrible grips of a thought-monster rapidly devouring their last glimmers of self-respect.

People think their freedoms are being limited by external authority, by the law, by police. Then they can feel righteously outraged at the 'enemy' and fight against them.

But you know this is not true. Think about it this way. What if the complainers win and take over? Who would they complain about then? People's freedoms cannot be limited by external authority. People's authority is imprisoned by walls inside of their memetic construct defining who they cannot be, what they cannot do. Your article uses memes to paint doorways through their stingy, frightened conclusions into a bigger, brighter, consciously responsible and loving interconnectedness.

Your article is not about trying to find readers who are angry about the same things you are angry about but don't know what to do with their lives. These are dropouts. There will always be dropouts, rousing the rabble. If you bring the dropouts into your Rebel Unschool they will be dropouts there too. You think people change? (If so, you should enter the Trainer Path.)

If you try to find readers who might be interested in what you are interested in, you are giving your own authority away. Write freely. Light the fuse to your internal dynamite. Blow yourself up all over the page. Explode your inhibitions to claim and occupy new space. Let your fingers surprise your soul.

When the assumption walls stop crumbling, when the dust of dynamited conclusions begins to settle, lift your eyes and look around. See who is still with you. These are your allies.

These are the ones who are not 'possibly interested' for a few moments of distracting entertainment. Dilettantes exited the scene the instant they heard the fuse hissing towards the TNT.

The readers still with you, dusted in fragments of their previously imprisoning paradigms... these are the committed ones. These are your true partners in evolutionary adventure. Write your heart out to them. Rally them to write to others with you.

# Learning Self-Assessment

| | |
|---|---|
| **For** | The learner (filled by you, for you), held lightly by the CM in anonymized aggregate |
| **When** | Part 1 at enrollment and again after Day 10 · Part 2 before each High module (Days 5, 6, 7, 9) |
| **What it is** | A self-verification instrument. Not a quiz. Not scored. No badge. No pass, no fail. |
| **Reads alongside** | `01 - Course Architecture and Pedagogy.md` (the learning outcomes this restates) · `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` §L (how the aggregate feeds the post-cohort review) |

PM is verified in lived experience, not multiple choice (Doc 02 §J). This instrument matches that. Every statement here is something you check against **your own direct experience** — the course's core instruction is to verify each distinction in your own body, not to take anyone's word for it (Doc 01, pedagogical premise §5). So this is not a test you pass. It is a mirror you look into, twice, and notice what moved.

**There is no score.** You will not add anything up. The *delta*, what shifted between the start and the end, is the only signal, and it is a signal for you first. A distinction that is "not yet" at Day 10 is not a failure; it is information about where your direct experience hasn't landed it yet, which is exactly the kind of honesty the work runs on.

**The rating scale (three words, used everywhere in this file):**

- **not yet:** I can't locate this in my direct experience. Maybe I understand the idea, but I don't *have* it in my body.
- **starting:** I catch glimpses. Sometimes, in some moments, I can find it. It's not reliable yet.
- **landed in my body:** I can find this in my direct experience, more or less on purpose. It's mine, not borrowed.

A note on "landed in my body": this is not "I understand it intellectually." The whole course is built against the trap of swallowing PM into "interesting ideas I now know about" (Doc 01, five-body method). If you can explain a distinction cleanly but can't *feel* it or *use* it in a live moment, that's **starting**, not landed. Be honest in the direction of "not yet." The honesty is the practice.

## Part 1 — Enrollment baseline and Day-10 re-rate

Rate each statement twice: once now, at the start, and once after Day 10. Don't look at your start answers when you re-rate — answer fresh, then compare. These eleven statements restate the course's per-day learning outcomes (Doc 01, the learning arc) in the first person.

Mark one box per column per row.

| # | Statement (rate against your direct experience) | Start: not yet / starting / landed | Day 10: not yet / starting / landed |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **(Day 1: context, radical responsibility)** I can catch the moment I'm about to react from my old default, and choose a different *context* to act from instead, not just a different behavior. | ☐ ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ ☐ |
| 2 | **(Day 2: Box, thoughtware)** I can notice my Box make a move in real time — a survival reaction running, and see it as the Box, not as *me*. | ☐ ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ ☐ |
| 3 | **(Day 3: liquid state, center/ground/bubble, five bodies)** I can center, ground, and set a bubble on purpose, and feel the shift when my Box loosens into liquid state. | ☐ ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ ☐ |
| 4 | **(Day 4: feedback, experiments)** I can take a piece of feedback without defending, and turn it into a small specific experiment I actually run in my life. | ☐ ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ ☐ |
| 5 | **(Day 5: feelings vs emotions, Numbness Bar)** I can tell a clean *feeling* (present, in my body, no story) from an *emotion* (about something, with a story attached), and I can locate a small amount of a feeling (5%) without becoming it. | ☐ ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ ☐ |
| 6 | **(Day 6: mixed emotions, emotional healing process)** I can notice when more than one feeling is present at once, and let a feeling move through rather than managing it away. | ☐ ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ ☐ |

| 8 | **(Day 8: listening, speaking, completion loops)** I can listen to someone without fixing, advising, or planning my reply, and I can close a conversation cleanly instead of leaving it open and leaking. | ☐ ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ ☐ |
| 9 | **(Day 9: ego states, problem ownership)** I can tell which part of me is speaking (Parent, Child, Adult, Gremlin) and choose to come back to Adult instead of collapsing into one of the others. | ☐ ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ ☐ |
| 10 | **(Day 10: bright principles, three powers)** I can declare something and stand on it (rather than wish or intend it), and I can tell when I'm sourcing from a bright principle versus from fear. | ☐ ☐ ☐ | ☐ ☐ ☐ |

### Reading your own delta (for you, no one grades this)

After Day 10, lay the two columns side by side. You are looking for *movement*, not for a row of "landed."

- **Rows that moved one or two steps:** that's the thoughtware installing. That's the course working as designed.
- **Rows still at "not yet":** not a failure. Either that distinction needs more lived reps after the cohort (the work continues in your own life, per Doc 01), or it's a distinction your direct experience genuinely hasn't met yet. Both are honest. Name which it is.
- **A row that went *backward*** (landed → starting): this happens and it's often a *good* sign: you knew less than you thought at the start, and now you can feel the edge of what you actually have. PM calls this kind of honesty an upgrade, not a regression.
- **The biggest mover:** notice it. That's where this cohort did its clearest work in you.

Nothing here goes to anyone with your name on it. If you choose to share your deltas with the cohort, that's the Day 10 closure work (Playbook §8), not this form.

## Part 2 — Per-HIGH-module readiness self-check

Before each High module (Days 5, 6, 7, 9), answer a short stem honestly, *to yourself*, before you start. This is not the safety unlock checklist (that's Doc 04 §D: partner reachable, 90 minutes, not in crisis; your CM gates that separately). This is a *learning*-readiness check: does the equipment the next module depends on actually exist in your direct experience yet?

The questions cascade on purpose. Each High module assumes the prior distinctions are at least partly installed (Doc 01, "how distinctions cascade"). If you answer "not yet" to a foundation question, that's not a stop sign. It is a signal to revisit the earlier module's practice, slow your cadence, or take it to your partner before you go in. The honest "not yet" here is what catches a gap *before* the module instead of during it.

Rate each line **not yet / starting / landed in my body**, then read the note under each block.

### Before Day 5 — Feelings vs Emotions

- Can I find my center and ground on purpose? (Day 3 equipment)
- Can I tell a *feeling* (present, in my body) from an *emotion* (about something, story attached)?
- Can I locate a small amount (5%) of a feeling in my body without becoming it?
- Can I notice when I've hit my Numbness Bar (the point where I go flat instead of feeling)?

> If "can I ground on purpose" is **not yet**, go back to the Day 3 practice before Day 5: the emotional-body work stands on that equipment (Doc 01, "why embodiment before feelings"). If telling feeling from emotion is **not yet**, that's fine. Day 5 is where you build it; just go in knowing that's the distinction you're there to meet, not one you should already have.

### Before Day 6 — Mixed Emotions and Emotional Healing Process

- Can I name and locate at least one clean feeling in my body? (Day 5)
- Can I let a feeling move through me for a few breaths without managing it away or telling its story?
- Can I notice when more than one feeling is present at the same time?
- Do I know how to ground and pause if a feeling becomes more than I can hold right now? (Doc 03 §D, §E)

> Day 6 is the most demanding module in the course. If locating a single feeling is still **not yet**, you are not ready for *mixed* feelings yet. Revisit Day 5, and tell your partner and CM you're stretching the cadence here. There is no prize for rushing this one. The grounding-and-pause question is the one to be most honest about: if the answer isn't a solid "landed," re-read Doc 03 §D before you start.

### Before Day 7 — Low Drama and Gremlin Food

- Can I feel my own fear, anger, sadness, or joy as energies, rather than only thinking about them? (Days 5–6)
- Can I catch myself *avoiding* a clean feeling by getting busy, blaming, rescuing, or telling a story?
- Can I name what "gremlin food" is for me: the payoff I get from drama, righteousness, or suffering?

> Low Drama is, mechanically, a way of avoiding your own feelings by routing them through someone else (Doc 01, "why emotional before relational"). If feeling your own feelings as energies is **not yet**, you'll likely *see* the Drama Triangle as a concept but not *catch* it in yourself, which is okay for a first pass. Going in, the move to watch for is the avoidance, not the role-name.

### Before Day 9 — Ego States, Problem Ownership

- Can I hold the distinction that my Box is not me, and my Gremlin is not me, that I *have* both and am *neither*? (Day 2, Day 7)
- Can I tell, in a live moment, roughly which part of me is running things?
- Can I come back to Adult (centered, present, choosing) when I notice I've dropped into reaction?

> Ego states (Parent, Child, Adult, Gremlin, Demon) only work if you can already hold Box-vs-Being-vs-Gremlin (Doc 01, distinction 7). If "my Box is not me" is **not yet**, revisit Day 2 and Day 7 before Day 9; otherwise the ego-state map becomes five new labels instead of a live tool. This is also the module where archetypal material runs strongest; the readiness here is as much about being grounded as about being clever.

### How to use a "not yet" on these

A "not yet" on a foundation question is the instrument doing its job: it caught a gap before the module instead of during it. Your move, in order of preference:

1. **Revisit the prior module's embodied practice** (the Solo Centering and Grounding Scripts and the relevant Day file). Often one more rep is all it takes.
2. **Take it to your partner** as your next exchange — speak the edge you're on out loud and be witnessed (this is what the pairing is for).
3. **Stretch your cadence** for the upcoming module (Doc 02 §I). There is no schedule the work has to keep.
4. **Flag it to your CM** if the gap feels bigger than a slow-down can close. Not because you're in trouble, but because the CM can point you to the right resource (and because a repeated readiness gap across the cohort tells the CM something about the module, see below).

None of these is failing. All of them are the course working the way it's meant to: you, verifying in your own direct experience, choosing your own pace.

## Part 3 — The anonymized collection flow (operator + CM steps)

Parts 1 and 2 are the learner's mirror. This part is the plumbing that turns the cohort's mirrors into a no-name aggregate without ever attaching a rating to a person. The mechanism matters: a "we promise we won't look" collection is not anonymization. The flow below makes the promise structural.

### The submission mechanism (no-name by construction)

The learner transcribes three things from their own filled copy (never the copy itself, which stays with them):

1. The two columns of Part 1 (start rating and Day-10 rating, eleven rows).
2. Their Part 2 "not yet" answers, by module (which foundation questions were "not yet" before which High module).
3. Optionally: which row was their biggest mover, and one line of comment.

Two collection channels. The operator offers at least one; offering both raises the return rate:

- **Anonymous form.** A web form with **no login, no name field, no email field**, reachable by a single link shared with the whole cohort (so the link itself identifies no one). The form tool must meet the platform privacy minimums (Doc 04 §H) and must be verified, before the window opens, not to log IP addresses or require an account. If the operator's stack cannot meet that bar, use the paper channel instead.
- **Printed drop.** The learner prints or hand-copies the transcription page (the three items above on one sheet, no name line anywhere on the sheet) and mails or hands it in. For fully remote cohorts, the photographed unsigned sheet goes through the anonymous form as an upload rather than through any channel that shows a sender.

### Operator steps (in order)

1. **Announce at Module 11.** One feed post: what is collected (the transcription, not the form itself), why (course revision, never learner evaluation), the channel(s), and the window. State plainly: *submission is optional and anonymous by construction; skipping it has no consequence of any kind.*
2. **Open the window for 7 days** from cohort close. The [Cohort Calendar Template](Cohort%20Calendar%20Template.md) carries the date.
3. **One reminder, mid-window, to the whole cohort.** Never to individuals. The operator must not track who has or has not submitted, and with this mechanism, cannot.
4. **Close the window and compile** the aggregate (format below) before the post-cohort review.
5. **Small-cohort guard.** If fewer than five submissions arrive, report ranges and patterns in prose rather than exact counts, so a rating cannot be triangulated back to a person. If only one or two arrive, fold them into the next cohort's aggregate rather than reporting them alone.

### The aggregate format (what the operator compiles)

One page, three blocks:

**Block 1: Part 1 deltas.** One row per statement, counts only (example numbers shown):

| Part 1 row | Start: not yet / starting / landed | Day 10: not yet / starting / landed | Moved ≥1 step |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (context, responsibility) | 4 / 5 / 1 | 0 / 4 / 6 | 8 of 10 |
| 2 (Box, thoughtware) | … | … | … |

**Block 2: Part 2 readiness gaps.** One row per High module: how many submissions reported a "not yet" on a foundation question before that module, and which question recurred. Example reading: *"6 of 9 arrived at Module 5 with 'ground on purpose' still not yet: that points back at Module 3, not at the learners."*

**Block 3: one paragraph of pattern.** The smallest-delta row, the largest-delta row, the most repeated readiness gap, and any comment-line theme. No interpretation beyond that; the review does the interpreting.

### The feed-forward (where the aggregate goes)

The aggregate, together with the **completion-by-module pull** (how many learners unlocked each module: operator-facing counts from the platform, never published to learners, never turned into streaks, badges, or leaderboards), goes into the post-cohort course-revision review (Doc 04 §L). The two read together on purpose: the completion pull says *where* learners stopped; the aggregate says *what wasn't landing* when they stopped. The review's required output is one concrete improvement for the next cohort, and these two instruments are the cleanest place to find it.

After the review, the operator archives the aggregate (it is already anonymous) and destroys any paper submissions.

## How the aggregate feeds the post-cohort review (operator + CM)

This instrument is the **learning axis** of the post-cohort review (Doc 04 §L). The review already reads the action log for safety signal (green/yellow/red flags by module); this adds a signal for whether the *teaching* is landing.

The CM collects only the **anonymized aggregate**, never an individual learner's ratings tied to their name. What the aggregate surfaces:

- **Baseline → Day-10 deltas across the cohort.** If a given outcome (a row in Part 1) barely moved for most learners, that's a content signal: the distinction isn't landing from the module as written. (Per Doc 03 §L step 5: a pattern across multiple learners on one module means the module gets revised before the next cohort. A learning-delta pattern is the gentler, earlier version of that same signal.)
- **Repeated readiness gaps (Part 2).** If many learners hit "not yet" on the same foundation question before the same High module (say, most of the cohort arrives at Day 5 unable to ground on purpose), that points back at Day 3, not at the learners. The gap-catch the review identifies becomes a concrete "strengthen the Day 3 grounding practice" improvement.
- **One concrete improvement.** Doc 04 §L asks for exactly one improvement per cohort. The learning-axis aggregate is one of the cleanest places to find it: the module with the smallest deltas, or the foundation distinction the most learners arrived without.

What the aggregate is **not**: a ranking of learners, a completion metric, or a gate. No learner passes or fails on the basis of these ratings, and the course adds no badge or certificate (Doc 02 §J). The instrument respects the no-quiz/no-score stance completely: its only use, individually, is the learner's own mirror, and its only use, in aggregate, is to tell the operator and CM where the course itself can land better next time.

---

A closing word, learner to self: be honest in the direction of "not yet." The course has no interest in you performing competence, and the gremlin's appetite for looking-good is exactly the thing PM is built to catch (Doc 02 §J). The distinctions you mark "not yet" at Day 10 are not the course's failures or yours. They are the live edges you get to keep walking, in your own life, after the cohort closes. The work was never the badge. It was always the noticing.

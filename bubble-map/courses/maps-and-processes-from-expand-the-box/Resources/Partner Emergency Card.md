# Partner Emergency Card

*One printable page for the partner/witness. Print it when you pair; keep it with your course materials. If you are reading this because your partner is in trouble right now, start at §1.*

---

## 1. If your partner may be in danger: the stop script

Ask one question, plainly:

> **"Are you in immediate danger right now?"**

- **If yes:** contact local emergency services or your country's crisis line AND the CM **immediately**. Do not wait for course protocol. Do not wait for the CM's 24-hour SLA. Stay with your partner in the message thread until they have connected with crisis services.
- **If no:** voice-message the CM the same day. The CM will reach out to your partner directly.

This is the immediate-danger protocol in the partner agreement you signed. Crisis-line numbers are on the **first page of your referral packet**; the international catch-all is **findahelpline.com**.

---

## 2. If your partner names more than the exchange can hold

> If your partner names material that exceeds the module's scope (trauma surfacing, ideation, intent to act on the original event in their adult life) — gently say so. *"What you are describing is bigger than this exchange can hold. I am glad you told me. I am also asking you to message the CM today, and if there is a clinician you work with, them too. I am still with you. And I am being honest about the limits of what I can do here."* Then continue witnessing. The honesty is part of the witnessing.

Say it close to word for word. The script does three things at once: it keeps you present, it routes the material to the right container, and it stays honest about your limits. All three are the witnessing.

---

## 3. The 60-second grounding: read it aloud

If your partner is flooded, dissociating, or spiraling on a live call, read this to them slowly, one line at a time, and wait for them to do each step. (On voice message: record it the same way, with pauses.)

> **Grounding (60 seconds).**
>
> 1. Stop whatever you were doing in the module. Close the video or the document.
> 2. Stand up. Both feet on the floor. Knees soft.
> 3. Breathe out longer than you breathe in, three times. Audible exhale is fine.
> 4. Name three things you can see in the room.
> 5. Name one thing you can hear.
> 6. Place one hand on your sternum, the other on your belly. Notice that your body is here.
> 7. When you have come back, decide: continue, pause for 10 minutes, or stop the module for today.

Then ask them to say out loud which they chose: continue, pause, or stop. Their answer, in their voice, is your signal that they are back enough to choose.

---

## 4. When to hand off

You are a witness, not a clinician. Hand off (and keep witnessing while you do) when any of these is true:

| What you hear or see | Where it goes |
|---|---|
| Immediate danger to themselves or someone else | §1: emergency services / crisis line + CM, now |
| Suicidal ideation without immediate danger | CM the same day; the CM takes it from there |
| Trauma material (flashback, body memory, sensory replay) | Their clinician today + CM today (the §2 script) |
| Still escalating after the grounding script | CM today; do not run the practice again |
| Anything that leaves you over your head | CM. That is the rule: the partner contacts the CM, not the learner's professional supports |

Handing off is part of witnessing. It is not abandoning your partner; it is refusing to improvise care they need from someone qualified.

---

## 5. The three things you never do

1. **Analyze.** No interpreting, no "this sounds like your father," no therapy vocabulary. Naming their experience for them takes the pen out of their hand.
2. **Fix.** No advice, no suggestions, no "have you tried." The urge to fix is the Rescuer wanting its own discomfort to stop.
3. **Minimize.** No "it's probably fine," no "everyone feels that way," no changing the subject. A minimized disclosure does not shrink; it goes underground.

What you do instead: stay. Hold your own center, ground, bubble; your state is the container. Say less than you think you need to. The witness's energy of being present is the medicine.

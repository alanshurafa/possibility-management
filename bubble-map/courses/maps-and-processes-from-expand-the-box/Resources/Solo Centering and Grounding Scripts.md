# Solo Centering and Grounding Scripts

> A library of script-ready solo practices for the learner. Read once. Then use.

---

## When to use this file

This file is for you, the learner. It contains eleven scripts. Some are 60 seconds — for when you need to come back into your body in the middle of a module and decide what to do next. Some are 12 minutes — the standard energetic-body practice you will run before every High module from Day 5 onwards. Some are emergency scripts for the specific things that happen: crying that won't stop, going numb, the disorientation of liquid state, the over-stimulated nervous system at the end of a processing day.

None of these are meditations. They are PM practices. They install distinctions through the body — center, ground, bubble, cube, the four feelings as energies, the difference between feeling and dissociating — and they are most useful when run as scripts you read aloud (or record once and play back) rather than as ideas you "do in your head." Doing them in your head is the Box's performance of doing them.

**How to use this file:**
- Bookmark it. You will reach for it more in Weeks 2–4 of the course than in Week 1.
- Record the ones you use most as voice memos on your phone. Your own voice reading these to you is more useful than reading silently mid-state.
- Run them as written before you customize. Run as written for 30 days. Then adapt if you must.
- Share scripts 2 and 7–10 with your pairing partner — those are the ones a partner may need to talk you through over voice message if you are flooded.
- These scripts do not replace the safety pathway in Doc 03. If you are in crisis, ground first, then reach the buddy and the CM. The scripts are the *first move*, not the only move.

The 60-second universal grounding script is reproduced from Doc 03 Section D for use anywhere — including for sharing on the cohort feed when you want to ask the cohort to ground with you. The other ten scripts are new and live only here.

## 1. The 60-second universal grounding script

This is the script. The one at the top of every module. The default action whenever you notice you have gone far away from yourself.

> **Grounding (60 seconds).**
>
> 1. Stop whatever you were doing in the module. Close the video or the document.
> 2. Stand up. Both feet on the floor. Knees soft.
> 3. Breathe out longer than you breathe in, three times. Audible exhale is fine.
> 4. Name three things you can see in the room.
> 5. Name one thing you can hear.
> 6. Place one hand on your sternum, the other on your belly. Notice that your body is here.
> 7. When you have come back, decide: continue, pause for 10 minutes, or stop the module for today.

Use this any time you notice:
- Feeling far away from yourself
- Inability to follow the words on the page or in the video
- Heart racing without clear cause
- Watching yourself from outside
- Crying that does not feel clean — feels stuck
- Strong urge to flee, attack, or shut down

These are nervous-system signals, not signs something is wrong with you. Ground first. Decide second.

## 2. The 5-minute centering practice

For the start of every module. Run it before you open the file. It is the difference between the module landing in your body and the module landing in your head as content.

> **Script. 5 minutes. Read aloud or play back. Eyes open or closed — your choice.**
>
> **Minute 1 — arrive.** Sit. Both feet on the floor. Spine upright but not braced. Shoulders down. Hands resting on your thighs or in your lap.
>
> Three breaths. Each exhale longer than the inhale. Make the exhale audible on at least one.
>
> Notice the chair under you. Notice the floor under your feet. Notice the temperature of the air on your face.
>
> **Minute 2 — find your center.** Move your attention to your lower belly — a hand's-width below your navel, behind the abdominal wall. This is the hara. For most people, this is where your center actually lives.
>
> Say out loud, or in your head if you need to: *"My center is here."*
>
> If your attention drifts to your head — to thoughts, to planning, to the day — notice. Bring it back to your belly. The drift is not a problem. The noticing-and-returning is the practice.
>
> If your center is not in your belly — if it actually lives behind your sternum, in your heart — locate it there. Where it actually is.
>
> **Minute 3 — drop a grounding cord.** From your center, declare a thick energetic cord — ship's-rope diameter — going down through your seat, through the floor, through the building, into the earth, all the way to the center.
>
> Say out loud: *"My grounding cord is dropped."*
>
> Not visualization. Declaration. It exists because you said so.
>
> Let excess energy drain. Tension from your day. Charge from the conversation you just had. The buzz of the screens. Allow. Do not push.
>
> **Minute 4 — set a bubble.** Declare a sphere of energy around you. Arm's length in every direction. Under your feet. Above your head.
>
> Say out loud: *"My bubble is set."*
>
> Inside the bubble: your body, your breath, your feelings, your rhythm, this module. Outside the bubble: the room beyond arm's length, the street noise, the partner you were thinking about, the email you have not answered. Real, *not yours, not right now*.
>
> If the bubble feels too big — pull it in. Too small or collapsed — push it out. Your choice.
>
> **Minute 5 — hold all three and arrive.** Center. Grounding cord. Bubble.
>
> Three breaths. Slow. Audible exhale.
>
> Now, out loud: *"I am here. I am centered. I am grounded. I am bubbled. I am ready to begin the module."*
>
> Open the document. Begin.

Run this before every module. Every single one. Including the Low intensity ones. By Module 4 it will take you 90 seconds because the equipment is yours; until then, run all five minutes.

## 3. Center + grounding cord + bubble — the standard practice (10 minutes)

This is the longer-form version of Practice 2. Run it once a day during the course if you can, and always before any High module (5, 6, 7, 9) — that is what the consent check is referring to when it says *"I know how to ground."*

> **Script. 10 minutes. Chair, water, no phone, no music. Read it through once, then run it.**
>
> **Minute 1 — settle.** Sit. Both feet on the floor. Drink water. Three audible exhales.
>
> Notice three things you can see. One thing you can hear. The temperature of your hands.
>
> **Minutes 2–3 — find center.** Move attention to the lower belly. Hand's-width below the navel, behind the abdominal wall.
>
> *"My center is here."*
>
> Stay there. Attention will drift up — to head, to thought, to plan. Each time you notice the drift, return. The rep is *noticing and returning*, not staying.
>
> If your center actually lives in your heart (some people, particularly artists and healers, do live there), locate it where it actually is. Trust where your body says it is.
>
> Place one hand on your center. Feel the rise and fall of breath through the hand. Sixty seconds.
>
> **Minutes 4–5 — drop the grounding cord.** From your center, declare a thick cord. Ship's-rope diameter. Down through your seat, through the floor, through whatever is beneath the floor, into the earth, into the bedrock, all the way to the core of the planet.
>
> *"My grounding cord is dropped."*
>
> Notice if your cord wants to be a different size or material. Some people's cord is a tree trunk; some, a column of light; some, a steel cable. Do not pick. Notice what it actually is.
>
> Let excess discharge. Energy that is not yours — what you absorbed from the last conversation, the news, the city — drains down the cord. Do not push it. Allow.
>
> Notice colour, weight, temperature in the cord. Do not interpret. Just note.
>
> **Minutes 6–7 — set the bubble.** Declare a sphere of energy around your body. Arm's length in every direction. Under your feet, above your head, behind you.
>
> *"My bubble is set."*
>
> Walk the perimeter of the bubble with your attention. Right. Behind. Left. Front. Above. Below. The edges are where your body's "yours" meets the world's "not yours."
>
> Notice the size. Too big — your energy is stretched thin, you are leaking into the room. Pull it in. Too small or collapsed — you are squeezed, you have given your space away to whoever or whatever is in your head. Push it out to arm's length.
>
> Inside: body, breath, feelings, rhythm, this practice. Outside: real, *not yours*.
>
> **Minutes 8–9 — hold all three.** Center. Cord. Bubble.
>
> Three slow breaths.
>
> Body scan, fast. Top of head — present? Throat — present? Heart — present? Hands — present? Belly — present? Pelvis — present? Legs — present? Feet — present? Note where you are clearly here and where you are still partly somewhere else. Do not fix it. Notice.
>
> **Minute 10 — choose.** What is next?
>
> If you are entering a module: *"I am centered, grounded, bubbled. I am beginning the module."*
>
> If you are entering a conversation: *"I am centered, grounded, bubbled. I am entering this conversation."*
>
> If you are entering sleep: *"I am centered, grounded, bubbled. I am letting the day complete."*
>
> Drink water. Stand up. Go.

The equipment compounds. By the third or fourth pass it is yours and you can run it in 60 seconds when you need to. Until then — full ten minutes. The reps build the perceptual interface.

## 4. The golden cube — workspace setup (12 minutes)

Run this when you are about to declare something or run a specific experiment in your actual life that the module surfaced. The golden cube is the energetic workspace inside which you place one piece of thoughtware work — a distinction to land, a piece of your Box to look at, a declaration to test, an experiment to design.

Not for daily use. For when there is something to construct or test.

> **Script. 12 minutes. Chair, water, notebook open. Read aloud or record.**
>
> **Minutes 1–4 — center, ground, bubble.** Run the practice from Script 3, shortened. Center. Cord. Bubble. Three breaths.
>
> Confirm: *"I am centered. I am grounded. I am bubbled."*
>
> Do not open the cube until the three are stable. If they are not stable, run Script 3 in full first.
>
> **Minute 5 — open the cube.** Two paces in front of you, declare a cube of golden light. Size of a small room — large enough to walk into if you wanted to, though you will not. Edges crisp. The interior is a workspace.
>
> *"The cube is open."*
>
> Notice the cube. Notice that it is two paces away, not on top of you, not inside you. The distance is the point. You are looking at it. You are not in it.
>
> **Minutes 6–7 — place the work inside.** Decide what you are placing inside the cube. **One piece only.** Common pieces:
> - A distinction you are trying to land (*"feeling vs. emotion in my relationship with my brother"*)
> - A piece of your Box you want to look at from outside (*"the rule that says I do not raise my voice"*)
> - A situation this week you are about to walk into (*"the conversation with my boss tomorrow"*)
> - A declaration you are considering (*"I am running the daily box catch for 30 days"*)
> - An experiment you are designing (*what / by when / what I will notice*)
>
> Say out loud what you are placing inside: *"I am placing inside the cube: [the piece]."*
>
> See it inside the cube. As a sentence, as a shape, as a memory, as a phrase. However your body wants to put it there.
>
> **Minutes 8–10 — look.** Stay centered, grounded, bubbled. Eyes soft. Attention on the cube.
>
> Do not solve. Do not figure out. Do not plan.
>
> Look.
>
> Notice what becomes visible about the piece that was not visible before you put it in the cube. Often: where the Box is hiding inside the piece. What ego state is running the piece. What feeling is underneath. What declaration would change everything. What experiment would test the smallest version.
>
> Do not write yet. Look. Two to three minutes.
>
> **Minute 11 — capture.** Open your notebook. Write fast, no editing. One to three sentences:
> - What became visible inside the cube
> - What the next move is — declaration, experiment, conversation, pause
> - What you are not yet ready to do
>
> Close the notebook.
>
> **Minute 12 — close the cube.** Out loud: *"The cube is closed."*
>
> The cube dissolves. The work stays.
>
> Confirm: *"I am centered. I am grounded. I am bubbled."*
>
> Three breaths. Drink water. Stand up. Go.

The cube is a workspace, not a refuge. Opened to construct or test. Closed when done. A learner who walks around with the cube permanently open is leaking — close it.

## 5. The dissolution script (8 minutes)

Run this at the end of any High module — Day 5, 6, 7, or 9 — to clear residue from the practice. The emotional body has been worked. The energetic body has held a charged container. Both want clearing before you return to ordinary life. Without this, you carry the module's residue into the next conversation, the next meal, the next sleep — and call that residue your mood.

> **Script. 8 minutes. Standing if you can, sitting if you must. Notebook open at the end.**
>
> **Minute 1 — confirm equipment.** Stand. Both feet on the floor. Center. Grounding cord. Bubble.
>
> Confirm out loud: *"I am centered. I am grounded. I am bubbled."*
>
> Three audible exhales.
>
> **Minute 2 — name what was worked.** Out loud, in one sentence: *"In this module, I worked [the piece]. What surfaced was [the feeling or material]."*
>
> Naming completes the practice. Until named, the practice has not finished.
>
> **Minutes 3–4 — discharge through the cord.** Bring attention to your center. Then to the cord.
>
> Out loud: *"Anything that is not mine, drain."*
>
> Feel — or imagine until you feel — the cord widening. Whatever you absorbed from the practice that is not yours moves down. Old emotional charge that surfaced and was not yet ready to process. The residue of your Box's noise. Tension in shoulders, jaw, gut. Allow.
>
> Sixty seconds, eyes soft, do nothing but allow.
>
> **Minute 5 — release the bubble's content.** The bubble has been the container for the practice. Now release its content.
>
> Out loud: *"Whatever entered my bubble in this practice that is not mine, leaves."*
>
> Imagine — or know — your bubble's edges becoming briefly permeable, then re-sealing. What was witness energy from your partner's earlier message, what was charge from the video, what was your Box's commentary — all releases.
>
> *"My bubble is set."* Re-confirm.
>
> **Minute 6 — physical movement.** Move the body. Shake hands and arms loosely for 30 seconds. Roll shoulders. Roll neck once each direction. Stretch jaw — yawn audibly, twice. Stamp feet ten times.
>
> Stored energy that did not move during the practice leaves now. The body completes what the practice started.
>
> **Minute 7 — drink and re-orient.** Drink a full glass of water, slowly. Cold water is fine. Hot water is fine.
>
> Look around the room. Name three things out loud. *"I see the lamp. I see the chair. I see the door."*
>
> Name what time of day it is. Name what you are about to do next.
>
> **Minute 8 — capture and close.** Notebook open. Write three lines, fast:
> - What I worked: ___
> - What surfaced: ___
> - What I am leaving in the practice (not carrying into the rest of the day): ___
>
> Close the notebook. Out loud: *"The practice is complete."*
>
> Walk away. The work is done. Trust that what wants to integrate will integrate while you do other things. Do not chew on it.

Run this after every High module. Skipping it is what makes Day 5 still being processed on Day 8. Run it.

## 6. The "I started crying and can't stop" emergency script (3 minutes)

Use the moment you notice the crying is not coming in waves with arrival, peak, and dissipation — it is looping, falling, or feels like there is no exit. This is emotion (per Day 5), not present-time sadness. The container needs to shift.

> **Script. 3 minutes. Read it aloud to yourself between sobs if you have to.**
>
> **Step 1 — interrupt.** Out loud: *"Stop."* Or: *"Pause."* Whatever word your body responds to.
>
> Stand up. If you cannot stand, sit upright and put both feet flat on the floor.
>
> **Step 2 — water on the face.** Get to the sink. Cold water — splash on your face. Once. Twice. Three times. The cold shocks the vagus nerve and resets the cry response. This is physiological, not punishment.
>
> Look in the mirror. See your own eyes for two seconds.
>
> **Step 3 — five senses.** Name out loud:
> - Five things you can see *("the mirror, the tap, the tile, my hand, the towel")*
> - Four things you can hear *("the tap dripping, my breath, the fridge, traffic")*
> - Three things you can feel *("water on my face, floor under my feet, the towel in my hand")*
> - Two things you can smell *("the soap, my own skin")*
> - One thing you can taste *("the inside of my mouth")*
>
> The crying will slow or stop during this. If it does not, repeat the five senses naming once more.
>
> **Step 4 — locate the emotion, do not process it.** Sit down. Put one hand on your chest, one on your belly.
>
> Out loud: *"This is an emotion. Not a present-time feeling. I am not processing it right now."*
>
> Name what age it might be from, if anything comes: *"This is old. This is from [a time, an age, a memory]."*
>
> Do not story. Do not figure out. Naming and stopping is the move.
>
> **Step 5 — partner message, then choose.** Pick up the phone. Voice message your pairing partner. Short: *"I hit an emotion in the module. I am grounded. I stopped. I am okay. I am letting you know."*
>
> Then decide:
> - Stop the module for today. Re-enter tomorrow.
> - If the crying restarts, run this script again, and message the CM.
> - If you have a therapist, this is a clean time to use a session. Be specific: *"I am doing a feelings-vs-emotions module. I hit an emotion. I am grounded. Can we work with it?"*
>
> Drink water. Move your body for two minutes — walk to another room, look out a window. Do not return to the module today.

The crying that loops is an open wound waiting for the right conditions to close. The right conditions are *not* finishing this module today. The right conditions are: ground, witness, professional support if needed, time. The course will be here tomorrow.

## 7. The "I went numb" emergency script (3 minutes)

Different shape from crying that won't stop. Numbness shows up as: you cannot feel anything in your body. You are watching the module from outside yourself. The words on the page are not landing. You notice you have stopped breathing fully. You feel a flatness that is somehow louder than feelings.

This is the Box re-installing the Numbness Bar in real time, because the previous practice loosened it. Numbness is information.

> **Script. 3 minutes.**
>
> **Step 1 — name it.** Out loud: *"I have gone numb. This is the Numbness Bar."*
>
> Naming brings consciousness to it. Without naming, you stay underneath.
>
> **Step 2 — move physically, hard.** Numbness will not lift from sitting still and trying to feel.
>
> Stand. Stamp your feet, hard, ten times. Hear the stamp.
>
> Press your palms together as hard as you can for ten seconds. Feel the resistance.
>
> Push against a wall — both hands flat against the wall, lean in, push with your legs, twenty seconds. Feel the muscles in your arms, shoulders, legs.
>
> Numbness lifts through pressure, not through softness.
>
> **Step 3 — find one sensation.** Sit back down. Center, ground, bubble — fast version.
>
> Pick one body location. Hands. Just your hands. Look at them.
>
> Out loud: *"In my hands right now, the sensation is [warm / cold / tingling / heavy / nothing yet — say nothing yet if that is what is true]."*
>
> If you found a sensation, locate one more — feet, or jaw, or chest. Name it.
>
> If you still feel nothing — drink cold water. Touch something with a sharp temperature. Hold an ice cube if you have one. Feel the cold. *Name the cold out loud.*
>
> **Step 4 — find one feeling at 2%.** Now, much smaller: *"In my emotional body, right now, at 2%, is there some anger, sadness, fear, or joy?"*
>
> Take whatever comes. *"Some fear, 2%, in my chest."* Or *"Some sadness, 3%, behind my eyes."* The 2% is enough. Do not chase a bigger version.
>
> If nothing comes after sixty seconds — that is data. Note it. The Numbness Bar is high today. That is information, not failure.
>
> **Step 5 — partner message, then decide.** Voice message your partner: *"I went numb in the module. I am grounded. I stopped. I will re-enter tomorrow."*
>
> Decide: stop the module today. Do something physical and grounding for an hour — walk outside, cook a real meal, take a hot shower, talk to another human about something unrelated. Numbness lifts through ordinary embodied life, not through more module.
>
> Re-enter the module tomorrow with Script 2 first.

Numbness is not failure. The Box did its old job — it cut the signal because the signal was going to be more than the system thought it could handle. You teach the system, slowly, that the signal is workable. The teaching is the work.

## 8. The "I'm in liquid state and don't know who I am" reorientation script (5 minutes)

The disorientation that arises mid-course is real, and it is normal. The Box has loosened enough that the old answer to *who am I* is not snapping back into place. The new answer has not formed yet. You are between thoughtwares. The feeling can be unmistakable — *I don't know what I think about anything anymore* — and is often confused for crisis. It is not crisis. It is liquid state. It is exactly what the course is for.

But you need to walk while you are in it, and walking requires equipment.

> **Script. 5 minutes.**
>
> **Minute 1 — name the state.** Out loud: *"I am in liquid state. This is not crisis. The old thoughtware is loose. The new thoughtware has not stabilized yet. This is what the course is for."*
>
> Naming relocates the disorientation from *something wrong with me* to *the predictable consequence of the work I am doing*.
>
> **Minute 2 — center, ground, bubble.** Fast version. Stand if you can.
>
> Center — hand on lower belly. *"My center is here."*
>
> Cord — *"My grounding cord is dropped."*
>
> Bubble — *"My bubble is set."*
>
> Three breaths.
>
> The equipment is what lets you stay in liquid state without flooding. Without the equipment, liquid state destabilizes. With it, liquid state works.
>
> **Minute 3 — what is still true.** Out loud, list what is still true. Things that have not become uncertain.
>
> *"My name is ___. I live in ___. The people I love today are ___. The work I do is ___. The body I am in is mine. The date is ___. I am taking the Expand the Box course and I am on Day ___."*
>
> If some of those are also currently in question, say only the ones that are not.
>
> The point is: there is a floor. The floor did not disappear. The Box was claiming credit for the floor — when the Box loosens, it feels like the floor is going too. It is not. Find the floor.
>
> **Minute 4 — what is in flux.** Out loud, name what is in flux. *"What is in flux right now is [my certainty about my job / my story about my marriage / my picture of who I am at work / my relationship with my parents]."*
>
> Naming it as flux distinguishes it from collapse. Flux is movement. Collapse is the absence of structure.
>
> *"This is liquid state in [the area]. I am letting the old thoughtware loosen. I am not yet rebuilding the new."*
>
> **Minute 5 — choose the next small thing.** Liquid state is not the time for large decisions. The decisions made in liquid state, sourced from disorientation, are usually the Box trying to grab any thoughtware to stop the discomfort of having none.
>
> Out loud: *"I will not make any large decisions in the next 48 hours. I will not quit, leave, end, or commit. I will pause."*
>
> Then choose **one** small concrete next thing. Drink water. Eat a meal. Walk for 20 minutes. Voice-message my partner. Sleep.
>
> Do that small thing. Then the next small thing. The thoughtware reformation happens on its own — your job is not to construct it on the spot. Your job is to *be alive while it happens*.

If the disorientation persists for more than 48 hours and does not respond to grounding, contact the CM. There is a difference between liquid state (workable with equipment) and a state that has spilled past what the course can hold (needs professional support). The CM helps you tell which.

## 9. Pre-sleep grounding for processing days (4 minutes)

Run this on the evening of any High module day, before you get into bed. The emotional and energetic body have been worked; sleep is when integration happens, but only if the system is not still spinning. This script signals to the system: *the work is complete for today, you may sleep now*.

> **Script. 4 minutes. Sitting on the edge of the bed, lights dim, phone away.**
>
> **Minute 1 — settle the room.** Put your phone in another room or face-down on do-not-disturb. Lights low. Water by the bed.
>
> Sit on the edge of the bed. Both feet on the floor.
>
> Three audible exhales, each longer than the inhale. Make them slow.
>
> **Minute 2 — center, ground, bubble.** Fast version, lying-down-ready.
>
> Center — hand on belly. *"My center is here."*
>
> Cord — *"My grounding cord is dropped. Anything that is not mine drains."*
>
> Bubble — *"My bubble is set. The day is outside it. The bedroom is the size of my bubble."*
>
> **Minute 3 — close the day.** Out loud, in one sentence each:
> - What I worked today: *"Today I worked [Day __, the practice, the partner exchange]."*
> - What surfaced: *"What surfaced was [the feeling / the realization / the question]."*
> - What is incomplete: *"What is incomplete and waiting is [name it, do not solve it]."*
> - What I am setting down for sleep: *"I am setting down [the worry, the conversation, the loop]. It will be there in the morning if it matters. I am letting sleep do its work."*
>
> Naming closes the loop. Unnamed loops keep running through the night.
>
> **Minute 4 — release the cord and rest.** Out loud: *"The work for today is complete."*
>
> Lie down. Feel the bed under you — full length of your body. Hands resting at your sides or on your belly.
>
> Center is still here. Grounding cord is still dropped — it does not turn off for sleep. Bubble is the size of the bed.
>
> Three slow breaths. Do not try to sleep. Let sleep come.
>
> If thought-loops start — name them: *"That is thought. I am not following it tonight."* Return attention to the cord, to the belly, to the breath.

The integration that happens in sleep after a processing day is real and large. The system is metabolizing what the module loosened. Protect the sleep — no scrolling, no late drink, no late food, no opening one more email. The pre-sleep ground is the move that says: *the day is closed; the integration can begin*.

## 10. Grounding outdoors (walking or sitting in nature)

Same principles, adapted for outside. Use when you are walking on processing days, or when you have stepped outside between module sections to clear your head. Many learners report the outdoor version is faster than indoor because the body is already in motion and the senses are already engaged.

> **Script. Variable length — works at 3 minutes or 30. Read it once, then go.**
>
> **First — choose your spot or your path.** A park. A street. A trail. A garden. Even a city block. The outdoor is what matters; it does not need to be wild.
>
> Phone in pocket, on silent. No earbuds. The point is to listen with the body.
>
> **Settle into the body.** Stand for thirty seconds. Both feet on the ground (or earth, or pavement — it works on pavement). Notice the contact through the soles.
>
> Three audible exhales.
>
> **Find your center while standing.** Hand on lower belly. *"My center is here."*
>
> Notice that center can be found while standing, while walking, anywhere. The Box wants to convince you grounding is only for sitting with eyes closed. It is not.
>
> **Drop a grounding cord through the ground.** *"My grounding cord is dropped."*
>
> The cord goes through the ground or pavement, through whatever is under it, into the earth. Pavement does not break it. The cord exists because you said so.
>
> Walking with a dropped cord feels different from walking without one. You will feel the difference in 30–60 seconds — a kind of weight in the lower body that was not there before, a steadiness.
>
> **Set the bubble — outdoors size.** Out loud or in your head: *"My bubble is set, arm's length."*
>
> Outdoors the bubble is the same size as indoors. Resist the pull to make it bigger because you are in open space. Arm's length keeps it operational.
>
> **Begin walking, or sit if you are sitting.** Now use the senses.
>
> *Three things you can see, named in your head as you walk: "tree, fence, sky."*
>
> *Two things you can hear: "bird, car."*
>
> *One thing you can smell: "grass, wet pavement, rain."*
>
> If you are walking, match your breath to your stride for a few minutes. Inhale three steps, exhale four. Find the count your body wants.
>
> **Notice the feeling in the body.** *"In my emotional body, right now, at low intensity, is there some anger, sadness, fear, joy?"*
>
> Outdoors, feelings often arrive that have been blocked indoors. The open space gives them room. Name what arrives, locate it, do not story it. Keep walking.
>
> **Close when you return.** Before you come back inside, pause at the door. *"I am centered. I am grounded. I am bubbled. I am returning indoors."*
>
> If you sat — same close, before you stand to leave.

The outdoor practice does not replace the indoor one. It runs alongside it. The combination — daily indoor practice plus regular outdoor grounding — is what installs the energetic-body equipment most reliably across the 30 days of the course and the 90 days after.

---

## 11. Final note

These eleven scripts are the equipment. You do not have to do all of them every day. You have to know which one to reach for in the moment you need it.

Three rules:

1. **Run as written before you customize.** The scripts have been tuned. They land cleanly if read as written. Customizations are best made after 30 days of as-written practice, not before.
2. **Read them aloud or record them.** PM is a body practice. Scripts read silently land in the head. Scripts read aloud — or played back in your own voice — land in the body. The difference is large.
3. **Do not skip the practice because you are "fine."** *Fine* is often the Box. The practice is the rep. The rep is the work.

When you come back to this file in Week 4, you will find some of the scripts you needed in Week 2 you no longer need — the equipment has installed. New scripts (3, 4, 5) will be the ones you use most. That is the arc.

Welcome to your own equipment.

  • Theory Behind Segue Phrases

    'Segue' is pronounced 'seg-way'.

    The term 'Segway' refers to the 2-wheeled electric transports.

    “Don’t explain. Don’t defend. Segue.”

    WHAT IS A SEGUE PHRASE?

    A 'Segue Phrase' is a transitional expression used to smoothly move through the Gap from one theme, idea, or impossibility to a new Space with a new Context. It serves as a bridge, making transitions that feel natural rather than abrupt.


    Examples of Segue Phrases:

    • "That reminds me..." Used to connect a current topic to a new one (e.g., "That reminds me, did you see the email?").
    • "Speaking of (nonlinear topic jump)..." A common way to shift focus while acknowledging the previous topic.
    • "By the way..." Introduces a tangential theme or Space3, more conversational and seemingly informal.
    • "Moving on to..." Commonly used in presentations, business meetings, or lectures to indicate an Intentional change in focus.
    • "That leads into..." Explicitly links the previous thought to the next, but can go orthogonal out of the current Space.
    • “Let’s try a different context for a moment.”
    • “What if we set aside being right and look at what’s possible?”
    • “What if we step into curiosity instead of holding onto a conclusion?”
    • "Let us become Experimenters for a moment..."
    • "Do you know what a Gap in Space is?"

    • Bridge (the woman walking) over the Gap, without extra tools, no certainty, just conscious movement.
    • Right side (green forest) is the New Space, with a new Context, new Distinctions, and new Possibilities.

    She is not panicking.

    That is the Segue Phrase embodied.

    A Segue Phrase is a precise linguistic bridge that allows a person or group to leave one Context without resistance and enter another Contex without confusion. The new Context offers new Possibilities.

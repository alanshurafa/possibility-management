Day 1 · Embodied Practice · ~15 minutes

The Red-Pill Ceremony

This is the analogue of the live ETB ceremony — the moment the trainer asks blue pill or red pill? and gives everyone 90 seconds to walk out and get their money back. Online and async, you do it for yourself.

Find a place where you will not be interrupted. Stand. Have voice. Have the next 15 minutes.

Read the next step in your body, not in your head.

Step II · Body

Stand. Feet on the floor.

Both feet on the floor. Shoulders down, away from your ears. Hands loose at your sides. Spine long, not stiff.

Standing matters. Don't sit down for this.

Step III · Breath

Three breaths. Exhale longer than inhale.

Breathe in
3

Audible exhale is fine. The body knows what to do.

Step IV · Speaking

Out loud, now:

In the next 30 days I am going to do this course.

Say it. Don't think it — say it.

Notice what your body did when you said that. Tightened? Braced? Brightened? That's data.

Step V · Speaking

Now, this one:

I can stop at any time. I can pause at any time. I can put it down at any time. Nothing makes me do this.

Notice what your body did that time. Different?

Step VI · The blue pill

Speak this out loud — feel it land:

Blue pill. I'm not doing this. The course is interesting but I'm not actually in it. I'll watch the videos when I get to them. I won't pair with anyone. I won't run the experiments. I'm a tourist.

Pause. Notice what your body said back.

Step VII · The red pill

Now this one. Out loud.

Red pill. I am in this. For the next 30 days I am the author of what this becomes for me. I will pair with my partner. I will run the experiments. I will use the safety practices when I need them. I will not pretend to be in if I am not. I am in.

Pause. Notice. Which sentence did your body recognize as true?

Step VIII · The choice

Choose now.

Option I
I take the blue pill
Option II
I take the red pill

Neither is wrong. Honesty is the only thing being measured.

Clean choice

That's a clean answer.

The course will be here in three months. Or six. Or in a year. Talk to your community manager about pausing your enrollment. Doing this course as a tourist is worse than not doing it.

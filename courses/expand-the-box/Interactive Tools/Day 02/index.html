The tool · the practices
I.
Interactive inventory · 3 layers · M04

Box Inventory.

Sit with a three-layer Box diagram. Click each layer. Name what lives there. Watch your items appear in the layer they belong to.
Surface (behaviors, habits) is easiest. Story (beliefs, rules) takes some looking. Survival (identity-level commitments) the tool gates behind a short consent prompt — Day 2 stays at surface and story on purpose. Your inventory persists on this device. You will return to it on Day 9.
~20 min · solo · paper-grade work Open →
Off-screen · in your day

Two practices the tool can't do for you.

PRACTICE I · Catch-and-name In the next 48 hours, catch your Box in the act once. The moment you notice you're reacting from a rule set by something that already happened, say silently: this is my box. I am not my box. Don't try to change the reaction.
PRACTICE II · Partner voice exchange Record a 3–8 minute voice message to your partner. Name one specific surface-layer pattern from your inventory. No backstory. No fixing. End with: I have a ___. I am not my ___.
· · ·

How to use this.

Read the Day 2 module first (`Days/Day 02 - Thoughtware, Thoughtmaps, Box Technology.md`) and watch the three source videos. Then come here.

Do not run the Box Inventory the same hour you watched the videos. The videos go in the intellectual body. The inventory has to land somewhere else. Spread Day 2 across 2–3 days.

What the tool saves.

Your inventory and reflection persist in your browser's local storage — on this device only, never sent anywhere. Return visits open the layer you last filled.

Clear your browser data and the tool forgets. That is the design. These are your practices, not the course's records. You will return to your inventory on Day 9.

Day 3 · Energetic-body practice · ~15 minutes

Center · Ground · Bubble · Cube

The four energetic-body practices PM rests on. The equipment that lets you stay present while thoughtware is loosening. Installed once here; sharpened by use over the rest of the course.

Find a chair, or stand. Have the next fifteen minutes uninterrupted. Have water nearby. Phone away. Eyes can close, or stay soft-open.

Read the next step in your body, not in your head.

Step II · Centering · ~3 minutes

Find your center.

Breathe in
10
0:00
Move your attention to your lower belly — a hand's-width below the navel, behind the abdominal wall.
"My center is here."

Not a metaphor. The energetic location your choices, will and authority come from. If it lives elsewhere in your torso — locate where it actually is. Do not move it.

The rep is noticing you have left, and returning.

Step III · Grounding cord · ~3 minutes

Drop the cord.

Breathe in
10
0:00
Declare — by intention, not picture — a thick energetic cord from your center, through your seat, through the floor, to the centre of the earth.
"My grounding cord is dropped."

It exists because you said so. Now let excess energy — shoulder tension, charge from your day — drain down it. Don't push. Allow. If a colour comes, note it without meaning anything by it.

Discharge. Stability. Not insulation, not protection.

Step IV · Bubble · ~3 minutes

Set the bubble.

Breathe in
10
0:00
Declare a sphere of personal space around you — arm's length in every direction, including under your feet and above your head.
"My bubble is set."

A distinction, not a protection. It does not block anything. It clarifies what is yours and what is not. Inside: your body, breath, feelings. Outside: the room, street noise, whoever you were just thinking about.

Too big? Pull it in. Collapsed against your skin? Push it out. Roughly arm's length.

Step V · Golden cube · ~2 minutes

Open the workspace.

Breathe in
10
0:00
From centered, grounded, bubbled — open a cube of golden light in front of you, roughly the size of a small room, two paces away.
"The cube is open."

Place inside one small piece of thoughtware work — a distinction to look at, a piece of your Box from Day 2, a situation this week. Stay centered, grounded, bubbled while you look. Do not solve. Look.

Workspace, not refuge. Architect's drafting table.

Step VI · Closing

"The cube is closed."

Your center is yours.
Your grounding cord is yours.
Your bubble is yours.
Your workspace is yours.
They will be there when you reach for them next time.

Three breaths. Open your eyes if they were closed. Drink water.

In your own words, fast, no editing — one or two sentences. What shifted?

Saved on this device only. Re-readable on your next visit.
Witnessed. Dated. Saved.

The equipment is installed.

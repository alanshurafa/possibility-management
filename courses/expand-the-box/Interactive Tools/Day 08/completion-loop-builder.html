Day 8 · Interactive Practice · the loop builder

A conversation that hasn't ended.
Build the loop that completes it.

Pick one communication in your life that has been hanging — paused, said but not landed, walked-away-from. In six steps, you will construct the actual completion loop you could run to finish it.

Step 1 · the hanging communication

What conversation is still hanging?

Short — the structure, not the script. Recent enough to be locatable; small enough to be your first rep. Don't pick the marriage-during-a-fight one. Pick the small thing you walked away from.

Step 2 · the gap

What you said vs what you meant.

Communication has a gap by default. Locate yours. The completion loop's job is to close it.

The gap The completion loop's job is to close that gap by checking what the other person actually received — not by saying it louder. They cannot read your intention. They received your encoding. The loop verifies the receipt. PM's account: the gap is not the exception. It is the structural fact. Five named places it can open — intention, encoding, reception, decoding, response. After three turns of unchecked gaps, most conversations are two parallel monologues touching only at the surface.

Step 4 · the 12 roadblocks

Roadblock check.

Before running the loop, check: does your draft contain any of Thomas Gordon's 12 Roadblocks? All twelve look helpful. None of them are listening.

These are not "bad." Most have a domain. The point is to notice that doing any of these instead of listening will quietly break the loop you are trying to close. Tick the ones your draft might contain. The loop-friendly alternative is on the right.

Step 6 · after running it

Come back here once you have actually run the loop. Not before. The debrief is for the rep, not the plan.

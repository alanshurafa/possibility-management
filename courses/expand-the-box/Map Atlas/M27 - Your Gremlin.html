The Underworld · Drama & Its Food

Your Gremlin.

The King or Queen of your Underworld — the part of you that wants drama, suffering and righteousness as food, and runs your life from below your awareness until you meet it on purpose.

What this map shows

A portrait, not a diagram. A resident, not an abstraction.

A hand-drawn gremlin — impish, clawed, grinning. The map is not a diagram of parts; it is a portrait of the thing itself, drawn so you stop treating it as an abstraction and start treating it as a resident. The text on the map names what it is: Your Gremlin: the King or Queen of your Underworld. And it owns your life. Is currently on a low-drama gremlin feast.

The Gremlin is the part of you that wants gremlin food — drama, suffering, righteousness, being right, being a victim, being special, distraction. It is sovereign down below: the King or Queen of your Underworld, running the show from beneath your awareness. And the map states the cost plainly — until you meet it consciously, it owns your life.

The map also names two things the rest of this tool unpacks: the Gremlin comes in types (the map marks Type 1 / Type 2 — different people's Gremlins have characteristic flavors), and there is a longer road past this course — Gremlin Transformation: Preface, Chapter 0, Chapter 1 — which is a separate container, not this one.

Scope note. This map is a locator and an introduction. Meeting and naming your Gremlin, and catching its low-drama feast in progress, is in scope for this course. Deep Gremlin process work — Gremlin Transformation (the map's Preface, Chapter 0, Chapter 1) — is a separate, longer container held by a PM trainer (the GT training). Do not attempt to "transform" your Gremlin solo. Naming it is the work here. (Same discipline as the Demon scope note in M17: naming is the entry; processing is a different room.)

Say it · the verbal map

Now explain it aloud.

This is the spoken version of the map. Read it through once, then close your eyes and say it in your own words. You teach the map by speaking it.

You have a Gremlin. It is the part of you that wants gremlin food — drama, suffering, righteousness, being right, being a victim, being special, distraction. PM calls it the King or Queen of your Underworld, and the phrase is exact: it sits sovereign below your awareness and runs the show from down there. The map says it plainly — it owns your life — and that stays true until the day you meet it on purpose.

Here is the distinction that makes it usable. Your Box keeps you safe — it is survival thoughtware, it speaks to protect you according to its rules. Your Gremlin is a different animal. It does not want safety. It wants food. It wants the charge that comes off drama and suffering and being right. It is usually louder than the Box and more cunning, and it speaks in a low, intimate, conspiratorial voice — they never appreciate you, you're better than them, just give up, go on, have one. The trap is that the voice feels like insight. It feels like what you really think. It feels like what you really feel. It is none of those. It is the Gremlin reaching for its next meal.

Right now, today, your Gremlin is most likely on a low-drama feast — eating small. Not the big catastrophe; the daily diet. The sigh, the eye-roll, the complaint you have made four times, the rescue nobody asked for, the forty-five seconds of internal righteousness in the car. That is the feast the map is pointing at. And the Gremlin eats in both directions — it is just as well fed by I'm the worst as by I'm the best. So it is not your inner critic. It feeds on the glory as happily as the shame.

Two more things. The Gremlin comes in types — the map marks Type 1 and Type 2 — so your Gremlin has its own characteristic flavor, its own favorite food. And the Gremlin is not the enemy. You do not kill it. You cannot. The work is to meet it, know it, learn its diet, befriend it, and eventually put it to conscious use. A Gremlin you know can be directed. A Gremlin you refuse to look at runs your life. So the question is not how do I get rid of it — it is what is my Gremlin's favorite food, and can I catch it at the table.

Embody it · solo practice

Meet your Gremlin's favorite food.

This is a locator, not a transformation. You are going to let your Gremlin have a voice on paper for five minutes and then name its favorite food out loud. You are not going to argue with it, fix it, or transform it. Naming the food is the rep. Sit. Both feet on the floor. Center, ground, drop a bubble (Day 3 equipment — re-read M07 if it has fallen out of your body).

Solo · ≤ 15 minutes · pen and paper required
  1. Give the Gremlin the pen (5 min). On paper, let your Gremlin say what it says when it wants its favorite food. Don't curate it. Let it be petty, righteous, superior, collapsed, smug — whatever it actually is. Prompt it: what does it want right now? To be right? To be wronged? To be special? To be the victim? To be above it all? Write in its voice, sotto voce, the real sentences — they never appreciate what I do; I'm the only one who sees clearly; nobody understands what I go through; I had no choice. Notice the small charge of pleasure that comes with some of them. That charge is the food being eaten. Notice it without indulging it.
  2. Name the favorite food, out loud (2 min). Read back what you wrote. Find the one food that shows up most, or the one that gives the biggest charge. Then say it plainly, out loud, once: "That's my Gremlin, and its favorite food is ___." Be specific. Not "being right" in the abstract — "my Gremlin's favorite food is being the one who was carrying everything while no one noticed." That specific.
  3. Do not argue with it. Do not fix it. This is the whole discipline of the practice. The moment you start debating the Gremlin (that's not fair, I'm not really like that, the other person actually was wrong) you are feeding it a fresh meal — being right, or being beneath it all. Just name the food and stop. Naming starves it slightly. That is enough for today.
  4. Close. Put the pen down. Three breaths, audible exhale. Out loud: "I have a Gremlin. I know one of its foods now. I am not it. I am the one who can know it." Stand up. Notice you are in your room, in your present-time body, not inside the Gremlin's story.
  5. Write three lines: what is my Gremlin's favorite food · where in my body did I feel the charge when I named it · where in my week is it most likely eating right now.
If anything intense surfaces — if the Gremlin turns the practice into self-disgust, I've been like this my whole life, I'm beyond hope — that is the Gremlin producing a new food (being especially awful), not the truth. Ground, stop for today, voice-message your partner. This map is the locator only. Deeper work belongs in Gremlin Transformation or with a PM trainer.

Ground. Now.

  1. Stop. Stand up — both feet flat on the floor. Feel the contact.
  2. Breathe out, longer than in — three slow exhales.
  3. Name three things you can see in this room. Out loud is best.
  4. Name one thing you can hear. The fridge. Traffic. Your own breath.
  5. One hand on your sternum, one on your belly. Notice your body is here.
  6. Then decide: continue, pause 10 minutes, or stop for today. Any of the three is fine.

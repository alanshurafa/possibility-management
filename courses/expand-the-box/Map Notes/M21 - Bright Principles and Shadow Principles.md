# M21 — Bright Principles and Shadow Principles

*The cosmology underneath archiarchy and patriarchy — every action is sourced from a principle, and consciousness of which principle is in the driver's seat is the work, not the suppression of one in favour of the other.*

*(No dedicated map image — the closest are the Map of Matrix and Map of 3 Games.)*

**What it is.** Every action you take is sourced from some principle — there is no neutral action. PM distinguishes two structural classes by the culture their sourcing generates, not by moral grade. Sourcing bright principles (love, integrity, possibility, courage, presence, creation) generates archiarchy and adds aliveness to the room; sourcing shadow principles (domination, control, scarcity, righteousness, war) generates patriarchy and subtracts it. The principle is upstream; the culture is downstream. The map is a diagnostic — *what is sourcing this action right now?* — and a prerequisite: a declaration (M23) only holds when sourced from bright.

**At a glance.** Bright → archiarchy, generative of consciousness · Shadow → patriarchy, generative of separation · Structural, not moral (bright≠good, shadow≠evil — shadow has real domains: the surgeon's control) · Children are sourced by the room; adults choose · The shadow you cannot name owns you — naming puts it in the room where you can choose for or against it.

## How to explain it verbally

Every action you take is sourced from some principle; there is no neutral action. PM splits principles into two classes, not by moral grade but by the culture their sourcing generates. Sourcing bright principles, love, integrity, possibility, generates archiarchy and adds aliveness to the room. Sourcing shadow principles, domination, control, scarcity, generates patriarchy and drains it. The principle is upstream; the culture is downstream. Bright is not good and shadow is not evil: shadow has real domains, like the surgeon sourcing control during an operation. The map is a diagnostic you run per action, *what is sourcing this*, and a prerequisite: a declaration only holds when sourced from bright.

**If you only remember one thing:** sourcing bright generates archiarchy and sourcing shadow generates patriarchy; the principle is upstream of the culture, never the other way round.

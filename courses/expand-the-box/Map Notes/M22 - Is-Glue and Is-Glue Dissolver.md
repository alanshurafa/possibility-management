# M22 — Is-Glue and Is-Glue Dissolver

*The linguistic mechanism by which the Box defends itself — the verb "to be" fusing perception to identity and identity to permanence — and the simple swap that restores the perception's provisional status without making it less true.*

![Is-Glue — Possibility Management map](../Maps/M22.png)

**What it is.** The Box defends itself with the cheapest, most invisible verb there is: "to be." "I AM stupid," "She IS controlling," "This IS impossible" weld a transient, partial perception to a permanent identity, and once welded the learner cannot perceive anything that contradicts it — the room is closed. The **is-glue dissolver** is a swap — "is" → "feels," "appears to be," "right now," "from my Box" — that restores the perception's provisional scope without denying it. Dissolving is-glue does not mean the perception was wrong; it means it is recognised as provisional.

**At a glance.** Is-glue → any *is/am/are* fusing perception, state, or role into permanent reality · Dissolver → swap to "feels / right now / from my Box," reopening the room · NOT softening or hedging — the dissolver is precise, appeasement is a different Box move · Dissolved ≠ removed — a provisional perception can later be re-spoken as a grounded finding · The work is catching the weld in real time, not banning the verb.

## How to explain it verbally

Is-glue is how the Box defends itself, using the cheapest verb there is: to be. *I am stupid. She is controlling. This is impossible.* Each one welds a passing, partial perception onto a permanent identity, and once welded you cannot perceive anything that contradicts it. The room is closed. The dissolver is a small swap: change *is* to *feels*, *appears to be*, *right now*, or *from my Box*. That restores the perception's provisional scope without pretending it was wrong. This is not softening or hedging; appeasement is a different Box move. A dissolved perception can later be re-spoken as a grounded finding.

**If you only remember one thing:** swapping *is* for *right now* reopens a room the verb *to be* had quietly welded shut.

# Practicing in a Group

*The learner-facing page for doing this course alongside other people. Optional. If you are solo and want to stay solo, you can close this file now: nothing below is required, and the whole course works without it.*

---

## What the group layer is, and what it is not

You can run Expand the Box with other people. A private community on Telegram, everyone holding the same course, each person at their own module, all sharing one weekly pulse. This page is the learner's map of that layer: how to find your people, how the accountability works, how to call on a witness, and what stays private.

Read one distinction before anything else, because everything here depends on it.

**The community is company and visibility. It is not your container.** Your container is small: your dyad (you and one witness) or your pod (3 to 12 people who do the rituals together and witness each other's High work on a call). The held work of this course happens there, in a place small enough for one person's attention to actually hold it. The wider community around the pods is for coordination, declaration, sharing Low and Medium material, celebrating Beeps, and finding people. It is not held. Nobody in the community at large has agreed to witness you, and a hundred-person room cannot hold a liquid state.

So the layers nest:

| Layer | Size | What it holds | Your agreement |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dyad** | 2 | One witness pair; voice messages and EHP witnessing | The 7-line partner agreement (Module 00) |
| **Pod** | 3–12 | Rituals together, teach-backs, High-work witnessing on calls | The [Pod Charter](../Resources/Pod%20Charter.md), which extends the 7 lines |
| **Community** | up to ~100 | Declaration, loop-closing, witness-finding, module rooms, celebrating Beeps | The pinned self-screen you acknowledge when you join |

One line carries the safety of the whole thing, and it never bends:

**High work never happens over text, and never in the community.** The Emotional Healing Process (Module 6) and the Demon-locator (Module 9) run live, on a call, inside a dyad or pod, with a qualified witness present. The community is where you find that witness and declare that you are about to do the work. It is not where you do it.

## Finding or forming a pod

A pod is the held group: 3 to 12 people who run the rituals together and witness each other. It is where the community's company becomes an actual container.

**To find one:** go to the **Pod Directory** topic in the community. Pods post when they have room, with their rough timezone, call rhythm, and how many seats are open. Reply to one that fits your hours. A pod is not a class and has no teacher. One person holds the **Pod Anchor** role, which is scheduling and keeping the charter, not facilitating. The Anchor is a peer who happens to own the calendar.

**To form one:** post in the Pod Directory that you are starting a pod, name a rough rhythm and timezone, and gather 2 to 11 others. Before your first ritual call, the whole pod reads and adopts the [Pod Charter](../Resources/Pod%20Charter.md) together. The Charter extends the 7-line dyad agreement to the pod: same confidentiality, same crisis line, same line-4 commitment, with the additions a small group needs (who witnesses whom, what happens on a call, what the Anchor does and does not do). A pod without an adopted Charter is a chat, not a container. Adopt it first.

Inside the pod you still pair. For voice-message exchanges and for EHP witnessing you have one named partner, your dyad inside the pod, on the 7-line agreement. The pod is the circle; the dyad is your two-person cell inside it.

## Staking: the accountability, from your side

The course already teaches Declaring as one of the Three Powers, and the Day-4 experiment format (*what I will do / by when / what I will notice*) is already a complete commitment. Staking is those two things made public. There are no points, no streaks, no leaderboards, and nothing to win. Visibility is the whole mechanism: you are more likely to run an experiment when you have said, where people can see it, that you are about to.

Here is the choreography from where you sit.

1. **Stake before you run it.** Post your experiment to the **Stake Board** in the Day-4 format, before you do it, not after. A template is in the community. Posting is staking your word in front of people who will remember.
2. **Two witnesses reply.** Up to two people reply "Witnessed 🕯" to your stake. They are now the two humans you report back to, a named hook rather than a notification. You do the same for theirs.
3. **Close the loop, publicly, within the capture discipline.** When you have run it, reply to your own stake: what happened, what you noticed. This is the [Daily Practice Spine](Daily%20Practice%20Spine.md)'s capture-within-ten-minutes discipline pointed at the board. Capture it in your Beep! Book first, then post the closing. The book is the record; the post is the visibility.
4. **Post Beeps proudly.** A Beep is information that something did not work as planned: a design specification, not a verdict. Failed experiments are Beeps, and Beeps are the most useful thing on the board, because they are data nobody else has. Post them with the same posture you would post a Go. A pod that only celebrates wins is running a scoreboard, and this course keeps no score.
5. **The Sunday skip-read.** Once a week the community posts the spine's review question as a thread: *"what got skipped, and what does the skip say about my Box?"* Answering publicly is optional; the prompt itself is the ritual. A week of skips is a precise reading of where your Box routes you around the work, not a failed week.

If you want the worked versions (what a good stake, a good closing, and a good Beep post actually look like), the community section of the [Worked Exemplars Pack](../Resources/Worked%20Exemplars%20Pack.md) shows them.

## Calling on a witness

When you need a witness beyond your standing partner (your partner is unreachable before a High module, or you want a fresh witness for a specific piece), you ask on the **Witness Board**.

The ask has a shape, so the person answering knows what they are agreeing to:

```
Seeking a witness for a small EHP.
Tue or Wed evening, CET.
I have read the witness annex and the Partner Emergency Card.
Looking for one person who has too.
```

Offer the same way when you can hold space for someone: name your hours and what you can witness.

A witness for this course is not just anyone who replies. The qualification path has three parts, and all three are required before any High work:

- **The agreement.** You and the witness share the 7-line partner agreement (or the [Pod Charter](../Resources/Pod%20Charter.md), which contains it). No agreement, no witnessing.
- **The witness annex.** The witness has read the witness annex in [03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework](../03%20-%20Safety%20and%20Facilitation%20Framework.md): what a witness does, what a witness does not do, and the overflow script for when material exceeds the exchange's scope.
- **The Partner Emergency Card.** The witness has the [Partner Emergency Card](../Resources/Partner%20Emergency%20Card.md) to hand, so the crisis route is in front of them before they ever need it.

A witness who has not done these three things can keep you company. They cannot hold your High work. That distinction is the gate, and the gate is what makes the deep work safe.

## Running the rituals on pod calls

Some of this course's pieces are built for two or more people in the same room. In a pod, they run live on a call. The call scripts are not new material to learn: they are the scripts already in the [Audio Companion Recording Kit](../Resources/Audio%20Companion%20Recording%20Kit.md), read aloud on the call instead of recorded.

- **The red-pill ceremony.** A pod can run the red-pill ceremony together at the start, each person speaking the choice out loud in turn. The script is verbatim in the Kit. One person reads the framing; each person makes their own choice, alone, in their own room, with their body present.
- **Teach-backs at Modules 3, 6, and 9.** At each teach-back module, one pod member explains the module's map to the others in their own words, using the "how to explain it verbally" material from the Map Notes. Teaching it is how you find out whether it landed.
- **The Module 8 exchange.** The listening-and-speaking exchange runs as a live pod pairing, on the call, with the Kit's script as the structure.
- **High-work witnessing.** When a pod member runs their Module 6 EHP or Module 9 Demon-locator, it happens on a call, witnessed by a qualified pod partner, never as a group performance and never over text. The witness's job is to be present, not to fix, advise, or narrate.

The Audio Companion Recording Kit holds the exact words. Treat it as your call-script book.

## Privacy: say it plainly

A private group is still on someone else's server. Telegram hosts the community, which means the operator chose where it lives and Telegram can see what passes through it. Read these as house rules, not fine print.

- **Share at the depth you choose.** The community is for Low and Medium material. High material goes to your dyad or pod call, not the board. You decide what is visible.
- **The Beep! Book stays private by default.** It is your paper notebook. Nothing in it goes to the community unless you choose to post a specific line. Staking is *chosen* exposure: you pick the one experiment you make visible, and the rest of the book stays yours.
- **Your practice data stays in your browser.** The interactive tools store everything locally, on your device, sent nowhere. Export it once a week from [the journal](../Interactive%20Tools/journal.html) so a browser-data clear cannot take your records. The community never sees this data; it cannot.

And once more, because it is the load-bearing rule: **the hard work never goes over text.** Not in a direct message, not in a pod chat, not on the board. It happens live, with a witness, in a held container. Text is for finding the witness and declaring the work, never for doing it.

## If you are solo, you lose nothing

This whole layer is optional. The solo path in [Module 00](../Days/Day%2000%20-%20Start%20Here%20and%20Getting%20Your%20Container.md) is a fully supported way to take this course, and every Low and Medium module is completely yours alone. The community changes who you do it near. It does not change the course. If a pod is not available, or not for you, the witness gate is met the same way it always was: recruit one witness, or defer the two High practices until a witness exists. You are not missing a tier. You are choosing a container that fits your life.

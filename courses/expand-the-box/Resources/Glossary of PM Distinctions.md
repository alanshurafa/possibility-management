# Glossary of Possibility Management Distinctions

*The canonical reference for every PM term used in the Expand the Box online course.*

---

## How to use this Glossary

This is the single source of truth for the vocabulary the course teaches. A facilitator can paste any entry into a cohort feed reply. A learner can look up a term they half-remember. A new contributor can confirm wording before drafting a module, post, or coaching note.

This is **not a dictionary.** PM distinctions are *tools*, not definitions. Each entry gives:

- The term, as it is named in PM
- The operational definition — what the distinction *does*, not just what it "means"
- A distinguishing note where common confusions exist — usually with another modality (Karpman, Transactional Analysis, NVC, Rogers, Gestalt, popular psychology, religious vocabulary)
- A pointer to where in the course the term is installed: *See Day NN; Map Note MNN.*
- Cross-references to related entries, in `[[bracketed]]` form

Entries are 3–8 lines. Terse. Use PM's own vocabulary — do not paraphrase a distinction into the language of another modality. That collapse is itself a Box move.

The visual companion to this Glossary is the **Periodic Table of Possibility Management Distinctions**, a chart from the PM source library (not shipped with this course) — many of the canonical terms appear there in chart form.

## The terms

### 12 Roadblocks

Thomas Gordon's catalog of twelve moves people make *instead of* listening: ordering, threatening, moralizing, advising, lecturing, judging, praising-to-control, name-calling, analyzing, sympathizing, interrogating, distracting. PM adopts the list as-is. The point is not that the moves are bad — most have a domain — but that doing any of them is *not listening*, and the one you reach for first is a free X-ray of your Box. *See Day 08; Map Note M16.*

### 4 Listeners

See [[4 Listenings]].

### 4 Listenings

Four distinct stances a listener can occupy: (1) listening to confirm what you already think, (2) listening for what the other person means, (3) listening to what is being said, (4) [[Possibility Listening]]. The first three exist in many traditions; the fourth is PM-distinctive. Not a quality scale — different listenings serve different rooms — but only the fourth opens what was not yet there. *See Day 08; Map Note M14.*

### 4 Speakings

Four locations speech is sourced from: speaking from the [[Gremlin]], speaking from the [[Box]], speaking from [[Adult Speaking|Adult]], and [[Possibility Speaking]]. You have been speaking from one of these for the last hour. The work is not "always speak from Possibility" — it is to *know which one* and to be the one choosing. *See Day 08; Map Note M15.*

### 5-minute test

The simplest field test for distinguishing [[Feeling]] from [[Emotion]]: if it is still happening after five minutes, you are in an emotion, not a clean feeling. Feelings move through in 3–5 minutes when allowed to. Anything past that has stopped flowing and is running on stored material. *See Day 05; Map Note M10.*

### Adapted Child

The variant of the [[Child (ego state)]] that learned early that being itself was unsafe — so it performs, complies, pleases, hides, or rebels to stay in the room. Often runs adult life under the cover of "maturity." Distinct from [[Free Child]], which is spontaneous and alive. *See Day 09; Map Note M17.*

### Adolescent responsibility

The second of the [[Levels of responsibility]]. Stance: *I rebel against whoever is responsible. Watch me prove them wrong.* Defiance as identity. Not "growth"; just a costume-change from Child. Common in adults who treat *radical responsibility* as the thing to rebel against. *See Day 01.*

### Adult (ego state)

The present-time grown human. Not "mature" in the moralistic sense. The state from which you hold distinctions, contract, choose on purpose, take responsibility, and act. **PM is practiced from here.** When you cannot find your Adult, you cannot do PM. Distinct from Transactional Analysis's Adult, which excludes the Gremlin and Demon as separate states; PM names those on purpose. *See Day 09; Map Note M17.*

### Adult responsibility

The third of the [[Levels of responsibility]]. Stance: *I am responsible for me. I will do my share.* Cooperative, contractual. The level most professional life runs on. Distinct from [[Radical responsibility]], which goes further — "I am at cause" rather than "I'll do my part." *See Day 01.*

### Adult Speaking

Speech that does seven things well: passes cognitive information, shares feelings, makes distinctions, declares, asks for something, agrees or disagrees, sets borders. Clear, contractual, responsible. The default speaking for most of professional life and the load-bearing speaking for most marriages. One of the [[4 Speakings]]. *See Day 08; Map Note M15.*

### Advice (in PM)

Speech that looks like [[Feedback (PM)]] but is sourced from the [[Rescuer]] — the part that cannot tolerate someone else's discomfort and wants to make it stop by fixing them. Three sentences can look identical on the page; what makes one *advice* is its source, not its words. Distinct from [[Coaching (PM)]], which offers *possibilities* the client could try, never an answer. *See Day 04.*

### Archan

The unit of value in [[Archiarchy]] — qualitative, non-material, generated through conscious presence and bright-principle action. Used in PM only loosely in this course; mentioned for vocabulary completeness. The course does not teach archan economics; the term appears in PM source material the learner will meet later.

### Archetypal body

The fifth and largest of the [[Five bodies]]: larger-than-personal patterns moving through you. Where [[Ego state|ego states]] live (Parent, Child, Adult, Gremlin, Demon) and where [[Bright principle|bright]] and [[Shadow principle|shadow]] principles operate. Fed by initiation, archetypal work, talismans, service. Distinct from Jung's *collective unconscious*: PM treats the archetypal body operationally — as a location you stand in — not as a psychic geography. *See Day 03; Map Note M06.*

### Archiarchy

The culture **generated by sourcing [[Bright principle|bright principles]]**: distributed authority, conscious relating, creating rather than dominating. The culture PM points toward. Not utopia; a different operating cosmology. Contrasted with [[Patriarchy]]. The course does not require the learner to adopt archiarchal politics — only to notice that many of their default reactions are patriarchal thoughtware, and that other moves are available once the thoughtware shifts. *See Day 10; Map Note M21.*

### Awareness vs consciousness

**Awareness** is the capacity to notice (*I am breathing; I am anxious*). **Consciousness** is awareness + the capacity to hold [[Distinction|distinctions]] and choose from them (*I am noticing anxiety, I can distinguish it from fear, and I can ground rather than react*). PM is a consciousness training, not an awareness training. Mindfulness builds the first; PM uses the first to install the second. *See Course Architecture §7.*

### Beep!

Information that something did not work — a precise design specification for the next [[Shift]], **not a verdict on the person**. The Beep does not say *you are bad*; it says *that specific thing did not produce that specific result.* Treating the Beep as a verdict is the [[Gremlin]] reading the Beep for you. *See Day 04.*

### Beep · Shift · Go

PM's [[Rapid learning]] loop. **Go** — act. **Beep!** — something did not work. **Shift** — change one specific thing. **Go** again. Most learners break the loop in three places: not Go-ing (sitting in head considering whether the thing might work), treating the Beep as a verdict, or Shifting vaguely (*I'll be better next time* is not a Shift). *See Day 04.*

### Beep! Book

A literal notebook. Engineering log for [[Experiment|experiments]], [[Beep!|Beeps]], [[Shift|Shifts]], and noticings. Not a journal — no reflection prose, no self-talk. Date-stamped reps. Carried for the duration of the course and ideally beyond. *See Day 04.*

### Being

What you actually are, prior to the [[Box]]. Often experienced as still, curious, present, slower, more space. Not always locatable, and that's normal. Distinct from the Box (survival/protection thoughtware) and from the [[Gremlin]] (drama-eating part). You have a Box. You have a Gremlin. You are neither. You are Being, which has both. *See Day 02; Course Architecture §7.*

### Box

The survival-and-protection thoughtware you assembled in childhood. **What kept you alive then.** Also what you have been identified with — most adults, asked "who are you?", accurately describe their Box. Not the enemy. Designed for an environment that is no longer here. The single most important PM move: *you have a Box; you are not your Box.* Distinct from "limiting beliefs" (too small — beliefs are content; Box is thoughtware) and from "shadow self" (different lineage — Jungian shadow is repressed material; PM Box is operational survival code). *See Day 02; Map Note M04.*

### Box detection

The core skill of [[Day 02]]: catching, in real time, that you are reacting from a rule set by something that already happened. Most of the time you catch it *after* — three minutes, an hour, the next morning. Fine. The reps compound. Three signals make catching easier: the body signal (a familiar tightening), the script (sentences that come out with no apparent author), the predictability (same fight, same shape). *See Day 02; Map Note M04.*

### Box layers

Three depths the Box organizes itself in. **Surface** — behaviors, habits, automatic reactions (most visible). **Story** — beliefs, rules, narratives the surface rests on. **Survival** — identity-level commitments formed early in response to threat, where the Box says *if I let this go I will die*. Day 2 stays at surface and story on purpose; survival material needs the equipment from Days 3, 5, 6, 9 before it is safe to work. *See Day 02; Course Architecture §5.*

### Bright principle

A principle whose sourcing **generates [[Archiarchy]]**: love, integrity, possibility, courage, presence, clarity, creation, devotion, truth-telling, witnessing, holding-space, hospitality, gratitude. The principle is upstream; the culture is downstream. Structural, not moral — bright is not "good"; bright is *generative of consciousness*. A [[Declaration]] only holds when sourced from a bright principle; sourced from shadow it collapses. Distinct from "values" in the soft modern sense — values are descriptions of preference; principles *have hold of you* during action. *See Day 10; Map Note M21.*

### Bubble

An arm's-length energetic sphere declared around you, including under your feet and above your head. **A distinction, not a protection.** Does not block anything. Clarifies *what is yours and what is not.* Most adults walk with the bubble too big (enmesh with everyone), too small (collapsed into the head), or too porous (pick up every charge in the room and call it theirs). A clean bubble lets you be in contact without merging. *See Day 03; Map Note M07.*

### Causal paradigm

The thoughtware that explains the present by reference to the past: *I am like this because of my childhood / my trauma / my conditioning.* Descriptively useful; developmentally honest; also a closed loop where every present moment is sourced from a fixed past. The default thoughtware of most therapy. Distinct from the [[Possibility paradigm]], which does not deny the past but relocates the source of action from past-as-cause to context-as-source. *See Course Architecture §2.*

### Center

The energetic location in your body (typically the lower belly / hara; for some, the heart) **from which your choices, your will, and your authority come.** Not a metaphor. You do not pick where it is — you find where it actually is. When you are not centered, your authority is somewhere else: in the other person's reaction, in your phone, in yesterday's conversation, and the [[Box]] runs the show. *See Day 03; Map Note M07.*

### Centering

Moving your attention — by intention, not visualisation — to where your authority actually lives, and remaining there. You will not stay. You will center, take three breaths, and discover thirty seconds later you have left. Not failure. **The rep is *noticing you have left and returning*.** *See Day 03; Map Note M07.*

### Child (ego state)

Your actual childhood self, still alive in your nervous system. Two sub-states: [[Free Child]] (spontaneous, curious, alive) and [[Adapted Child]] (the version that learned to perform or hide to survive). Distinct from Transactional Analysis's Child state in that PM names the Adapted Child as *the version that often runs adult life* — not a developmental stage left behind. *See Day 09; Map Note M17.*

### Child responsibility

The first of the [[Levels of responsibility]]. Stance: *Someone else is responsible. I am acted upon.* Phrases: "It's not fair." "They did this to me." "I have no choice." Not about chronological age — said by 45-year-olds daily. *See Day 01.*

### Choosing (Power of)

The first of [[The Three Powers]]. The radically responsible move of declaring "**I am the one who chose this**" even when the choice was made on autopilot. You did not choose your first reaction; you can choose to own that you reacted, and choose your second move on purpose. The choice is to **own the choice**. Without this, the other two powers do not exist. Distinct from "free will" debates — PM makes no metaphysical claim; the Power is an *operational* move that makes the next action available. *See Day 10; Map Note M23.*

### Coaching (PM)

Future-oriented offer of *possibilities* the client could try in service of a different result. **Does not solve the client's problem.** A PM coach who tells you the answer has made themselves your authority and undone the work. A PM coach who hands you fifteen things to try has handed you back your own life. Distinct from [[Feedback (PM)]] (past-oriented). Distinct from "life coaching" in the popular sense, which often substitutes the coach's plan for the client's exploration. *See Day 04.*

### Cohort feed

The forum, group voice thread, or shared channel where learners post one-line reflections, questions, and witness each other's process. Lightly moderated by the [[Community Manager (CM)|CM]]. Not the container for EHP content or partner-exchange material. Open during the cohort run; archives 14 days after the closing post. *See Course Architecture §"Cohort design"; Safety Framework §K.*

### Community Manager (CM)

The non-clinical role that holds the operational container of a cohort. Triages cohort-feed posts, maintains the [[Referral packet|referral list]], re-matches partners when pairings drop out, and applies the [[Triage rubric (Green / Yellow / Red)|triage rubric]]. Does **not** provide coaching or therapy. CM reachable by DM within 24 hours for routine messages; same-day for Red flags. Has their own supervisor or peer to debrief with. *See Safety Framework §G; Gatekeeping Protocol §A–F.*

### Completion loop

The five-step discipline that closes a communication so it leaves no residue. (1) Speaker speaks. (2) Listener reflects back, in their own words, the part that landed. (3) Speaker confirms or corrects — loop until clean. (4) Listener acknowledges (*I got it* — receipt, not agreement). (5) Both name the conversation as complete, out loud: *Are we complete on this? — Yes.* Step 5 is the PM-distinctive piece; without it even a beautifully paraphrased exchange leaves [[Energetic debt]]. *See Day 08; Map Note M16.*

### Conscious feelings work

Feeling on purpose, at chosen intensity, for chosen duration, in service of what you actually care about. Distinct from "expressing your feelings" (often unprocessed emotion dumped on a present-day person) and from suppression. Day 5 installs the map; Day 6 begins the practice. *See Day 05; Day 06; Map Note M10.*

### Consciousness

See [[Awareness vs consciousness]].

### Consent check

The short prompt at the top of every [[Intensity tags|High-intensity]] module: partner reachable, 90 uninterrupted minutes, not in acute crisis or under the influence, knows how to ground, knows how to reach the CM. **Not bureaucratic** — the consent moment that distinguishes a chosen practice from a thing happening to the learner. Consent given at enrollment does not carry forward into every exercise. *See Safety Framework §I.*

### Content

What is happening *inside* the [[Context]] — the topic, the facts, the words, the substance. Distinct from context (the source-frame the content is happening inside). Same content in two contexts means two different things. Most arguments are about content; most lasting change is about context. *See Day 01.*

### Context

In ordinary usage *context* means *surroundings*. In PM, **context is the invisible source-frame that determines what actions, meanings, choices, and results are even available.** Upstream of mindset, beliefs, and intentions. Two people in the same [[Content]] can be in radically different contexts and produce radically different lives. The leftmost link of [[The chain]] and the most leveraged place to intervene. Distinct from "context" in linguistics or pragmatics; PM context is structural, not situational. *See Day 01; Map Note M01.*

### Criticism (in PM)

Speech shaped like [[Feedback (PM)|feedback]] but sourced from the [[Gremlin]] — wants the other to feel smaller so the speaker can feel bigger. Three sentences can look identical on the page; what makes one *criticism* is the source, not the words. Contracts the receiver in the body. *See Day 04.*

### Critical Parent

Sub-state of the [[Parent (ego state)|Parent ego state]]: the internalized judging voice. *"You should…", "Good people don't…", "That's not how this is done."* Most learners discover they have been running Critical Parent on themselves daily — often while claiming Adult. Useful in narrow domains (correcting a child reaching for a hot stove); tyrannical when it is the only Parent voice available. *See Day 09; Map Note M17.*

### Dangerous question

A question whose answer leads somewhere the asker cannot un-know. *What would I have to be willing to feel to leave this job? What am I pretending not to know? Who would I be if I dropped this story about myself?* Safe questions return information that doesn't change anything. Dangerous questions dissolve a floor so a new floor can appear. The second of [[The Three Powers]]. Asked of yourself with [[Liquid state]] and [[Holding context]] — engine of possibility. Asked of another without consent — violence. The danger is in the answer, not in the asking; the responsibility is in the conditions. *See Day 10; Map Notes M20, M23.*

### Declaration

A speech act that brings a new context into being. *I am the author of this conversation. This relationship is now over. I am a person who tells the truth.* **Not a promise** (content — "I will do X by Friday"). **Not an opinion** (reactive — "I think X is true"). **Not a wish** (passive — "I hope X happens"). Only holds when sourced from a [[Bright principle]]; sourced from shadow it produces dissonance and collapses, usually within hours. Volume is not the marker — *"I am willing to be wrong about this,"* said quietly, can be a more powerful declaration than anything shouted. Distinct from "affirmations" — affirmations are wishes wearing declaration grammar. *See Day 10; Map Note M23.*

### Declaring (Power of)

The third of [[The Three Powers]]: the move of speaking a [[Declaration]] into being. Sequential — requires [[Choosing (Power of)|owning the choice]] and [[Dangerous question|asking the dangerous question]] first. Without those, "I declare" is grandiosity wearing PM vocabulary. With them, "I declare" creates. *See Day 10; Map Note M23.*

### Demon

The [[Ego state]] with the **capacity for real harm** — to self, to others, to what you have built. **Not a metaphor.** Not a sacred shadow. Not a creative force. Every adult has one. The work is **not to integrate the Demon**; the work is to *know* it — its texture, its triggers, its preferred targets — so that when it stirs, you can name it and not let it drive. PM uses the word *Demon* on purpose — chosen because softer words ("shadow," "dark side," "inner critic") let the learner pretend the part is less dangerous than it is. Distinct from religious usage: PM makes no metaphysical or moral claim, only an operational one. A learner who finishes Day 9 saying *"I have integrated my shadow"* is being run by the part they think they have transcended. *See Day 09; Map Note M17.*

### Disidentification

The move from *I am this* to *I have this.* The grammatical shift is the energetic shift. *I am angry* puts you inside; *I have anger right now* puts you outside, looking at it. The first PM move and the **precondition for [[Liquid state]]**. Disidentification is **not destruction** — you will likely have your [[Box]] for the rest of your life. The work is to **stop confusing it for yourself**, so the choice the Box never had becomes available. *See Day 02; Map Note M04.*

### Distinction

A perceptual cut — once seen, it changes what's available without requiring any "skill." Examples: *feeling vs emotion · box vs you · low drama vs responsible game · is-glue vs has-glue*. PM does not give "techniques" the learner masters; it gives distinctions the learner uses. A distinction lives in all five bodies, not just the intellectual. *See Course Architecture §"Pedagogical premise."*

### Ego state

A coherent location from which you speak, feel, and choose. **Not a personality.** A *state* you are in for the duration of a sentence, a phone call, a decade. PM names five: [[Parent (ego state)|Parent]] (with Critical and Nurturing sub-states), [[Child (ego state)|Child]] (with Free and Adapted sub-states), [[Adult (ego state)|Adult]], [[Gremlin]], and [[Demon]]. You move in and out of all five, sometimes within one sentence. Related to Transactional Analysis's three-state model (Parent / Adult / Child) but **not the same map** — TA does not include the Gremlin or the Demon. PM names those on purpose; without them the learner mistakes gremlin food for adult choice and shadow material for personality. *See Day 09; Map Note M17.*

### Emotion

A **past-time, mixed, story-laden state.** A [[Feeling]] that arose earlier, was not allowed to move, got stored, and is now running on autopilot — often triggered by something in the present that resembles the past. Lasts longer than five minutes. Mixes with other feelings. Comes with a story. Does not take responsible action; repeats. Distinct from feeling in the popular sense (where the two are used interchangeably). PM separates them on purpose because the confusion is the single most common error in emotional work. *See Day 05; Map Note M10.*

### Emotional body

The second of the [[Five bodies]]: present-time feelings (anger, sadness, fear, joy) as sensations in the chest, belly, throat, soft places. Fed by naming, by letting feeling move, by being witnessed. The body where [[Feeling|feelings]] live (and where stored [[Emotion|emotions]] wait). Worked directly in Days 5 and 6. *See Day 03; Map Note M06.*

### Emotional Healing Process (EHP)

The structured PM practice for completing a stored emotion so its energy returns to you. **Six positions** (Position 0 through Position 5): set foundation · make contact · navigate to liquid state · in liquid state, work the original event (block / old decision–new decision / unmix and process) · start closing · the end. Non-linear; no script — a moment-to-moment discovery journey. Requires a [[Witness]] for anything beyond small material. **Scope:** ordinary stored emotional residue. **Not for trauma** — trauma requires a qualified clinician with a trauma-built protocol (EMDR, somatic experiencing, TF-CBT, IFS, sensorimotor). Distinct from catharsis (uncontained discharge) and from "expressing emotion" (which acts emotion out *at* a person and creates damage). *See Day 06; Map Note M12.*

### Energetic body

The fourth of the [[Five bodies]]: the subtle field around and through you — presence, charge, contraction, expansion, the "feel" of you in a room. Fed by [[Centering]], [[Grounding cord|grounding]], [[Bubble]] work, and attention. The body the [[Day 03]] energetic-body practices are training. *See Day 03; Map Notes M06, M07.*

### Energetic debt

The accumulated weight of incomplete communications. Observable, not metaphorical — walk into a room where two people have been silently carrying a three-year-old unfinished conversation and you can feel it before either speaks. **Gremlin food.** Paid down by [[Completion loop|completion loops]], including retroactive ones. *See Day 08; Map Note M16.*

### Experiment

The operational unit of PM practice. A small, specific, time-bounded test of new thoughtware in real life. Three required parts: **what I will do** (one specific behavior, in a real situation) · **by when** (a specific window, not "this week") · **what I will notice** (the data). One rep. Reps compound. Thirty in a row change how you are with your father, even when any single rep looks like a failure. Insight does not install thoughtware; reps do. *See Day 04.*

### Expressing vs processing

The load-bearing distinction of Day 6. **Expressing** = acting a feeling out at a person — yelling, crying at, dumping. Expressing a stored [[Emotion]] *creates damage* because the emotion has nothing to do with the person in front of you. **Processing** = completing a stored emotion through the [[Emotional Healing Process (EHP)|EHP]], with a [[Witness]], in a held container. Different practices. Different rooms. PM separates them on purpose. *See Day 06; Map Note M12.*

### Fantasy vs possibility

Fantasy is escape from "what is" with no enterable threshold — daydream, someday-when, the place you go to rest from reality. It never produces action. [[Possibility (as space)|Possibility]] is a real zone you stand in where action originates. The body knows the difference: fantasy floats free of the floor; possibility puts weight on it. *See Day 10; Map Note M20.*

### Feedback (PM)

Information about *how a person was* when they did what they did — presence, body-state, honesty, location-of-self while speaking. **Not about content.** Sourced from the [[Witness]] (the part that just observed without verdict). Given only inside a consenting transformational container — outside one, even skillful feedback lands as hostile. Distinct from [[Criticism (in PM)|criticism]] (sourced from Gremlin) and [[Advice (in PM)|advice]] (sourced from Rescuer). Distinct from corporate "feedback culture," which is mostly content notes. *See Day 04.*

### Feeling

A **present-time archetypal energy** in the emotional body. One of [[Four feelings|four]]: anger, sadness, fear, joy. **3–5 minutes at full intensity, then it passes.** Informational — tells you what you care about and what to do next. Clean — no story, no interpretation. Each takes a responsible action: *Anger says make a boundary. Sadness says let this go. Fear says prepare. Joy says more of this.* Distinct from [[Emotion]] (past-time, mixed, stored) and from "feelings" in popular usage (where the word is used for any internal weather). The discipline of the four is what makes the work clean. *See Day 05; Map Note M10.*

### Feelings Form

The daily instrument installed at Module 5: one row per day, filled in the morning sit — a 0–10 bar reading for each of the [[Four feelings]], a mixed check, a numbness check, one line. From Module 8 the row carries one [[Completion loop|completion-loop]] line item. The 30-day spine of rows is how a learner reads their own [[Numbness Bar]] moving; the trend is evidence no insight substitutes for. Spec and printable form: `Practice/Feelings Form.md`. *See Module 05; Daily Practice Spine.*

### Fighting reality

The first move of every [[Low drama]]. The internal sentence: *whatever is happening should not be happening.* The traffic. The colleague. The diagnosis. The weather. Once you object to the fact of what is, you enter the [[Low Drama Triangle|triangle]]. The [[Responsible Game]] does not require you to like what is happening — it requires you to stop arguing with the fact of it. *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### Five bodies

What an actual human is, in PM: [[Intellectual body|intellectual]] (brain, words, plans), [[Emotional body|emotional]] (feelings as sensation), [[Physical body|physical]] (bones, muscles, breath, gut), [[Energetic body|energetic]] (subtle field around and through you), [[Archetypal body|archetypal]] (larger-than-personal patterns). The intellectual body is the loudest for most modern adults; the other four run on emergency power. A distinction lives in all five bodies; experiencing a distinction means registering it across multiple bodies, not understanding it intellectually. *See Day 03; Map Note M06.*

### Four feelings

Anger, sadness, fear, joy. Treated as **archetypal energies** with characteristic body location, purpose, asked-for action, and shadow form when stored. **Four. Not five. Not eight.** "Love" is not a feeling — it is an archetypal force much larger than the emotional body. "Frustration," "overwhelm," "stress," "burnout" are not feelings — they are stories about mixed emotions. The discipline of the four is what makes the work clean. *See Day 05; Map Note M10.*

### Four working agreements

The four things a learner agrees to on entering the course. (1) **Choice is always available** — pause, skip, slow down, stop. (2) **Consent is ongoing** — consent at enrollment does not carry into every exercise. (3) **The learner is responsible for their own state** — uses the grounding script and safety practices. (4) **The buddy/partner is alive** — confirmed reachable before Days 5, 6, 7, 9. Structural, not aspirational. *See Safety Framework §B.*

### Free and Natural Adult

The Adult who has un-installed the principles that were given to them in childhood and **chooses, action by action, which principle is in the driver's seat now.** Not a finished state — a stance. The Free and Natural Adult is who PM trains the learner to become; the [[Trainer Path]] formalizes the practice. *See Day 10; Map Note M21.*

### Free Child

Sub-state of the [[Child (ego state)|Child ego state]]: spontaneous, curious, alive — the part that delights, plays, sulks, dances without shame. Distinct from [[Adapted Child]], which performs safety. Many learners arrive disconnected from Free Child and discover it in the Day 9 [[Ego state]] Locator. *See Day 09; Map Note M17.*

### Gameworld

A bounded space the learner enters by choice, with explicit rules, defined roles, agreements, consequences, a duration, and a boundary. The Expand the Box course **is** a gameworld — calling it that on purpose. Tells the learner that the rules of this space differ from the rules of their default life, and that they are entering an explicit, finite, agreed container — not joining a cult, not becoming a "PM person." Distinct from "gamification" in the consumer sense; PM gameworld theory comes out of Clinton Callahan's later work on parallel cultures. *See Course Architecture §"Gameworld"; Day 10.*

### Giving your center away

The PM name for what most adults do most of the time: handing authority to whoever in the room seems to have it. Asking permission you do not need to ask. Pre-emptively softening your answer. Once seen, seen everywhere. *See Day 03; Map Note M07.*

### Go

The act in [[Beep · Shift · Go]]. **Actually doing the thing.** Sitting in your head considering whether the thing might work is not Go. The actual conversation, the actual message, the actual rep is Go. The part of the loop most often skipped. *See Day 04.*

### Golden cube / workspace

A portable, **declared** creative space — a cube of light, roughly the size of a small room, opened in front of you when you have something specific to construct or test. Used to try on a distinction, run an experiment in imagination, examine a piece of your Box from outside, or test new thoughtware before bringing it into your life. **Workspace, not refuge.** Repeatable. By Day 10 it should be openable in seconds. *See Day 03; Map Note M07.*

### Gremlin

The part of you that wants [[Gremlin food]] — drama, suffering, righteousness, distraction. Distinct from the [[Box]] (which keeps you safe) and from [[Being]] (what you actually are). **Hungrier than the Box, more cunning, louder.** Part of the system — naming the Gremlin is not condemnation. You have a Gremlin. You are not your Gremlin. The Gremlin will be with you for life. The work is to know its diet and to catch it in the act of being fed. Also one of the five [[Ego state|ego states]]; speaks *sotto voce* and often passes for "what I really feel." *See Day 07; Day 09; Map Notes M13, M17.*

### Gremlin food

What drama is actually for — the emotional/energetic substances the Gremlin eats. Being right. Being wronged. Being special. Being misunderstood. Being a victim. Being a savior. Being above it all. Being beneath it all. Most people have a primary food, two or three secondary, and one they are deeply uncomfortable admitting they enjoy. **The uncomfortable one is usually the most operational.** *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### Grounding cord

An **energetic line declared** — by intention, not visualisation — from your [[Center]] to the centre of the earth. Ship's-rope diameter, not wire. Two functions: **discharge** (energy not yours drains down so you do not carry the room home) and **stability** (when intensity rises, the cord lets you stay in your body rather than fly out). Not insulation. Not protection. A path for energy that needs a place to go. *See Day 03; Map Note M07.*

### Grounding script

The 60-second universal action available at the top of every module file. Stop the module. Stand. Three exhales longer than inhales. Name three things you can see, one thing you can hear. One hand on sternum, one on belly. Decide: continue, pause ten minutes, or stop for today. **Default action whenever uncertain.** *See Safety Framework §D.*

### High drama

Taking **radical responsibility at gameworld scale**: consciously sourcing [[Bright principle|bright principles]] to hold and shift what happens in a whole field of commitment — a team, a family, a culture, a [[Gameworld]]. The course's own map M20 places high drama under **Conscious Purpose**, in the [[Responsible Game]]. [[Low drama]] is unconscious purpose at kitchen scale; high drama is conscious purpose at world scale. Not [[Gremlin food]] — only low drama feeds the Gremlin. High drama is the *destination* of the drama work, not a variant of the disease. *See Day 07; Maps/M20.*

### High drama vs low drama

The axis is purpose and scale, not volume. [[Low drama]] runs on unconscious purpose, below the threshold of noticing, at kitchen scale, and produces [[Gremlin food]]. [[High drama]] runs on conscious purpose, sourced from bright principles, at gameworld scale, and feeds nothing to the Gremlin. The drama work moves a person from the first toward the second. *See Day 07; Maps/M20.*

### Holding context

The structural conditions inside which a [[Liquid state]] becomes *transformative* rather than merely spectacular. Names what is happening, supplies distinctions for new thoughtware to take shape around, gives the learner equipment to stay present, closes the container before they leave. This course is one holding context. A competent therapy, an in-person ETB, a [[Possibility Lab]] — also holding contexts. Substance-induced liquid states without a holding context are spectacles, not upgrades. *See Day 03; Map Note M05.*

### I have a Box / I am not my Box

The disidentification sentence. The grammar matters. *I am* puts you inside the Box, looking out through it. *I have* puts you outside, looking at it. Said out loud, in the body, when the Box is being seen. The single most-used sentence in [[Day 02]]. *See Day 02; Map Note M04.*

### I-statement / you-statement

The field test for [[Problem ownership]]. *"I am angry. I want a clearer agreement"* owns the problem. *"You're being difficult. You always do this"* misowns it. The grammar is the giveaway. *You*-statements are almost always misowned problems wearing a costume. *See Day 09; Map Note M18.*

### Intake screens (the five)

Five gate questions a learner answers before course access opens: (1) Suicidality. (2) Trauma & dissociation. (3) Active addiction or compulsive behavior. (4) Coercive or unsafe environment. (5) Mania, psychosis, spiritual emergency. Each has a specific question, a threshold, and an action (defer, CM consult, proceed with conditions, proceed). **The screening is the gate, not a recommendation.** *See Gatekeeping Protocol §A.*

### Intellectual body

The first of the [[Five bodies]]: thoughts, concepts, language, planning. The loudest body for most modern adults — and the body most likely to swallow PM into "interesting ideas I now know about." Every module engages at least one other body on purpose so the learning lands in the place where new behavior originates. *See Day 03; Map Note M06.*

### Intensity tags

Each module is tagged **Low** (Days 1, 2, 3, 4, 10), **Medium** (Day 8), or **High** (Days 5, 6, 7, 9). The tag influences pacing and partner requirements. High modules have required pre- and post-partner check-ins and are gated by the [[Unlock checklist]]. *See Safety Framework §C.*

### Is-glue

The linguistic mechanism by which the Box defends itself: the verb *to be* + a noun, used to fuse a transient perception to a permanent identity. *I AM stupid. She IS controlling. He IS a narcissist. This IS impossible.* Once said, the noun becomes the apparent reality. Every subsequent observation is filtered through it; confirming evidence is admitted, contradicting evidence is invisible. The Box has used a two-letter verb to close the room. *See Map Note M22.*

### Is-glue dissolver

The simple linguistic swap that restores a perception's provisional status without softening it. Replace *is* with *feels*, *appears to be*, *right now*, *in this moment*, *from my Box*, *as I am perceiving it*. *"I AM stupid"* → *"I am noticing a thought that I am stupid right now, from my Box."* The perception is preserved; the metaphysical claim is withdrawn. **Not hedging or appeasement** — those are different Box moves. The dissolver is precise. *See Map Note M22.*

### Karpman triangle

Stephen Karpman's drama triangle (Transactional Analysis, 1968) — Persecutor, Victim, Rescuer, rotation between them. PM's [[Low Drama Triangle]] uses the same shape but a different mechanism: in Karpman the roles are *dysfunctional patterns* to escape; in PM they are **gremlin food production** to see and starve. Different exit. If Karpman is already in the learner's vocabulary, hold the two frames side by side; do not collapse them. *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### Learning Spiral

The actual shape of integration over time. **Try → notice → name → adjust → try again.** Each lap, more becomes visible — including things you could not see the first time because the first lap installed the distinction that lets you see them. The course itself has been a spiral; PM practice continues to spiral for years. **Not linear self-improvement.** The spiral does not converge on mastery; it deepens. *See Day 09; Map Note M19.*

### Levels of responsibility

PM distinguishes four levels — [[Child responsibility|child]] · [[Adolescent responsibility|adolescent]] · [[Adult responsibility|adult]] · [[Radical responsibility|radical]]. Readable as developmental stages **and** as moves available at any moment. In any conversation you are speaking from one of these. *See Day 01.*

### Linear vs nonlinear possibility

**Linear possibility** is more, better, or different of what already exists — reachable by ordinary steps from the current context. **Nonlinear possibility** does not extend from the current situation at all; it becomes available only by shifting context, and arrives as a discontinuity, not an improvement. The [[Map of Possibility]] work is about the second kind. *See Module 10; Maps/M44.*

### Liquid state

The temporary condition in which solid thoughtware loosens and new thoughtware can be installed. Symptomatic across all five bodies: confusion (intellectual), intensity (emotional), fatigue/restlessness (physical), disorientation (energetic), upheaval (archetypal). **Not every liquid state is transformative** — substance-, exhaustion-, or rupture-induced liquid states without a [[Holding context]] dissipate without changing thoughtware. Transformative only when held by a context that names what is happening, supplies distinctions, provides equipment, and closes the container. *See Day 03; Map Note M05.*

### Low drama

The daily-life, sub-threshold kind of drama — sigh, eye-roll, "I had to," "you always," the rehearsed internal monologue in the shower, the complaint made for the fortieth time about the same person. **Low** not because the stakes are low (over years they are enormous; most relationships die from it) but because it operates **below the threshold of noticing**. Distinct from [[High drama]], which is conscious purpose at gameworld scale — the destination of the drama work, not its target. Low drama is the kind PM goes after on Day 7. *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### Low Drama Triangle

Three positions of one [[Box]]-pattern, rotating through three faces and producing [[Gremlin food]]. [[Persecutor]] (attacks, blames, makes wrong). [[Victim]] (collapses, controls through helplessness). [[Rescuer]] (steps in unasked, builds dependency). **Not three personality types** — one machine, three costumes; a person flips between them inside a single conversation. Can also run entirely between parts of yourself — see [[Telenovela with yourself]]. Distinct from [[Karpman triangle]] (same shape, different mechanism). *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### Low-intensity feelings

Feelings registered at 2–20% — the almost-imperceptible kind that arrive in real time, give you information, and let you respond before the situation has compounded into [[Emotion]]. **The most useful kind in real life.** A 5% anger noticed when a colleague crosses a line lets you say *"that one I want to come back to."* A 70% anger noticed three weeks later in your kitchen at your partner is an EHP waiting to happen. The Day 5 practice works the low end on purpose — the rep that lowers the [[Numbness Bar]]. *See Day 05; Map Note M10.*

### Map is not the territory

Borrowed from Korzybski, used in PM with discipline: **no PM map is *true*.** The [[Box]] is not a literal box in your head. Anyone who treats a PM map as doctrine has already turned it into a Box. Maps are tools, tried on like a t-shirt; if a map doesn't fit, take it off. *See Day 02; Map Note M03.*

### Map of Communication

The architecture of any exchange: **intention → encoding → speech → reception → decoding → response.** Five named joins where gaps can open. **Intention gap** (speaker not clear *why*). **Encoding gap** (clear on why, no words). **Reception gap** (listener running confirming-listening). **Decoding gap** (same words, different meanings). **Response gap** (responding to a constructed version). After three turns, two parallel monologues. Distinct from Shannon–Weaver communication theory: same circuit, different account of what determines whether the gaps open (the [[4 Listenings]] and [[4 Speakings]]). *See Day 08; Map Note M16.*

### Map of Possibility

The meta-map. **Possibility is not a list of options the world hands you; possibility is a space you enter on purpose.** Empty until someone steps into it. Entered by three keys held simultaneously: a [[Holding context|held context]], [[Liquid state]], and a [[Dangerous question]]. Every other PM map has a location on this one — the Box is the boundary you came in through; liquid state is the threshold; the four feelings are the energetic vehicles inside. Distinct from "manifestation" — the space delivers nothing; it makes new action available, which then operates by ordinary cause and effect. *See Day 10; Map Note M20.*

### Matrix

Your matrix is the **energetic structure you build, through practice, that determines how much consciousness you can hold**. Distinctions, feelings at higher intensity, responsibility at larger scale: each requires matrix to hold it. Matrix is built through conscious practice: reps, feelings work, holding attention, taking on responsibility slightly beyond current capacity. Thoughtware upgrades install onto matrix; without enough matrix, an upgrade has nowhere to live. Secondary sense, always written with a qualifier and never as bare "matrix": **the cultural matrix** — the default cosmology a person is raised inside (for most learners, the patriarchal matrix; see [[Patriarchy]]). Distinct from the film reference, though the film provided the [[Red pill / blue pill]] metaphor PM uses. *See Module 03 (the matrix-building section); Day 10; Maps/M46.*

### Meta-conversation

A conversation **about** the conversation. The single most useful move when a communication is stuck. *"I'm going to pause here. I'm going to have a meta-conversation with you."* Pulls back from content so the structure becomes visible, then both people choose where to re-enter cleanly. *See Day 08; Map Note M16.*

### Mixed emotion

A stored state that is part [[Feeling]], part memory, part story — two or three feelings tangled with the past event that froze them. **The default adult condition**, not the exception. Examples: collapse/hopelessness (sadness + fear); resentment (anger + sadness); anxiety/defensive rage (fear + anger); nostalgia (joy + sadness); "stress" (fear + anger + sadness, all numbed). Not on the list: *frustration, overwhelm, burnout, trigger* — those are stories about mixed emotions. The discipline of returning to the four is what makes the work possible. *See Day 06; Map Note M11.*

### My Map Book

The course's completion artifact. After each module's Close-the-loop, the learner writes one entry: one distinction in their own words plus one lived example of their own — twelve entries by the end, exchanged with the pairing partner at Module 11. It replaces certificates: a certificate records attendance; the Map Book records installed thoughtware in the learner's own vocabulary. Spec and template: `Practice/My Map Book.md`. *See Module 11.*

### New Map of Feelings

The PM map that replaces the [[Old Map of Feelings]]: four archetypal energies (anger, sadness, fear, joy), each with body location, purpose, asked-for action, and [[Shadow form (of a feeling)|shadow form]] when stored. None of the four is good or bad; each is a tool. *See Day 05; Map Note M10.*

### No "we" in responsibility

The precise claim: responsibility is **individual**. *"We have a problem"* hides whose [[Adult (ego state)|Adult]] is doing the work. The clean form: *I am responsible, and you are responsible, and we are choosing to collaborate.* The word *we* sounds inclusive and is often a slippage — it lets one person's Adult do the work while the other's Adapted Child watches. *See Day 09; Map Note M18.*

### Numbness Bar

A threshold in the [[Emotional body]] below which feelings do not register. Installed in childhood (often before age six) as protection — the feelings of the moment were too big, no one helped process them, so the bar cut them off so the child could keep functioning. **Intelligent then. Obsolete now.** Most adults live with the bar at 70–90% numb — experiencing 10–30% of their emotional body's signal and calling that their full range. The bar **lowers** through repeated low-intensity work — feeling 5%, then 10%, then 20% on purpose, in a held container. *See Day 05; Map Note M09.*

### Nurturing Parent

Sub-state of the [[Parent (ego state)|Parent ego state]]: the internalized helper voice. *"Let me take care of that for you."* Useful when actually nurturing a child (or someone in genuine acute need). Tyrannical when it is the only Parent voice available — produces compulsive helping, rescuing, [[Rescuer]] patterns. *See Day 09; Map Note M17.*

### Old Map of Feelings

The patriarchal-paradigm map most learners arrived with: feelings sorted into *good* and *bad*, three of four forbidden outright, the fourth (joy) diluted. Anger = dangerous, swallow it. Sadness = weakness, get over it. Fear = cowardice, hide it. Joy = self-indulgent, tone it down. **No good feelings** — only a thin permitted band of muted states ("fine," "good," "not too bad"). Maintained through numbness — the numbness is not a side effect, it is the load-bearing mechanism. *See Day 05; Map Note M08.*

### Over-identification with one body

The default condition most learners arrive in: running adult life from a single [[Five bodies|body]] (usually intellectual) while the other four operate on emergency power. The course is a recalibration. *See Day 03; Map Note M06.*

### Pairing partner

The other learner the course matches each learner with for the duration. Exchanges happen via voice message or scheduled async call. Structurally essential — several modules (5, 6, 7, 9) require the partner to be reachable before the module unlocks. The pairing is bounded by the [[Partner agreement]]. Not a romantic, financial, or professional relationship. *See Course Architecture §"Cohort design"; Gatekeeping Protocol §B.*

### Parent (ego state)

The internalized voices of authority — your literal parents plus teachers, religion, culture, bosses whose voice you absorbed. Two sub-states: [[Critical Parent]] (judges) and [[Nurturing Parent]] (takes care of). Both useful in their place; both tyrannical when one is the only state available. *See Day 09; Map Note M17.*

A learner who will not acknowledge the immediate-danger clause is not eligible for the course. *See Gatekeeping Protocol §B.*

### Patriarchy

The culture **generated by sourcing [[Shadow principle|shadow principles]]**: hierarchical, fear-based, control-oriented, built on numbness. Also the default cultural matrix most learners were raised inside. The principle is upstream; the culture is downstream. Contrasted with [[Archiarchy]]. **Not a moral indictment** — a structural description of the cosmology most contemporary adults' nervous systems were installed inside. *See Day 10; Map Note M21.*

### Persecutor

[[Low Drama Triangle]] position: *"It's your fault. Let me show you why."* Attacks, blames, controls, criticizes, makes wrong. Energetically big — chest out, jaw set, voice over the top. Food: being **right**, being **superior**, being **the one who sees clearly**. Does not require yelling — the most refined Persecutors do it quietly: the precisely-worded email, the calm comparison to a sibling. *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### Physical body

The third of the [[Five bodies]]: bones, muscles, breath, gut, skin. Fed by sleep, food, movement, touch. The body the embodied practices in every module engage. *See Day 03; Map Note M06.*

### Possibilitator

The PM-trained facilitator — a graduate of the [[Trainer Path]] qualified to deliver ETB and other PM trainings. **Not the same as a coach or therapist.** A Possibilitator holds the gameworld of a PM training; clients enter the gameworld by consent for the duration of the work. Not all coaches who use PM vocabulary are Possibilitators; the path is multi-year and explicit. *Not taught in this course; referenced as a post-course pathway.*

### Possibility (as space)

Not a list of options the world hands you. **A space you enter on purpose.** Empty until someone steps in. Once inside, what was previously invisible becomes available — not because reality changed, but because the [[Box]] stopped filtering it. See [[Map of Possibility]]. Distinct from "possibility" in casual usage ("there's a possibility that…") — PM possibility is a *location*, not a probability. *See Day 10; Map Note M20.*

### Possibility Lab

A periodic in-person or online lab where PM distinctions deepen through facilitated practice. The most common next step after ETB. *Referenced in Day 10 "What's next."*

### Possibility Listening

The fourth of the [[4 Listenings]]. You stop listening *for* anything. You become a [[Vacuum listening space|vacuum]] on which the speaker can lay out thinking, sensing and feeling without it needing to be reasonable, logical or understandable. **No nodding. No smiling. No yes-saying** (in PM's analysis, those train the speaker to stay in the parts you are comfortable with). A small neutral sound confirms presence. Otherwise zero resistance. Sourced from the [[Bright principle]] of possibility. Distinct from empathic listening, NVC reflection, or Rogerian counseling — those serve *accuracy* by mirroring; Possibility Listening serves *emergence* by holding open. *See Day 08; Map Note M14.*

### Possibility paradigm

The thoughtware that creates what becomes by reference to a chosen [[Context]] and a created future. Does not deny the past; relocates the source of action from past-as-cause to context-as-source. The thoughtware PM teaches and the course is run inside. Distinct from the [[Causal paradigm]] (default of most therapy). *See Course Architecture §2; Day 10.*

### Possibility Speaking

The fourth of the [[4 Speakings]]. Speech sourced from a [[Bright principle]] that **brings a new context into being** — lands a distinction the listener did not previously have, opens a door that was closed, makes available what was not available. Requires the bright principle to be active in the speaker — which is why this points forward to the [[Three Powers]] of Day 10. Distinct from "brainstorming" or "thinking out loud." *See Day 08; Map Note M15.*

### Possibility Team

A small ongoing group (often weekly) of PM practitioners meeting to ask each other dangerous questions, run experiments, and give feedback. **Open to anyone — no invitation and no prerequisite training**; you can join an existing team or start one. Often the most durable form of post-course community; Module 11 shows a course pairing how to continue as one. *See Module 11.*

### Problem ownership

The first PM question when something is "wrong" between two people: **whose problem is this?** Three honest options — *my problem* (I have an unmet need; I own it and act), *the other person's problem* (they have an unmet need; I witness, complete the loop, and do not take it on), *shared* (both of us have an unmet need, and we have **explicitly contracted** to work on it together — rarer than people think). The [[Rescuer]] pattern is structurally a misowning. *See Day 09; Map Note M18.*

### Radical responsibility

The fourth of the [[Levels of responsibility]]. Stance: **I am at cause. I am the author. Inaction is a choice with consequences.** Not "it's my fault." Not heroic. Not taking on everyone else's stuff. The stance from which the course operates and from which all of PM is taught. A learner does not have to live here full-time to start the work — only to step into it for the duration of the modules. *See Day 01.*

### Rapid learning

Learning at the speed the universe is already giving you feedback. **Not learning faster than is natural** — what happens when you stop the things you do to *prevent* learning: defending against Beeps, dressing up Shifts as identity crises, refusing to Go because Go is uncomfortable. *See Day 04.*

### Red pill / blue pill

The metaphor PM uses for choosing whether to actually **be in** the work. **Red pill** = in this. For the next 30 days I am the author of what this becomes. I will pair with my partner. I will run the experiments. I will use the safety practices when I need them. **Blue pill** = tourist. *I will watch the videos when I get to them. I will not pair with anyone. I'm not actually in this.* If a learner cannot honestly say the red-pill sentence, that is not a problem — it is data. The course is not for everyone right now. *See Day 01.*

### Referral packet

The per-learner PDF generated at enrollment containing region-appropriate crisis lines (listed *first*, not buried), licensed mental health directories, PM-aware coaches, local domestic violence and legal resources (if Screen 4 returned yes), and the not-therapy disclaimer. The CM verifies the packet exists before access opens. *See Gatekeeping Protocol §E.*

### Rescuer

[[Low Drama Triangle]] position: *"Let me fix it for you."* Steps in unasked. Takes over. Builds dependency. Food: being **good**, being **needed**, being **above the problem because solving it for someone beneath**. **The most morally camouflaged position** — rescuing looks like kindness and is often praised. The damage is structural: the Rescuer needs there to be a Victim, and so subtly cultivates one. Also visible from Day 9's vantage as a special case of misowning a problem (see [[Problem ownership]]). *See Day 07; Map Notes M13, M18.*

### Responsible Game

What is left when you decline drama. Simpler. Less stimulating. Feels less alive at first because the [[Gremlin]] is starving. Sourced from [[Adult (ego state)|Adult]] and [[Bright principle|bright principles]]. The move has a shape — see [[Shifting to the Responsible Game]]. *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### Retroactive completion loop

A [[Completion loop]] run on a conversation from two months ago, two years ago, or two decades ago. The original conversation does not need to be replayed. The unfinished piece is what gets named, reflected, confirmed, acknowledged, and closed — now. Opener: *"Small thing from [time] I want to circle back on, two minutes, nothing dramatic."* Recovers years of accumulated [[Energetic debt]]. *See Day 08; Map Note M16.*

### Shadow form (of a feeling)

What a [[Feeling]] becomes when stored as [[Emotion]]. Anger → rage, resentment, bitterness, passive aggression, collapsed compliance. Sadness → depression, self-pity, chronic grief, martyr stance. Fear → anxiety, paranoia, panic, avoidance, hyper-vigilance. Joy → mania, forced positivity, spiritual bypass, addictive pleasure. The shadow form is not "bad" — it is a signal that emotional-healing-process work is waiting. *See Day 05; Map Note M10.*

### Shadow principle

A principle whose sourcing **generates [[Patriarchy]]**: domination, manipulation, control, deception, scarcity, righteousness, comparison, abandonment-as-strategy. The principle is upstream; the culture is downstream. Structural, not moral — shadow is not "evil"; shadow is *destructive of consciousness*. **Has its domains** — a surgeon operates from control during an operation, an army from domination repelling an invasion. The work is not denial (which reinstalls it in disguise) but **consciousness of which principle is in the driver's seat now**. **The shadow you cannot name owns you.** *See Day 10; Map Note M21.*

### Shift

In [[Beep · Shift · Go]]: **one specific change** between two Goes — not an identity overhaul, not "I'll be better next time." A Shift names exactly what will be different next rep, in operational terms. *"Next time I will say the same sentence with my feet on the floor and my eyes on hers."* *See Day 04.*

### Shifting to the Responsible Game

The move out of the [[Low Drama Triangle|triangle]]. Four parts. **(1) Notice** you are in a drama position — body usually knows first (tight jaw is often Persecutor; collapsed chest is often Victim; pulled-forward posture is often Rescuer). **(2) Name it**, out loud or silently — *I am in Persecutor right now.* **(3) Step out** — change posture, breathe, [[Centering|center]], [[Grounding cord|ground]], drop a [[Bubble]]. **(4) Ask three questions** — what is actually happening (in fact, without my story)? what do I actually want? what can I actually choose, from here? *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### Source-frame

Synonym for [[Context]] in its proper PM sense. The invisible frame *from which* action is sourced, prior to mindset, beliefs, or intentions. *See Day 01; Map Note M01.*

### Sourcing

The act of acting from a principle. Every action is sourced from some principle (see [[Bright principle]], [[Shadow principle]]). There is no neutral action. The PM literacy: *what is sourcing this action right now?* — asked per-action, not per-life. *See Day 10; Map Note M21.*

### Stored emotion / open wound

The PM image: an unprocessed [[Feeling]] sits in the [[Emotional body]] like an open wound waiting for the right conditions to close. **Not pathology. Incomplete data.** Closed by the [[Emotional Healing Process (EHP)|EHP]] — feeling moved, decision unmixed, new decision named, present-time orientation restored, energy returns. *See Day 06; Map Note M12.*

### Survival strategy

In PM, the Box-pattern a person assembled in childhood to survive their family of origin — the specific shape their [[Box]] took. The [[12 Roadblocks]] reach for whichever roadblock first; the [[Low Drama Triangle]] position they rotate into first under stress — these are data about their survival strategy. *See Day 02; Day 08; Map Note M04.*

### Telenovela with yourself

A live-training phrase for the internal [[Low Drama Triangle]] — running [[Persecutor]] of an internal [[Victim]] with [[Rescuer]] trying to mediate, **all between parts of yourself**, no other person required. *"A certain kind of telenovela with yourself. Only you can be the victim and only you can be the persecutor and only you can stop the show."* Surfaces strongly in some learners on Day 7. *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### The chain

The seven-link causal sequence at the heart of [[Day 01]]: **new context → new thoughtware → new thoughtmaps → new options → new choices → new behavior → new results.** The arrow runs left to right only. The leftmost link is the most leveraged and the most invisible — which is why nearly all attempts at change start on the right side, where the symptoms live, and produce no durable shift. *See Day 01; Map Note M01.*

### The Three Powers

The operational moves a person can make from inside the space of possibility. **(1) [[Choosing (Power of)|The Power of Choosing]]** — own that you chose, even if on autopilot. **(2) [[Dangerous question|The Power of Asking Dangerous Questions]]** — populate the space with what was previously invisible. **(3) [[Declaring (Power of)|The Power of Declaring]]** — speak a new context into being. **Sequential, not parallel** — declarations skip the foundation collapse within 24 hours. Not techniques. Stances available to a person standing in possibility on purpose. *See Day 10; Map Note M23.*

### Thoughtmap

A specific cognitive map used to navigate — the [[Box]], the [[Five bodies]], the [[Low Drama Triangle]], the [[Map of Communication]], each is a thoughtmap. **The map is not the territory** — a tool, not a doctrine. The course teaches 23 named thoughtmaps. *See Day 02; Map Note M03.*

### Thoughtware

What you think **with** — the internal architecture, the operating system, the structures that determine which thoughts are even available to be thought. Distinct from the *content* (opinions, beliefs, conclusions) that runs on it. You cannot see your thoughtware the way you cannot see your glasses while wearing them. **The course upgrades thoughtware.** Most "personal change" swaps the content and leaves the thoughtware where it was — the new belief runs on the old operating system and the operating system eats it within a week. *See Day 02; Map Note M02.*

### Thoughtware upgrade

The unit of work in PM. Not insight (insight does not install thoughtware). Not behavior change (behavior installed on the old thoughtware reverts within days). Upgrade happens when [[Liquid state]] is held long enough inside a [[Holding context]] that the underlying architecture loosens and re-forms around a new [[Distinction]]. The course is, end to end, a thoughtware upgrade. *See Course Architecture §"Pedagogical premise."*

### Trainer Path

The multi-year training to become a [[Possibilitator]] — qualified to facilitate ETB and other PM trainings. Not a casual commitment. *Referenced in Day 10 "What's next."*

### Triage rubric (Green / Yellow / Red)

The CM's classification of every cohort-feed post and learner DM. **Green** — normal reflection, no action required. **Yellow** — stuck feeling >24 hours, increased numbing, partner-conflict surfacing, learner over their head but functional; CM DM within 24 hours. **Red** — suicidal ideation, imminent harm, abuse disclosure in progress, psychotic break, medical emergency; **same day, immediate**; crisis-line numbers in the first message; direct hand-off to clinician or emergency services. The CM does not "discuss" Red flags — they refer. *See Gatekeeping Protocol §F.*

### Unlock checklist

The structural gate for [[Intensity tags|High-intensity modules]] (Days 5, 6, 7, 9). Partner agreement signed; partner has been responsive; learner confirms partner reachable today and tomorrow; 90 uninterrupted minutes ahead; not in acute crisis; consent prompt read; if Screen 1 or 5 applied at enrollment, clinician confirmation on file. **Skip-after-24-hours override is removed.** If the gate is not met, the learner waits. *See Gatekeeping Protocol §D.*

### Unmixing

Naming the component [[Feeling|feelings]] of a [[Mixed emotion]], one at a time, so each one can move through. The first move of [[Emotional Healing Process (EHP)|EHP]] Position 3. *"In this I have some anger and some sadness."* Then let one move at a time. **Anger as anger. Sadness as sadness. Fear as fear.** Not all at once. *See Day 06; Map Notes M11, M12.*

### Vacuum listening space

The internal state of a [[Possibility Listening|Possibility Listener]]: empty of opinion, expectation, agenda, judgment, and even the need to understand. Still there. Just out of the way. **Holding the vacuum takes more of you than running a paraphrase** — the Box wants to comment, the Gremlin wants to fix, the Adult wants to be helpful; holding requires noticing each impulse and letting it pass. *See Day 08; Map Note M14.*

### Victim

[[Low Drama Triangle]] position: *"I have no choice. This is happening to me. Help me."* Collapsed, helpless, often physically smaller. Food: being **wronged**, being **innocent**, being **special in suffering**. **More powerful than it looks** — a skilled Victim runs entire households by making others do for them what they have declared themselves incapable of. The collapsed energy is itself a control: *if you do not rescue me, you are cruel.* The misery is part of the food. *See Day 07; Map Note M13.*

### Witness

Two related uses. **(1) Inner location** that [[Feedback (PM)|feedback]] is sourced from — the part that just observed without verdict, distinct from the [[Gremlin]] (who criticizes) and the [[Rescuer]] (who advises). **(2) Person holding space** during an [[Emotional Healing Process (EHP)|EHP]] or partner exchange: physically and energetically present while the feeling moves. Does not fix, advise, narrate, "send love," or talk about their own similar experience. **The witness's energy of being present is the medicine.** Sounds soft. Is not. It is the load-bearing structural element of the EHP. *See Day 04; Day 06; Map Note M12.*

# Reflection and Journal Prompt Bank

> A library of prompts the learner pulls from when the module's prompts have been used up — or when the module's prompts went somewhere the learner is not yet ready to go and a side-door is needed.

---

## How to use this file

Each Day-module gives you 4–5 reflection prompts in its own file. Those are the primary set. This bank is the secondary set — prompts to reach for when you want to go deeper, when you want a side angle, when you have a week to integrate and the module's prompts have all been worked.

There are eight sections. Day-by-day deeper prompts (Days 1–10). Cross-module integration prompts. Weekly review prompts. End-of-cohort prompts. The dangerous questions list. Prompts for when you are stuck.

**Pick one. Sit with it for at least 20 minutes before writing.** Journaling without sitting first produces the same thoughts you have already had on paper. Sitting first lets the question work on you. Then write.

**Longhand if you can.** The body is in the writing when it is in the hand. Typing is faster and lands less.

---

## The difference between a journal prompt and a self-help question

This is worth naming once.

A **self-help question** asks you to perform what you already know about yourself. *"What are your top three values?" "What are you grateful for today?" "What would your best self do?"* These produce predictable answers because the answer is constructed by the part of you the question addresses — usually your Adapted Child performing wellness, or your Nurturing Parent performing self-care.

A **PM-grade journal prompt** does something different. It is engineered to bypass the Box's prepared answers and land in a body location where you have not been looking. The form of a good prompt is: *not what you would say about yourself in a job interview, but what your body knows that your story has not let you say.*

Two tests:
1. **Does the question dissolve the floor a little?** If after asking it you feel slightly less certain rather than more, you are likely in the right territory.
2. **Did you already know the answer?** If yes, the question is performing — it is not yet PM-grade. Push to the version of the question whose answer you do not already have.

This file is full of the second kind. Some of them will be uncomfortable. That is the point.

## Day-by-day deeper prompts

Beyond what each module's own prompts cover. Reach for these in the integration period of each Day-cycle.

### Day 1 — Orientation, New Context, Radical Responsibility

1. The context I told my partner I am operating from when I recorded my first voice message, versus the context I am actually operating from today — what is the gap, and what is in the gap? (The gap is rarely zero. The gap is often where the work is.)
2. When I read the four levels of responsibility — child, adolescent, adult, radical — which one am I most fluent at *performing* without actually inhabiting? When I look back at my last seven days, where did I look like the level I am claiming, and where was I actually one level lower?
3. The red-pill sentence I chose — three weeks from now, having now seen what the course is, would I choose it differently? More cleanly? More honestly? Less performatively? What would the actual cleanly-chosen version say?
4. Whose authority have I been treating as mine, in the area of life this course is meant to address? Whose voice has been making my decisions and getting credit?
5. If I knew with certainty that the next 30 days of this course would change my life in a specific direction — what direction would I be most afraid it changed in?
6. What did the people who matter to me — partner, children, family, close friends — do when I announced (or did not announce) that I was taking this course? What did their reaction tell me about the container I am embedded in?

### Day 2 — Thoughtware, Thoughtmaps, Box Technology

1. Looking at the list of Box rules I cataloged, which rule is the load-bearing one — the one that, if removed, several others would fall away too? Why has it been load-bearing for me, specifically?
2. Whose Box did I install? Was it one parent, both, a teacher, a religion, an early painful event? When I name the source precisely, does the rule feel different?
3. There is a Box rule I have not written down because it would be too embarrassing or too revealing. Without writing it here either, can I name to myself that it exists? Where in my body do I notice it living?
4. If I let my Box continue running for the next 10 years exactly as it is now — what would my life look like at the end of those 10 years? Where would I be? What would have happened? What would not have happened?
5. Which Box rule does my partner (life-partner, not pairing-partner) most rely on me running? What would happen between us if I stopped running it?
6. The Box says *you are the Box*. What is the part of me that disagrees with that, and where does that part live in my body?

### Day 3 — Liquid State, Center-Ground-Bubble, Five Bodies

1. Of the five bodies, which one have I been treating as my whole self — and which one have I been treating as if it did not exist? What does the second one know about my life that I have been refusing to hear?
2. When I am off-center — handing my authority to someone else's reaction or yesterday's conversation or my phone — what is the consistent flavor of where my center keeps going? Whose center am I borrowing? Whose problem am I trying to be on top of?
3. The first time I dropped a grounding cord during a real conversation — what was different in the conversation, and what was different in me afterwards? If I had been grounded my whole life this way, what would my life look like now?
4. My bubble — historically too big, too small, too porous, or too rigid? What did the size do for me protectively, and what is it costing me now?
5. The golden cube was for placing work inside and looking at. What is one thing in my life right now that, if I placed it in the cube and looked at it from outside — without solving — would shift its weight?
6. The Box performs stillness. The Adult is actually still. What is the difference, in my body, between the two?

### Day 4 — Feedback, Coaching, Rapid Learning, Experiments

1. The last time someone offered me PM-grade feedback I could have used — what did I do with it? Did I rebut, redirect, deflect, or take it in? What does that pattern tell me about my Box's relationship to being seen accurately?
2. The Beeps I have caught in the last week — what is the pattern? Which Beep keeps repeating? What is the upstream thoughtware that keeps producing the same Beep?
3. There is a piece of feedback I have been receiving from multiple people for years that I have not taken in. What is it? What would it cost me to take it in fully? What has refusing to take it in cost me?
4. In which area of my life have I been parked at *"I read about it"* for years — meaning: I have the knowledge, I have not run the experiments? What is one experiment I could run this week?
5. The most generous coach I could have for the next 90 days — what kind of person would they be? Why am I not yet working with them?
6. PM coaching offers possibilities, not answers. The last time I asked someone for advice — was I asking for possibilities or trying to be told the answer? What would have happened if I had been given possibilities and asked to choose?

### Day 5 — Feelings vs Emotions, Old Map of Feelings, Numbness Bar

1. Of the four feelings, which one has the highest Numbness Bar over it for me? Specifically: which feeling, if it fully registered, would change my life most? Which is the Numbness Bar protecting me from?
2. The Old Map I came in with — who installed which rule? Map each of the four feelings to the specific person, institution or event that gave me the rule. The map is more precise than I expect when actually drawn.
3. The 5-minute test on my own current internal state: which "feelings" in my daily life are actually emotions, looping past the 5-minute mark, posing as present-time information about my present-time life?
4. The feeling I am most uncomfortable feeling at full intensity — what would I do with the information that feeling carries, if I let it land? Whose life would change?
5. There is a feeling that has been waiting under my numbness for years, asking to register. If I let 5% of it through this week, what would surface? What am I afraid that 5% would lead to?
6. My partner (life-partner, family member, child) — which of their feelings does my Numbness Bar prevent me from registering? Whose grief have I been not seeing? Whose anger have I been minimizing? What costs has that had to them, and to us?

### Day 6 — Mixed Emotions and Emotional Healing Process

1. The stored emotion I carry that I have most thoroughly disguised as personality — what is it actually? What feelings make up the mix? When did it start being mistaken for "who I am"?
2. The emotional wound that has not closed in me, despite years of trying — what conditions does it actually need to close, that I have not yet provided? What container, what witness, what time?
3. The trigger I keep falling for — the small thing in the present that activates the large emotion from the past. What is the trigger, what is the source, and what would change if I addressed the source rather than the trigger?
4. Which of my current important relationships is being run not by the present-day person but by the emotional charge from someone earlier? Whose ghost am I in conversation with when I am supposedly in conversation with this person?
5. The grief I have not yet allowed myself to feel — for the person, the relationship, the dream, the version of myself, the chapter — what would change if I gave it the full 3–7 days it actually needs?
6. There is an emotional healing process waiting, but not yet ready. What would have to be in place — what container, what witness, what professional support, what time of life — for me to do it cleanly? When?

### Day 7 — Low Drama, Gremlin Food, Shifting to Responsible Game

1. The gremlin food I am most addicted to — the one I would defend even now, even after this module — what is it? What does it provide that I do not yet know how to provide cleanly?
2. The drama position I run with my partner — Persecutor, Victim, Rescuer — and the drama position they run with me: how does our rotation work? Who initiates? Who follows? What gets produced?
3. There is a fight I keep having in my life. Same shape, different content. What is the shape? What gremlin food does it produce reliably? What would happen if I declined the fight one day and noticed what tries to take its place?
4. The Responsible Game move scares me because — what specifically? What am I afraid would be revealed if I dropped the drama and stood in what is actually here?
5. My favorite rescue — the help I deliver to others that they did not ask for, that allows me not to do my own work — who do I deliver it to most reliably? What does the rescue cost them in autonomy, and what does it cost me in honesty?
6. The narrative I tell about my hardest year (or relationship, or chapter) — how much of the narrative is true storytelling, and how much is gremlin food production? If I subtracted the gremlin food, what would the remaining story be?

### Day 8 — Listening, Speaking, Communication, Completion Loops

1. The conversation I have been not having for the longest time — with whom, about what, and what specifically is the unfinished piece? What would happen if I ran the completion loop this week?
2. Of the 12 Roadblocks, which is my go-to under pressure? When did I learn to deploy it? Who taught me — by example or by absence?
3. The person in my life I almost never offer Possibility Listening to — who is it, and what would change if I held that vacuum space for them once? What am I afraid would emerge?
4. Speaking from story versus sourcing — when I review my speech this week, what percentage came from each? When I sourced, what was different in the room?
5. The truth I have been trying to say for months, in many partial versions, and which is not yet landing — what is the one true sentence underneath all the partial versions? What does my body do when I imagine saying it?
6. Who in my life listens to me at the level I am capable of speaking from? Where is the gap between the speech I can produce and the listening available — and what does that gap make me do (over-explain, perform, withdraw, soften)?

### Day 9 — Ego States, Problem Ownership, Learning Spiral

1. My default ego state — the one I run most of my life from — what is it actually, when I am honest? (Common Box-trap: claiming Adult while running Critical Parent. Claiming Free Child while running Adapted Child. Claiming insight while running Gremlin.)
2. My Critical Parent's voice belongs to whom, originally? Trace the voice. What did it once protect me from? What does it now cost me?
3. My Adapted Child runs more of my adult life than I would like to admit. What does it most reliably perform? Pleasing? Hiding? Rebelling? Comply-then-resent? Whom does it perform for?
4. The problem I have been carrying that is not actually mine — whose is it? What did I gain by carrying it? What would change for them — and for me — if I gave it back?
5. My Demon — the part of me with the capacity for real harm — what is its specific capacity? Where, in my life, has it acted? What conditions reduce the likelihood of it acting again?
6. The learning spiral — the same lesson keeps coming back at deeper levels of the same territory. Which lesson is currently on its third or fourth pass through my life? What is the level underneath the one I have already worked?

### Day 10 — Map of Possibility, Bright Principles, Three Powers, Integration

1. The bright principle I am most willing to source from — the one I can name and feel? And the bright principle that has been quietly available but I have not yet picked up — what is it, and why have I not?
2. The shadow principle I have most quietly been sourcing from, throughout my adult life — what is it? What has it given me? What has it cost?
3. The declaration I am ready to make, but have not yet made — what is it? What is the cost of not making it? What is the cost of making it?
4. The dangerous question I have been carrying without asking — what is it? When will I ask it? What am I waiting for?
5. The three powers — choosing, asking dangerous questions, declaring. Which of the three is most underdeveloped in me? Which would change my life most if I practiced it for 90 days?
6. The version of me 90 days from today who has practiced these powers consistently — what is they doing that I am not doing today? What did they start doing on Day 1 after the course closed?

## Cross-module integration prompts

These connect distinctions across modules. The most generative integration work happens at the intersections.

1. Which feeling is most numbed for me, and which ego state covers for it? (Common pattern: Critical Parent covers numbed anger. Nurturing Parent covers numbed sadness. Gremlin covers numbed joy. Adapted Child covers numbed fear.) When I see the covering, what becomes available?
2. Where in my life is the Box still running, even though I now see it? Why has seeing it not yet stopped it? What does the Box still provide that I have not yet built another way to provide?
3. Which gremlin food am I most attached to, and what declaration would starve it? (The declaration that makes the gremlin food no longer available is the one worth making.)
4. The Old Map of Feelings I inherited, and the drama positions I run — how are they linked? Forbidden anger usually produces Victim or Rescuer; forbidden sadness usually produces Persecutor; forbidden fear usually produces all three at different times.
5. My default ego state and my default drama position — which combinations consistently appear together? (Critical Parent + Persecutor. Adapted Child + Victim. Nurturing Parent + Rescuer.) What thoughtware would interrupt the combination?
6. The Box rules I cataloged on Day 2, and the listening roadblocks I deploy on Day 8 — how are they related? Each roadblock is a Box rule's strategy for staying intact while seeming to listen.
7. The feeling I most struggle to register, and the bright principle I most struggle to source from — are they linked? Numbed joy and unsourced gratitude. Numbed anger and unsourced courage. Numbed sadness and unsourced honesty. Numbed fear and unsourced presence.
8. The conversation I most need to complete, and the ego state I would have to be in to complete it cleanly — are they aligned? Most uncompleted conversations are waiting for the Adult, while I keep showing up as Adapted Child or Critical Parent.
9. The dangerous question I am avoiding, and the decision I am stuck on — are they the same question in different costume? Most stuck decisions are stuck because the question underneath has not been asked.
10. What I learned on Day 5 about emotions and what I learned on Day 9 about problem ownership — how do they meet? A misowned problem is often a misidentified emotion. Whose anger have I been carrying? Whose grief? Whose fear?

## Weekly review prompts

End of each cohort week. 15–20 minutes. Reach for these in addition to (not instead of) the modules' own reflection prompts.

1. **What landed this week.** Not what was interesting, not what I knew already, not what I performed knowing. What landed — meaning: I am living slightly differently because of it. One sentence.
2. **What did not land yet.** Where the module did its work but the work has not yet integrated. The territory I am still working on. One sentence.
3. **What I avoided.** One thing the module asked me to do or look at that I did not do or look at. Why I did not. Without making it into a fresh task.
4. **The Box's strategy this week.** What did the Box do specifically to keep itself intact through this week's material? Which strategy did it favor — disengagement, intellectualization, sycophancy with the course, performative compliance, busy-ness, distraction?
5. **The pairing this week.** What was alive in my partner exchange that I have not yet said back to them? What did I notice about their work that I did not yet reflect? One thing I will say in the next exchange.
6. **The single most useful question I asked myself this week.** Why was it useful? Where can I ask it again in a different territory?

## End-of-cohort prompts (after Day 10)

These are substantial. Set aside an hour or two. These are the prompts that anchor the 90-day post-course period.

1. **The change of context.** What is the context I am now operating from in the area of my life that mattered most going in? Is it different from when I started? Where can I still feel the old context trying to reassert itself, and what does it sound like?
2. **The Box now.** What does my Box look like now that I have seen it for 30 days? What has shifted? What is unchanged? Which of its rules am I still living inside, and which have softened? Which new ones have appeared that were not visible 30 days ago?
3. **The cost of completing this course.** Every thoughtware upgrade has a cost — what something was paying for becomes more visible once that something is gone. What has it cost me to complete this course? Who in my life has paid for it alongside me? Who has not paid and is in a different position than they would have been if they had?
4. **What the course was not.** Where did the course not go, that I needed it to go? What did I bring in that I am leaving still unaddressed? Where will I take that material next — therapy, coaching, longer PM work, a different course, a relationship reset?
5. **The version of me that walked in.** When I write the introduction-letter to my pairing partner from memory now, who was that person? What had they been doing for years that made the course necessary? What were they unable to say or feel or do?
6. **The version of me walking out.** Who is this person? What can they do that the version walking in could not? Where are they still tender? What practice keeps them from collapsing back into the old version?
7. **What I will declare for the 90 days.** One specific declaration. Specific. Public to one person. Reviewed weekly. What is it?
8. **The PM-grade community I want.** Who in my life — pairing partner, course alumni, PM-aware coaches, eventual possibility team members — am I committing to staying in PM-grade contact with? Specifically — names, cadence, container.
9. **The first crisis ahead.** I can predict, roughly, the next crisis-shape that will come in my life — work, relationship, family, health, identity. When it comes, what from this course will I most need to remember? Where will I most likely forget it? What will I write to myself now to read then?
10. **What the next 10 modules would be.** If I designed Days 11–20 of my own continuation course — what would they cover? What territory am I newly able to enter that I could not have entered before Day 1? What is the next edge?
11. **The dangerous question I am ready to ask now.** Which dangerous question, of all those that surfaced across the 10 modules, am I now ready to ask cleanly? When will I sit with it?
12. **What I am taking with me.** Not what I learned. What I am taking. One distinction, one practice, one relationship, one declaration. Four things. No more. The four things that, if everything else from the course dissolved, would still embed.

## The dangerous questions list

PM-canonical. Each of these dissolves a floor when sat with for at least 20 minutes. Pick one at a time. Do not ask them all in one sitting. Do not ask them in liquid state without equipment. Do not ask them when alone if a partner-message check-in is not available afterwards.

1. What am I pretending not to know?
2. Who would I be if I dropped this story about myself?
3. What would I have to be willing to feel in order to leave this?
4. What am I getting out of this not changing?
5. Whose approval am I actually waiting for? What would happen if I stopped?
6. What am I afraid would happen if I were fully seen — and is that fear current, or is it from another time?
7. What is the truth I have been not saying, to whom, and what is its cost so far?
8. If I knew I had two years left to live, which of my current commitments would I keep, and which would I drop within the week?
9. What did I want, as a child, that I have been not letting myself want as an adult?
10. Where am I most counterfeit? Where am I performing a version of myself that no one would miss if I dropped it?
11. Whose problem have I been making mine in order to avoid my own?
12. What am I building right now that I would not build if I were sourcing from joy instead of from fear?
13. What did I once know clearly about my own direction, that I have let other people's voices outshout?
14. The thing in me that is broken or wrong — what if it is neither broken nor wrong, just unfinished?
15. What is the most generous version of the person I am most in conflict with — and what does seeing them that way ask of me?

## Prompts for when you are stuck

For the learner who is stalling between modules. Read the prompts in order. Sit with whichever lands first. Do not skip; do not power through.

1. **The stall is information.** What is it information about? Not "I am being lazy" — that is the Box's verdict, not the data. What is the actual reason I am not picking up the next module? What does my body know about the next module's content that I have not yet let my mind know?
2. **The cost of continuing right now.** What would it cost me, today, to do the next module? Time? Energy? Disruption to a relationship? Activation of a feeling? Confrontation with a story I have been holding? Name the actual cost. The Box conceals the cost; naming it returns choice.
3. **The cost of not continuing.** What does it cost me, today, to not do the next module? What is the chain that begins with "I am going to stall this week" — what does that lead to, in 30 days, in 90 days? Is the cost of stalling actually smaller than the cost of doing?
4. **The pause that is actually a stop.** Sometimes the stall is the right pause — the system has been working hard and needs time. Sometimes the stall is the Box's stop dressed as a pause. Which is mine? The tell: a real pause has a specific re-entry date and a clear reason. A Box stop has neither.
5. **What I would do if I did not have to do it perfectly.** If the next module did not have to be done well — if I just had to read the file, run the practice imperfectly, send one voice message that was not impressive — could I do that today? Often the stall is perfectionism wearing the costume of integrity.
6. **Reach for the partner.** Have I told my pairing partner that I am stalling, and asked them to witness it? Have I told the CM? The stall, named to another human, almost always changes shape. The stall in private, however, can extend indefinitely.

## Final note

This file contains roughly 95 prompts. You will not use most of them in any single course cycle. They are here so that when you reach the end of the module's own prompts and want more — or when a particular question has been circling and you want a precise version of it — there is a place to go.

Three rules:

1. **Sit before writing.** 20 minutes minimum, in silence, with the prompt held in mind. Skipping the sit lands you in your standard answers.
2. **Longhand if you can.** The body is in the writing when it is in the hand.
3. **The question whose answer you don't already know is the right question.** If you already know the answer, push to the question whose answer you do not yet have.

Welcome to your inventory.

## The v2 posture (read this before the table)

**Every module in this course is complete as text.** The written module file is the single canonical source for its module; the maps, practices, scripts, and tools all ship in full. **Videos are optional enrichment.** A learner who never watches a single video misses style and company, not content. No learner-facing file in v2 references a video by bare filename; learner-facing text says "videos are enrichment" and points here, and this manifest is the only place filenames live.

This resolves v1's dead end: v1 module headers cited files like `06 - LiquidState_EN.mp4` that no learner could click, stream, or locate. Those filenames referred to **private recordings from a live cohort, held by the course owner, outside the CC BY-SA grant that covers the written course.** They were never shippable, and one slot (08) never existed at all.

What this means for an operator, in one paragraph: you can run a full cohort with zero videos. If you want video, you have two routes per slot below: **obtain** (license or get permission from the rights holder to stream the private recording to your cohort) or **record** (make your own replacement; the module text is script-grade and liftable). Either way, host on a platform meeting the privacy minimums in Doc 04 §H, and link videos from your cohort platform, not from the course files.

## B. Orientation videos — the set an operator records fresh

Doc 02 lists a short per-module orientation video (3–5 minutes) as the first module artifact. These never existed in v1 either; they are the cheapest set to produce because **the scripts are liftable straight from the module intros** — each module's purpose section, intensity tag, pace recommendation, and safety pointer, read warmly by the person the cohort will actually be hearing from (operator or CM). One afternoon, twelve takes, done.

Per video, cover four beats: what this module is for (lift from the module's purpose paragraph) · the intensity tag and what it asks (time, partner reachability for High modules) · where the safety equipment is (grounding script, pause rules) · one sentence of pace honesty ("this one is heavier; take the pause day after").

Recording guidance: one take per video is fine; warmth beats polish. Face on camera, ordinary room, no slides (the maps live in the modules). Keep each under five minutes; if a script runs long, the module text already says it better. Read the safety sentences as written in the module file, not from memory.

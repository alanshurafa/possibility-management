What this is

A twelve-module course in thoughtware.

Expand the Box is a twelve-module, self-paced course in Possibility Management thoughtware: the Box, the four feelings, low drama and the Gremlin, listening and speaking as acts of possibility, and the three powers of choosing, asking dangerous questions, and declaring. You read, you practice, you exchange voice messages with one partner, and you run small experiments in your life. This edition adds the group layer: a community commons, pods of 2–12 who witness each other’s work, and the public Staking practice — see Practicing in a Group.

It is not therapy, coaching, or treatment. It is an education in distinctions and maps, taken at your own pace inside a real safety container.

Where this work comes from

Everything taught here originates in Possibility Management, the body of thoughtware developed by Clinton Callahan and the Possibility Management community since the late 1980s, and given to the world under World Copyleft. How the course runs

Twelve modules. One climb. A way back.

  • Twelve modules, 00 through 11, one every ~3 days, ~30 days total. Module 00 builds your container; Module 11 builds what comes after.
  • Each module runs in 2–3 sittings. The file marks where each sitting ends. Stop at the marks; the breaks are part of the design.
  • One daily practice, 5–10 minutes. A short morning sit that starts as a Beep! Book capture and, from Module 5, grows a Feelings Form bar reading inside the same sit. It never becomes a stack. Full spec: Daily Practice Spine.
  • One partner for the whole course. Voice messages, a few minutes each way, once per module. Your partner witnesses; they do not advise or fix.
  • Modules 5–9 are the climb. Four High-intensity modules and a heavy Medium, back to back. They are gated: a consent check and a reachable partner before each one, a built-in pause after Module 6, and a rest day between Modules 8 and 9.
  • Every module has two buttons. Study the map opens the interactive Map Atlas; Run the practice opens that module's tool in Interactive Tools. Everything those tools record stays in your browser. The journal shows your records and exports them; ground.html holds the 60-second grounding script, one tap away, always.
Your first three steps

Start here.

  1. Open Module 00 — Start Here and Getting Your Container. It walks you through getting your container: a cohort with a community manager, a recruited witness, or the honest solo path. Nothing else opens until your container exists.
  2. Install the Beep! Book. A literal notebook with "Beep! Book" on the cover. It is the daily practice from day one. Instrument spec: Beep! Book Guide.
  3. Put the schedule on your calendar. Twelve modules, the climb at 5–9, the pause after 6, the rest day before 9. Calendar it before you start, not after you slip.

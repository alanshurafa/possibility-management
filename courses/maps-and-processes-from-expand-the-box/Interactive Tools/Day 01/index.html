The three tools
I.
Interactive map · 7 links

The Chain.

Click each link to see what it is. Then walk it backward from a result and find where it bent.
The chain runs: new context → new thoughtware → new thoughtmaps → new options → new choices → new behavior → new results. Most people try to start on the right. The work is on the left.
~10 min · solo Open →
II.
Rephrase grid · 4 levels

Four levels.

Type a sentence you actually said. Rephrase it from each of the four levels of responsibility.
Child · adolescent · adult · radical. Most people think they live at adult. Most actually live at child or adolescent. The body knows which one is true. Use this to find out which one your sentence came from.
~12 min · solo Open →
III.
Guided ritual · ~15 min

The Red-Pill Ceremony.

The Day 1 commitment ceremony, guided. Stand. Breathe. Speak the sentences. Choose.
The analogue of the live ETB ceremony where the trainer asks "blue pill or red pill?" and gives everyone 90 seconds to walk out and get their money back. Online and async, you do it for yourself. Your chosen sentence is saved.
~15 min · standing Begin →

How to use these.

Read the Day 1 module first (`Days/Day 01 - Orientation, New Context, Radical Responsibility.md`). Then come here. The tools are not replacements for the module — they are the embodied companions to it.

Run The Chain first, while you still have the module fresh. Run Four Levels the next day, with a real sentence from your week. Run The Red-Pill Ceremony only when you can stand alone in a quiet room for 15 minutes.

What the tools save.

Each tool stores your work in your browser's local storage — on this device only, never sent anywhere. Your commitment sentence, your chain walks, your rephrases all stay with you.

Clear your browser data and the tools forget. That is the design. These are your practices, not the course's records.

Day 4 · Interactive Practice · the rep, designed

Design one experiment.
Run it. Notice.

An experiment is the operational unit of PM practice. What I will do / by when / what I will notice. If any of the three is missing, it is not yet an experiment — it is a wish. This tool gives you the scaffold, a library of starters, and a place to track the reps. Reps compound.

Step 1 · Pick a domain

What part of the work is this experiment for?

The domain is just a way in. Pick the one the Box is most uncomfortable with right now.

Step 2 · Pick a starter — or write your own

A menu. Not a script.

Pick the one that produces "I'd rather not, actually." The Box's resistance is the signal you are at the right experiment.

Step 3 · Write the experiment

What / by when / what I will notice.

Concrete enough that a witness could verify. If it's vague, it's a wish.

One specific behavior, in a real situation. Not "be more present" — write the actual sentence, the actual move, the actual rep.
A specific window — date, event, or time-bound. Not "this week." Usually within 48 hours.
The data you'll collect. What in your body, what in the room, what in the other person. What would tell you the rep produced new information.
Beep! Book, voice memo, note in this tool. Most learning is lost in the first hour — pick a method you'll actually use within 10 minutes.
A pairing partner, a friend in transformational context — or NONE. An experiment without a witness is not invalid; it is just smaller in ripples.

Feeling Locator.

Sit with your body. Find the sensation. Locate it. Identify which of the four feelings it most closely is. Then receive the action the feeling is asking for.

A consent check before you begin.

This tool engages your emotional body directly. Material that has been managed by avoidance — anger you weren't allowed to feel, sadness you cut off, fear you learned to call something else, joy you suppressed — may surface. You're choosing to do this.

Confirm to yourself, out loud if you can, each of these:

  • My partner is reachable today and tomorrow.
  • I have 30+ uninterrupted minutes ahead of me.
  • I'm not in acute crisis or under the influence right now.
  • I know how to ground — and the GROUND NOW button (top right) is there the whole time.

If any of those isn't true, pause and come back when they are. Doing this tool unsafely creates exactly the kind of stored material PM is trying to help you metabolize.

Not now
Step I · Body scan

Take a breath. Drop into your body. What's present in you right now? Where?

Touch each region of the figure where you feel a sensation, however small. Multiple zones are normal. Look smaller than you think you should.

2% counts. 5% counts. Stay with the sensation, not the story about it.

No zones selected yet.

The body is the only place a feeling actually lives. The thought about the feeling is the story; the sensation in a specific place is the feeling itself.

Anger lives in bones, jaw, hands. Sadness lives in heart and throat and eyes. Fear lives in belly, skin, nervous system. Joy lives in face, chest, the whole organism.

Don't decide what feeling it is yet. Just find where the sensation lives. The map comes after.

Click any region. Click again to deselect.
Step II · Quality of sensation

Describe the quality of the sensation. Short phrases.

Pick any that fit. Multiple are normal — a sensation can be hot and contracting at the same time.

No need to be poetic. Plain language is best.

Step III · The four feelings

Which of these does the sensation most resemble?

About the suggestion Cards marked BEST MATCH are this tool's heuristic, based on where you said the sensation lives. The heuristic is a starting point — your body knows better than the heuristic does. Pick what you actually recognize, not what the map suggests. Multi-select is fine; mixed feelings are common, and a mix is technically an emotion, not a feeling. Day 6 is where mixed emotions get unmixed.
Step IV · The action it's asking for

For each feeling you picked, here is what the energy is for.

A feeling is information. The information includes an asked-for action — the thing the energy is built to do. Read it. Notice the body's response when you read it.

What's one small move you can make now?

Not the whole life-change. The next 24-hour-sized move. One sentence is enough.

Step V · The witness

Consider voice-messaging your pairing partner about what just happened.

The witness is the medicine

Say only three things. No story.

The feeling— The location— The action—

Resist the pull to explain. Resist the pull to make it interesting. The bareness is the practice — speaking the feeling instead of the story about the feeling.

Your partner doesn't have to do anything except hear it. No advice. No fixing. Witnessing only.

Ground. Now.

  1. Stop. Stand up — both feet flat on the floor. Feel the contact.
  2. Breathe out, longer than in — three slow exhales.
  3. Name three things you can see in this room. Out loud is best.
  4. Name one thing you can hear. The fridge. Traffic. Your own breath.
  5. One hand on your sternum, one on your belly. Notice your body is here.
  6. Then decide: continue, pause 10 minutes, or stop for today. Any of the three is fine.

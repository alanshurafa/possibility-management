Day 6 · Before you begin

The Emotional Healing Process is in scope for PM.
Trauma processing is not.

Read this. Choose honestly. The choice is structural — not soft.

The EHP is the structured PM practice for completing ordinary stored emotional residue — the third-grade teacher who shamed you, the friend who broke ranks at sixteen, the parent who could not receive your anger, the dog who died when you were nine. The EHP is built for exactly this material.

The EHP is not trauma processing. If the material you want to work with includes any of the following — pause this tool:

  • Flashbacks (you are in the original event sensorially, not just remembering it)
  • Dissociation, lost time, or watching yourself from outside
  • Recent acute trauma (within roughly 60 days) or a major loss
  • Anything you currently work on with a trauma-qualified clinician — EMDR, somatic experiencing, TF-CBT, IFS, sensorimotor, equivalent
  • Abuse history, single-incident trauma still producing body memory, sustained developmental trauma

Trauma needs a different room. PM is precise about this carve-out because treating trauma with the EHP can re-traumatize. This is not caution — it is structural.

Honest choice · not failure

That's the right answer.

What to do instead, today.

Trauma is a different kind of injury and needs a different kind of room. The EHP and trauma protocols are different tools — not competing, not interchangeable.

Right next moves, in order:

  • If you have a trauma-qualified clinician — message them today. Name what came up. Bring it to your next session.
  • If you don't have one — search for a clinician trained in one of: EMDR, somatic experiencing, TF-CBT, IFS, sensorimotor psychotherapy. Ask their training before booking.
  • Your CM — message them today. They can offer a referral pathway, and they need to know you paused this module.
  • For the next hour — ground (top-right button), drink water, do not consume content, do not start a hard conversation.

You did not fail Day 6. You read what the tool was for, you noticed the material you have is not what it's for, and you stopped. That is the protocol working exactly as designed. The course will be here.

Position 2 continued · Make contact

Position 3 · Unmix

A mixed emotion is made of multiple pure feelings stuck together.

Which of the four pure feelings are in here? Often two or three. Sometimes one disguised as another. There's no wrong answer.

Anger
asks to be made into sound or movement
Sadness
asks to be released
Fear
asks for presence and preparation
Joy
asks to expand

Pick at least one · usually two or three

Position 3 continued · One feeling at a time

Let it move. Alone — as itself.

Anger as anger. Sadness as sadness. Fear as fear. Joy as joy. Not all at once. The timer is gentle — an invitation, not a requirement.

Position 4 · Witness moment

Now — witness.

If you have a witness available, voice-message them now. Two minutes maximum. Three things: what the mixed emotion was, what feelings were in it, what moved. The witness's job is to witness, not fix.

A feeling does not complete in isolation. It completes in the presence of a being who can be with it without flinching. The energy of being seen is the medicine — that sounds soft, it is not. It is the load-bearing structural element of the EHP.

No advice. No "have you tried." No "I had a similar experience." Witnessing is a different practice.

Position 5 · Completion

Notice. Is the stored emotion still as heavy as it was?

Often lighter. Sometimes the same — that means more rounds are needed, or a larger witness is required. Neither is failure. The work compounds. It does not "finish" in one session.

Before you write, stand up. Walk three steps. Drink water. Notice the temperature of the room. You are back.

Six positions (0–5) of the EHP

Ground. Now.

  1. Both feet flat on the floor. Notice the contact. Press down gently.
  2. Name three things you can see in this room. Out loud is best.
  3. Name two things you can hear. The fridge. Traffic. Your own breath.
  4. One slow breath — in through the nose, out through the mouth, longer on the exhale.
  5. Drink water. Notice the temperature.
  6. Say out loud: "I am [name]. I am here, in this room, now. It is [today's date]."

The EHP walk is paused. Take as long as you need.

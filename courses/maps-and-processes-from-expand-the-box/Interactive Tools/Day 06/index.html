Before you open the tool: read this.

The Emotional Healing Process is in scope for Possibility Management. Trauma processing is not. If the material you want to work with involves flashbacks, dissociation, lost time, recent acute trauma, or anything you currently work on with a trauma-qualified clinician (EMDR, somatic experiencing, TF-CBT, IFS, sensorimotor) — pause this tool and use it later with that clinician, or not at all.

The EHP is for ordinary stored emotional residue — a recurring small irritation, a small old sadness, a low-grade specific fear. Quality of the rep, not size of the material. The tool will gate-check this with you before it begins. Take the gate honestly.

Before you click "begin"

This one is not a casual click.

  • Read the module first. Days/Day 06 - Mixed Emotions and Emotional Healing Process.md. The tool assumes Day 5's distinctions are live.
  • Have a witness reachable. Partner / coach / therapist. The "after" voice-message is structural, not optional.
  • 90 uninterrupted minutes. Around the walk itself, not just during it.
  • Pick small material. The smallest one that still has charge. Solo EHP is not for major or trauma-coded material.
  • Know how to ground. The button is top-right. You may use it more than once.
  • Know how to reach your CM. If material exceeds the container, the CM and your clinician are the right calls — not pushing through.
· · ·

What the tool does.

It walks one stored mixed emotion through the six positions (0–5) of the canonical EHP map — foundation, contact, navigate to liquid state, unmix-and-move, completion. The path is not a script. The positions are the terrain. The tool helps you stay located, time the gentle 60-second invitations for each component feeling, and close the container cleanly.

What it doesn't do. It does not pretend to be a witness — only a being can be that. It does not pretend to be a clinician. It does not pretend the EHP is a quick technique. And it refuses to proceed for trauma material, on purpose.

What the tool saves.

On this device only, in localStorage: the date of each walk, which of the six positions (0–5) you traversed, which component feelings you named and which moved, your witness state, and the closing sentence you wrote about what shifted.

It does not save the text of the mixed emotion you typed, nor the details of what came up during the walk. That material is yours and belongs in your notebook, your partner exchange, or your clinician's room — not in browser storage. Clear your browser data and the tool forgets the structure. That is the design.

Day 7 · Interactive Practice · low drama detector

A drama from your week. Find where you were in it.

The Low Drama Triangle has three positions. A person rotates through all three. Pick one specific drama from the last seven days, name the position you were in, name what your gremlin was eating, and construct the move out.

Before you begin.

This module engages your daily-life drama patterns and the gremlin directly. You pick a real conflict from your week and look at the position you took inside it. Material you have not looked at — old scorekeeping, a habitual corner of the triangle — may surface.

Day 7 is High intensity · partner required Per the course Safety Framework, this is not a solo-only exercise. Confirm your pairing partner is reachable today and tomorrow before you start, so there is someone to debrief with if the drama opens further than expected.

Step 1 · Name the conflict

A drama from the last seven days.

Not the biggest drama of your life. A small one. The kind you would not normally have noticed.

You can't decline drama from within drama. Step out first. Then choose.
Step 2 · The triangle

Click the corner you were primarily in.

Try the first one that comes. The body usually knows before the intellect. Each corner is keyboard-focusable — Tab to it, press Enter or Space to select.

Step 3 · Name the gremlin food

What was your gremlin eating in this drama?

Notice which naming makes your body do something — a flinch, a flush, a quiet oh. That somatic response is the gremlin being spotted. Multi-select OK.

The gremlin I don't die when you starve me. I look for new food. Naming the food is half the work. The uncomfortable one is usually the most operational — it is the one I have been eating in secret.
Step 4 · The Responsible Game move

What's left when you decline the drama.

The Responsible Game feels less alive at first because the gremlin is starving. That's not a sign of failure — it's the work. Walk through the three questions. Don't write the story. Write the facts.

The Responsible Game is not heroic. It is not virtuous. It is what becomes available when you stop producing food.

Ground. Now.

  1. Stop. Let go of the drama for a moment — it will keep.
  2. Stand up. Both feet flat on the floor. Feel the contact.
  3. Breathe out, long — three slow exhales, each longer than the in-breath.
  4. Name three things you can see in this room. Out loud is best.
  5. Name one thing you can hear. The fridge. Traffic. Your own breath.
  6. Hand on your chest. Feel it rise and fall. You are here, now.
  7. Decide: continue, pause, or stop. Any of the three is fine.

The detector is paused. Take as long as you need.

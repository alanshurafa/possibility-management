How to use this.

Read the Day 7 module first (Days/Day 07 - Low Drama, Gremlin Food, Shifting to Responsible Game.md) and the map note for M13 — Low Drama Triangle. Watch 09 - LowDrama_EN.mp4. Then come here.

Pick a specific small drama from the last seven days before you start. Not the biggest drama of your life — the kind you would not normally have noticed as drama. The sigh. The eye-roll. The forty-five seconds of internal rehearsal. Then walk the detector.

Run it once a day for a week. The position becomes easier to catch in real-time, not just afterward.

What this tool saves.

Each detection — drama, position, gremlin food, Responsible Game move — stores in your browser's local storage on this device only. Nothing is sent anywhere. Clear your browser data and the tool forgets. That is the design.

After three or more entries, the tool surfaces a pattern hint — which corner of the triangle you favor, which food you most often name. That pattern is the work of Day 7 made visible across time.

The detector is not a replacement for the partner exchange or the 48-hour between-module experiment. It is the structured solo version of the practice in the module's Embodied Practice section.

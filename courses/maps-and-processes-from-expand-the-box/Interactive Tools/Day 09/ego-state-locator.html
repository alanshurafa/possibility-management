Day 9 · Interactive Practice · the five-state lens

Five states. One sentence.
Where did it come from?

Take something you said — out loud or in your head — and locate which ego state it came from. The tool gives you the linguistic markers and the body cues for each. Honest answers are usually surprising.

Step 1

The sentence.

What you said

Type something you said — out loud or in your head — recently.

One sentence is enough. The shorter and more honest, the easier to locate. If nothing comes to mind, listen to the next five minutes of your own internal monologue and write down one line.

Step 2

The five-state map.

click a node to highlight its card below

Five locations, not personality types.

You move in and out of all five — sometimes within a single sentence. The map is the lens that lets you notice which one is on the line right now.

Parent at the top. Adult to the right. Child below. Gremlin at the lower left. Demon at the left — small, bounded, named on purpose.

The point of the lens is not to be in Adult all the time. No one is. The point is to see which state is here, so you can decide whether to speak from it.

Step 4

Pick the actual state.

Which state did your sentence actually come from?

Be honest. Most learners think they're in Adult when they're in Critical Parent or Gremlin. The body knows. Ask your body, not your story about yourself. The Adult is the rarest of the five.

Noticed. Now — what state would Adult say it from?

Adult rephrase.

Present-time grown human. Plain voice. Takes responsibility for what's yours, asks the other to take responsibility for what's theirs. The posture is the least dramatic of the five.

Adult sounds like —
  • I am here. I can think clearly about this.
  • I am willing to take responsibility for my part.
  • I am asking you to take responsibility for yours.
  • Here is what I will do. Here is what I won't.

If your sentence came from Demon —

Notice it. The Demon is the part with the capacity for real harm. Not metaphor. Every adult has one. The work in PM is not to integrate it, claim it, or perform it. The work is to know it.

If you have identified Demon today: name it. That is enough for this tool. Log it. Voice-message your partner that you saw it. Ground. Drink water. Move on with your day.

Demon work that goes any further than naming requires a PM trainer or trauma-qualified clinician — see your referral list. The GT Gremlin Transformation training is the longer container designed for this work. The Ego State Locator is the lens, not the journey.

If the Demon is stirring action If naming the Demon surfaces actual urges toward self-harm or harm to another person — apply the buddy immediate-danger protocol (Doc 03 Section F) and the partner agreement (Doc 04 Section B). Crisis lines are in your referral packet. Use them. The Demon being known is the work. The Demon driving action is the cue to reach for outside help, immediately, with no shame.

Ground. Now.

  1. Stop. Stand up — both feet on the floor. Feel the contact.
  2. Breathe out longer than in — three slow exhales, each longer than the in-breath.
  3. Name three things you can see in this room. Out loud is best.
  4. Name one thing you can hear. The fridge. Traffic. Your own breath.
  5. One hand on your sternum, one on your belly — notice your body is here.
  6. Then decide: continue, pause 10 minutes, or stop for today. Any of the three is fine.

The locator is paused. Take as long as you need.

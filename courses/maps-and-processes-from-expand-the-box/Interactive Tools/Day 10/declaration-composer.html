Power I · The Power of Choosing
I.

Own the choice.

Before you can declare, own that you chose. Most attempts at growth reach for declaration without first owning the choice. The declarations do not hold. Type this sentence — edit it until it lands. Read it out loud. Then check the box.

Power II · The Power of Asking Dangerous Questions
II.

Ask a dangerous question.

A dangerous question is one whose answer leads somewhere you cannot un-know. Pick one — or write your own. Then sit with it for a minute before you write what came up.

What am I pretending not to know about my life?
Who would I be if I dropped this story about myself?
What would I have to be willing to feel to leave [thing] behind?
What is the bright principle I am here to source from?
What is the declaration that — if I made it — would change the shape of my next year?
What is one small move I have been refusing to make?
Where am I asking for permission I don't need?
What is mine to create that nobody else can create?

Power III · The Power of Declaring
IV.

Compose the declaration.

A declaration is a speech-act. Not a promise. Not an opinion. Not a wish. It brings a new context into being and walks you into it. Pick a template, or write from scratch. One sentence. Sized to your actual life.

I · Identity declaration
"I am a person who [does X]."
II · Context declaration
"I declare [the context that did not exist before is now in being]."
III · Creation declaration
"I am here to [create what only I can]."
One sentence. Sized to your life, not to a crowd. Sourced from —
Notice — possible shadow source
This declaration may be sourced from a shadow principle — righteousness, revenge, or domination. A declaration from there will collapse. Drop, breathe, and rewrite from the bright principle you picked.
V.

Witnessed. Dated. Saved.

The work begins now. Print this card. Put it where you will see it for the next 90 days — on a wall, on your laptop, on your bathroom mirror.

A few things to know about declarations
  • Loud declarations are weaker than quiet ones. The body knows the difference.
  • You don't declare to other people. You declare to the universe. They are welcome to overhear.
  • A declaration that collapses isn't a failure. It's information — it was sourced from shadow, or the dangerous question wasn't dangerous enough, or it wasn't yours.

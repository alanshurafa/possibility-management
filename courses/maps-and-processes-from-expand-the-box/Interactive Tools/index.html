How to use these.

Read the matching Day module first (`Days/Day NN - …`.md). Then open the day's interactive tool. The tools are not replacements — they are the embodied companions to the written modules.

Each tool stays in your browser. No data leaves the device. Your commitments, your experiments, your declarations, your chain walks, your ego-state log — all local to you. Clear your browser data and the tools forget.

High-intensity days (5, 6, 7, 9) include a consent gate and a grounding tool, and prompt you to confirm your partner is reachable before you begin. Day 6's EHP tool refuses trauma material and routes to clinician referral — that gate is structural, not optional.

What they're not.

These are not the course. The course is the 10 modules, the partner pairings, and the experiments the learner runs in their actual life. The tools are scaffolds — they make some practices repeatable, some distinctions tangible, some commitments visible.

They also don't replace a community manager, a partner, or a clinician. They are tools. The work happens in the learner's life.

Built standalone — double-click any `.html` file to use. The whole suite runs offline once the fonts cache. No servers, no accounts, no telemetry.

# M15 — The 4 Speakings

*Four distinct locations the speaker can speak from — and the fact that the location, not the topic, determines what the speech actually does in the room.*

![6 Types of Speaking — Possibility Management map](../Maps/M15.png)

**What it is.** Speech, like listening, has locations — you have been speaking from one of them for the last hour and may not have noticed which. The map names four: three that live in many traditions and one (Possibility Speaking) that is PM-distinctive and sourced from the bright principles installed on Day 10. Load-bearing claim, symmetrical to the listening map — *where* you speak from, not *what* you say, determines what the speech does in the room. The same sentence from the gremlin and from Adult are not the same speech act.

**At a glance.** Gremlin → drama, righteousness, manipulation; recognizable by its charge · Box → defended position, opinion-as-identity; cannot bend · Adult → clear, contractual, responsible (informs, shares feelings, distinguishes, declares, asks, agrees, sets borders) · Possibility → sourced from a bright principle, brings a new context into the room. Structural parallel of the 4 Listenings — each speaking wants its matching listening. Declaration is the highest form of Possibility Speaking — a spoken act that brings something into being, only real when the bright principle is active.

## How to explain it verbally

Speaking has locations too, parallel to the listenings, and you have been speaking from one of them for the last hour without noticing which. There are four. Gremlin speech carries a charge: drama, righteousness, making someone wrong. Box speech defends a position and treats your opinion as your identity, so it cannot bend. Adult speech is clear and contractual: it informs, shares feelings, asks, agrees, sets borders. Possibility Speaking is sourced from a bright principle and brings a new context into the room. The load-bearing claim is that *where* you speak from, not *what* you say, determines what the speech does in the room.

**If you only remember one thing:** the same words from the gremlin and from Adult are different speech acts; location, not topic, is what moves the room.

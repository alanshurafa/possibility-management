# Audio Companion Recording Kit

| | |
|---|---|
| **For** | The operator (or CM, or any graduate with a steady voice) recording the course's guided audio in one afternoon |
| **What it is** | Every practice script in the course, collected verbatim, with tone, pace, duration, and pause notes per track. The course ships scripts, not audio; this kit is how an operator turns one into the other. |
| **Reads alongside** | [Voice and Style Guide](Voice%20and%20Style%20Guide.md) §7 (the voice-anchor appendix; several tracks below are reproduced from it) · [Solo Centering and Grounding Scripts](Solo%20Centering%20and%20Grounding%20Scripts.md) · [Video Manifest](Video%20Manifest.md) §B (the sibling video task) |

## Why audio, and why this is cheap

The Solo Scripts file already tells learners to record the scripts in their own voice, because a script read silently lands in the head and a script heard lands in the body. An operator-recorded set gives every learner that benefit from day one. All the writing is done. Twelve tracks, roughly two and a half hours of finished audio, one afternoon with retakes.

## The rules of the session (read before pressing record)

1. **THE NO-IMPROVISATION RULE.** Safety scripts are read **as written**. No paraphrasing, no warming up the language, no adding encouragement, no skipping the lines that feel repetitive. These scripts were tuned; their exact wording carries safety function (stop conditions, scope boundaries, present-time anchors). If a sentence feels awkward to read, read it again, not differently. This rule is absolute for every track in this kit.
2. **Verbatim means verbatim.** The scripts below are quoted exactly from their canonical sources, including italics and punctuation. Words in *italics* are lines the learner speaks; read them slightly slower, as if dictating, so the learner can repeat them.
3. **Pause markers.** Markers like `[3s]` in each track's pause map are recording annotations from this kit, not part of the canonical scripts, and are never spoken. Where a script names a duration out loud ("Sixty seconds.", "Minutes 2–3"), say the line, then *hold actual silence for that duration*. The silences are the practice. An audio that compresses them is a reading, not a companion.
4. **Tone, globally.** Plain, unhurried, low warmth, zero performance. You are not narrating a meditation app and you are not hyping anyone. Read the red-pill ceremony aloud once before the session starts and match that register (the Voice and Style Guide calls this calibration).
5. **Production.** Quiet room, soft furnishings, phone on do-not-disturb, any decent USB or lapel mic. Mono, 44.1 kHz. One take per track is fine; re-record sentences you stumble on and edit, or just take the track again. No music, no intro sting, no outro. Name files exactly as listed so module pages can link them predictably.
6. **Spoken preamble per track.** Before the script, record one kit-supplied sentence (given per track below, marked "Preamble"). It is the only non-canonical speech on any track.

## Part 1 · The standing equipment (four tracks + the universal)

### Track 01 · The 60-second universal grounding script

- **Source (operator note):** v1 `03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework.md` §D, reproduced in the Solo Scripts file as Script 1 and in the Voice and Style Guide §7.5.
- **Filename:** `audio-01-grounding-60s.mp3` · **Target duration:** 1.5–2 min finished.
- **Tone/pace:** steadier than every other track. Slow, flat, certain. This is the recording a learner reaches for while far away from themselves; nothing in your voice should ask anything of them.
- **Preamble (spoken):** "This is the sixty-second grounding. Use it any time, for any reason."
- **Pause map:** `[2s]` after each numbered step; `[5s]` after step 4 and step 5 (the naming steps need real time); `[5s]` before step 7.

> **Grounding (60 seconds).**
>
> 1. Stop whatever you were doing in the module. Close the video or the document.
> 2. Stand up. Both feet on the floor. Knees soft.
> 3. Breathe out longer than you breathe in, three times. Audible exhale is fine.
> 4. Name three things you can see in the room.
> 5. Name one thing you can hear.
> 6. Place one hand on your sternum, the other on your belly. Notice that your body is here.
> 7. When you have come back, decide: continue, pause for 10 minutes, or stop the module for today.

### Track 02 · Script 2: the 5-minute centering practice (before every module)

- **Source (operator note):** [Solo Centering and Grounding Scripts](Solo%20Centering%20and%20Grounding%20Scripts.md), Script 2, verbatim.
- **Filename:** `audio-02-centering-5min.mp3` · **Target duration:** 5.5–6 min finished.
- **Tone/pace:** workmanlike. This runs before every module session including Low days; it should feel like equipment, not ceremony.
- **Preamble (spoken):** "The five-minute centering. Run this before you open any module."
- **Pause map:** honor each "Minute N" header as a real minute: read the minute's text in its first ~25 seconds, then hold silence to fill the minute. `[3s]` after each italic spoken line so the learner can say it.

> **Script. 5 minutes. Read aloud or play back. Eyes open or closed — your choice.**
>
> **Minute 1 — arrive.** Sit. Both feet on the floor. Spine upright but not braced. Shoulders down. Hands resting on your thighs or in your lap.
>
> Three breaths. Each exhale longer than the inhale. Make the exhale audible on at least one.
>
> Notice the chair under you. Notice the floor under your feet. Notice the temperature of the air on your face.
>
> **Minute 2 — find your center.** Move your attention to your lower belly — a hand's-width below your navel, behind the abdominal wall. This is the hara. For most people, this is where your center actually lives.
>
> Say out loud, or in your head if you need to: *"My center is here."*
>
> If your attention drifts to your head — to thoughts, to planning, to the day — notice. Bring it back to your belly. The drift is not a problem. The noticing-and-returning is the practice.
>
> If your center is not in your belly — if it actually lives behind your sternum, in your heart — locate it there. Where it actually is.
>
> **Minute 3 — drop a grounding cord.** From your center, declare a thick energetic cord — ship's-rope diameter — going down through your seat, through the floor, through the building, into the earth, all the way to the center.
>
> Say out loud: *"My grounding cord is dropped."*
>
> Not visualization. Declaration. It exists because you said so.
>
> Let excess energy drain. Tension from your day. Charge from the conversation you just had. The buzz of the screens. Allow. Do not push.
>
> **Minute 4 — set a bubble.** Declare a sphere of energy around you. Arm's length in every direction. Under your feet. Above your head.
>
> Say out loud: *"My bubble is set."*
>
> Inside the bubble: your body, your breath, your feelings, your rhythm, this module. Outside the bubble: the room beyond arm's length, the street noise, the partner you were thinking about, the email you have not answered. Real, *not yours, not right now*.
>
> If the bubble feels too big — pull it in. Too small or collapsed — push it out. Your choice.
>
> **Minute 5 — hold all three and arrive.** Center. Grounding cord. Bubble.
>
> Three breaths. Slow. Audible exhale.
>
> Now, out loud: *"I am here. I am centered. I am grounded. I am bubbled. I am ready to begin the module."*
>
> Open the document. Begin.

### Track 03 · Script 3: center + grounding cord + bubble, the standard practice

- **Source (operator note):** Solo Scripts, Script 3, verbatim. Module 3 names this the standing daily centering practice; it runs before every High module.
- **Filename:** `audio-03-center-ground-bubble-10min.mp3` · **Target duration:** 10.5–11 min finished.
- **Tone/pace:** slower than Track 02, with longer silences. The learner has nowhere to be for ten minutes; your reading should make that obvious.
- **Preamble (spoken):** "The ten-minute standard practice: center, grounding cord, bubble. Daily if you can, and always before a High module."
- **Pause map:** honor every "Minute(s) N" header as real elapsed time; `[3s]` after each italic line; the body scan in minutes 8–9 gets `[2s]` after each "— present?"; `[10s]` of clean silence before minute 10.

> **Script. 10 minutes. Chair, water, no phone, no music. Read it through once, then run it.**
>
> **Minute 1 — settle.** Sit. Both feet on the floor. Drink water. Three audible exhales.
>
> Notice three things you can see. One thing you can hear. The temperature of your hands.
>
> **Minutes 2–3 — find center.** Move attention to the lower belly. Hand's-width below the navel, behind the abdominal wall.
>
> *"My center is here."*
>
> Stay there. Attention will drift up — to head, to thought, to plan. Each time you notice the drift, return. The rep is *noticing and returning*, not staying.
>
> If your center actually lives in your heart (some people, particularly artists and healers, do live there), locate it where it actually is. Trust where your body says it is.
>
> Place one hand on your center. Feel the rise and fall of breath through the hand. Sixty seconds.
>
> **Minutes 4–5 — drop the grounding cord.** From your center, declare a thick cord. Ship's-rope diameter. Down through your seat, through the floor, through whatever is beneath the floor, into the earth, into the bedrock, all the way to the core of the planet.
>
> *"My grounding cord is dropped."*
>
> Notice if your cord wants to be a different size or material. Some people's cord is a tree trunk; some, a column of light; some, a steel cable. Do not pick. Notice what it actually is.
>
> Let excess discharge. Energy that is not yours — what you absorbed from the last conversation, the news, the city — drains down the cord. Do not push it. Allow.
>
> Notice colour, weight, temperature in the cord. Do not interpret. Just note.
>
> **Minutes 6–7 — set the bubble.** Declare a sphere of energy around your body. Arm's length in every direction. Under your feet, above your head, behind you.
>
> *"My bubble is set."*
>
> Walk the perimeter of the bubble with your attention. Right. Behind. Left. Front. Above. Below. The edges are where your body's "yours" meets the world's "not yours."
>
> Notice the size. Too big — your energy is stretched thin, you are leaking into the room. Pull it in. Too small or collapsed — you are squeezed, you have given your space away to whoever or whatever is in your head. Push it out to arm's length.
>
> Inside: body, breath, feelings, rhythm, this practice. Outside: real, *not yours*.
>
> **Minutes 8–9 — hold all three.** Center. Cord. Bubble.
>
> Three slow breaths.
>
> Body scan, fast. Top of head — present? Throat — present? Heart — present? Hands — present? Belly — present? Pelvis — present? Legs — present? Feet — present? Note where you are clearly here and where you are still partly somewhere else. Do not fix it. Notice.
>
> **Minute 10 — choose.** What is next?
>
> If you are entering a module: *"I am centered, grounded, bubbled. I am beginning the module."*
>
> If you are entering a conversation: *"I am centered, grounded, bubbled. I am entering this conversation."*
>
> If you are entering sleep: *"I am centered, grounded, bubbled. I am letting the day complete."*
>
> Drink water. Stand up. Go.

### Track 04 · Script 5: the dissolution script (closes every High day)

- **Source (operator note):** Solo Scripts, Script 5, verbatim. Runs at the end of Modules 5, 6, 7, and 9.
- **Filename:** `audio-04-dissolution-8min.mp3` · **Target duration:** 8.5–9 min finished.
- **Tone/pace:** the learner has just been worked. Lower your volume a notch from the other tracks; leave the minute-6 movement section roomy (they are shaking, rolling, stamping while you wait).
- **Preamble (spoken):** "The dissolution script. Run this at the end of any High module, before you return to your day."
- **Pause map:** honor the minute headers; `[3s]` after each italic line; `[60s]` of silence inside minutes 3–4 where the script says "Sixty seconds, eyes soft"; `[30s]` of silence during the minute-6 shaking; `[5s]` before the final "Walk away" paragraph.

> **Script. 8 minutes. Standing if you can, sitting if you must. Notebook open at the end.**
>
> **Minute 1 — confirm equipment.** Stand. Both feet on the floor. Center. Grounding cord. Bubble.
>
> Confirm out loud: *"I am centered. I am grounded. I am bubbled."*
>
> Three audible exhales.
>
> **Minute 2 — name what was worked.** Out loud, in one sentence: *"In this module, I worked [the piece]. What surfaced was [the feeling or material]."*
>
> Naming completes the practice. Until named, the practice has not finished.
>
> **Minutes 3–4 — discharge through the cord.** Bring attention to your center. Then to the cord.
>
> Out loud: *"Anything that is not mine, drain."*
>
> Feel — or imagine until you feel — the cord widening. Whatever you absorbed from the practice that is not yours moves down. Old emotional charge that surfaced and was not yet ready to process. The residue of your Box's noise. Tension in shoulders, jaw, gut. Allow.
>
> Sixty seconds, eyes soft, do nothing but allow.
>
> **Minute 5 — release the bubble's content.** The bubble has been the container for the practice. Now release its content.
>
> Out loud: *"Whatever entered my bubble in this practice that is not mine, leaves."*
>
> Imagine — or know — your bubble's edges becoming briefly permeable, then re-sealing. What was witness energy from your partner's earlier message, what was charge from the video, what was your Box's commentary — all releases.
>
> *"My bubble is set."* Re-confirm.
>
> **Minute 6 — physical movement.** Move the body. Shake hands and arms loosely for 30 seconds. Roll shoulders. Roll neck once each direction. Stretch jaw — yawn audibly, twice. Stamp feet ten times.
>
> Stored energy that did not move during the practice leaves now. The body completes what the practice started.
>
> **Minute 7 — drink and re-orient.** Drink a full glass of water, slowly. Cold water is fine. Hot water is fine.
>
> Look around the room. Name three things out loud. *"I see the lamp. I see the chair. I see the door."*
>
> Name what time of day it is. Name what you are about to do next.
>
> **Minute 8 — capture and close.** Notebook open. Write three lines, fast:
> - What I worked: ___
> - What surfaced: ___
> - What I am leaving in the practice (not carrying into the rest of the day): ___
>
> Close the notebook. Out loud: *"The practice is complete."*
>
> Walk away. The work is done. Trust that what wants to integrate will integrate while you do other things. Do not chew on it.

### Track 05 · Script 9: pre-sleep grounding for processing days

- **Source (operator note):** Solo Scripts, Script 9, verbatim. The evening of every High day.
- **Filename:** `audio-05-pre-sleep-4min.mp3` · **Target duration:** 4.5–5 min finished.
- **Tone/pace:** quieter than every other track. Read at the edge of a murmur; the listener is sitting on their bed with the lights low. Slow everything by a third.
- **Preamble (spoken):** "Pre-sleep grounding, for the evening of a High day."
- **Pause map:** honor the minute headers; `[3s]` after every italic line; `[4s]` between the four closing sentences of minute 3; let the recording end with `[10s]` of room tone rather than a crisp stop.

> **Script. 4 minutes. Sitting on the edge of the bed, lights dim, phone away.**
>
> **Minute 1 — settle the room.** Put your phone in another room or face-down on do-not-disturb. Lights low. Water by the bed.
>
> Sit on the edge of the bed. Both feet on the floor.
>
> Three audible exhales, each longer than the inhale. Make them slow.
>
> **Minute 2 — center, ground, bubble.** Fast version, lying-down-ready.
>
> Center — hand on belly. *"My center is here."*
>
> Cord — *"My grounding cord is dropped. Anything that is not mine drains."*
>
> Bubble — *"My bubble is set. The day is outside it. The bedroom is the size of my bubble."*
>
> **Minute 3 — close the day.** Out loud, in one sentence each:
> - What I worked today: *"Today I worked [Day __, the practice, the partner exchange]."*
> - What surfaced: *"What surfaced was [the feeling / the realization / the question]."*
> - What is incomplete: *"What is incomplete and waiting is [name it, do not solve it]."*
> - What I am setting down for sleep: *"I am setting down [the worry, the conversation, the loop]. It will be there in the morning if it matters. I am letting sleep do its work."*
>
> Naming closes the loop. Unnamed loops keep running through the night.
>
> **Minute 4 — release the cord and rest.** Out loud: *"The work for today is complete."*
>
> Lie down. Feel the bed under you — full length of your body. Hands resting at your sides or on your belly.
>
> Center is still here. Grounding cord is still dropped — it does not turn off for sleep. Bubble is the size of the bed.
>
> Three slow breaths. Do not try to sleep. Let sleep come.
>
> If thought-loops start — name them: *"That is thought. I am not following it tonight."* Return attention to the cord, to the belly, to the breath.

### Track 06 · The red-pill ceremony (Module 1)

- **Source (operator note):** v1 `Days/Day 01 - Orientation, New Context, Radical Responsibility.md`, embodied practice; reproduced verbatim in the Voice and Style Guide §7.1. The closing paragraph is canonical and is recorded too.
- **Filename:** `audio-06-red-pill-ceremony.mp3` · **Target duration:** 7–8 min finished.
- **Tone/pace:** plain and unhurried, zero drama. The ceremony's weight comes from the learner's own body, not from your delivery. Resist any temptation to make the red-pill option sound like the right answer; the blue-pill paragraph is read with exactly the same respect.
- **Preamble (spoken):** "The red-pill ceremony, from Module 1. Stand up before you press play, or pause now and stand."
- **Pause map:** `[5s]` after "Say it."; `[10s]` after each "Notice what your body did" line; `[10s]` after each of the two "Pause. Notice." lines; `[15s]` after "Choose." before the final instruction; `[5s]` before the closing paragraph, which is read more softly.

> **Script.**
>
> Stand up. Both feet on the floor. Shoulders down. Hands loose at your sides.
>
> Take three breaths, exhale longer than inhale. Audible exhale.
>
> Now, out loud: *"In the next 30 days I am going to do this course."* Say it.
>
> Notice what your body did when you said that. Did you tighten? Did you brace? Did you brighten? Whatever it did, that's information.
>
> Now, out loud: *"I can stop at any time. I can pause at any time. I can put it down at any time. Nothing makes me do this."*
>
> Notice what your body did *that* time.
>
> Now, the choice. Two options. Speak each one out loud, in order, and notice which one your body recognizes as true.
>
> *"Blue pill. I'm not doing this. The course is interesting but I'm not actually in it. I'll watch the videos when I get to them. I won't pair with anyone. I won't run the experiments. I'm a tourist."*
>
> Pause. Notice.
>
> *"Red pill. I am in this. For the next 30 days I am the author of what this becomes for me. I will pair with my partner. I will run the experiments. I will use the safety practices when I need them. I will not pretend to be in if I am not. I am in."*
>
> Pause. Notice.
>
> Choose. Say the chosen sentence one more time, alone, in the room you are in, with your body present.
>
> Sit down. Write the date and your chosen sentence on a piece of paper. Put it where you will see it during this course.

And the canonical closing paragraph, read after a pause:

> If you cannot honestly say the red-pill sentence right now — that is not a problem. That is data. The course is not for everyone right now. Pause. Talk to the community manager. The course will still be here in three months.

### Track 07 · Centering, grounding cord, bubble, golden cube (Module 3)

- **Source (operator note):** v1 `Days/Day 03 - Liquid State, Center-Ground-Bubble, Five Bodies.md`, embodied practice, verbatim. This is the module-length version that installs the equipment; Tracks 02–03 are its daily companions.
- **Filename:** `audio-07-equipment-install-15min.mp3` · **Target duration:** 15–16 min finished.
- **Tone/pace:** patient and concrete. The numbered sections are stations; announce each number cleanly, then slow down inside it.
- **Preamble (spoken):** "The Module 3 equipment practice: center, grounding cord, bubble, golden cube. You need a chair, water, a notebook, and silence."
- **Pause map:** every "Sixty seconds." is `[60s]` of real silence; `[3s]` after each italic declaration; in step 5, `[15s]` after the piece is placed in the cube and `[120s]` of silence for the looking ("Two or three minutes"); `[5s]` before step 6.

> **Script.**
>
> Sit upright, both feet on the floor, hands on thighs. Or stand, feet hip-width apart, knees soft, shoulders down.
>
> Three breaths. Exhale longer than inhale. Audible exhale.
>
> **1. Center.** Move your attention to your lower belly — a hand's-width below your navel, deep inside, behind the abdominal wall. Put a hand there if it helps. Say silently or aloud: *"My center is here."* Notice if your attention is actually there or has drifted to your head. Bring it back. Sixty seconds.
>
> If your center turns out to live in your heart or somewhere else specific in your torso — locate where it actually is. Do not move it to where you think it should be.
>
> **2. Grounding cord.** **Declare** — by intention — a thick energetic cord (the diameter of a ship's rope) dropping from your center, through your seat, through the floor, through the soil, to the centre of the earth. Not a visualisation. A declaration: *"My grounding cord is dropped."* It exists because you said so. Sixty seconds.
>
> Now use it. Let excess energy — tension in the shoulders, charge from your day, anything that wants to leave — drain down the cord. You do not push it. You allow it. Sixty seconds.
>
> If a colour comes — silver, blue, copper, anything — note it without making it mean anything. If no colour comes, note that too.
>
> **3. Bubble.** Declare a sphere of personal space around you — arm's length in every direction, including under your feet and above your head. *"My bubble is set."* Notice what is inside (your body, sensations, breath, feelings, thoughts) and what is outside (the room, street noise, whoever you were just thinking about). Sixty seconds.
>
> Check the size. Is the bubble bigger than arm's length — out into the next room, the conversation that ended an hour ago? Pull it in. Is it collapsed against your skin? Push it out. Roughly arm's length.
>
> **4. Hold all three.** Center. Cord. Bubble. All three live at the same time. Three breaths. This is the operating posture for everything ahead. Notice what it feels like — not what it *should* feel like. Quiet weight in the seat, easier breathing, a felt edge where the bubble is, or very little. Any of these is a report.
>
> **5. Golden cube.** From this centered, grounded, bubbled state, open a cube of golden light in front of you, roughly the size of a small room, two paces away. *"The cube is open."*
>
> Place **one small piece of thoughtware work** inside it — pick one:
>
> - The words *liquid state* — look at them in the cube; what rises in your body?
> - A piece of your Box from Day 2 — look at it from outside the bubble; what does it look like with this much distance?
> - A specific situation in your life this week — a conversation, a decision — ask, *"What would I do here from center, ground, and bubble?"*
>
> Stay centered, grounded, bubbled while you look at what is in the cube. Two or three minutes. Do not solve. Look.
>
> **6. Close.** *"The cube is closed."* Keep your center, cord, and bubble. Three breaths. Open your eyes if they were closed. Drink water.
>
> In your notebook, fast, no editing: *Center — where I found it · Cord — colour or sensation · Bubble — size before / after · Cube — what I placed, what I noticed.* Three or four lines. The point is not insight. The point is the rep.

### Track 08 · Low-intensity feelings practice (Module 5)

- **Source (operator note):** v1 `Days/Day 05 - Feelings vs Emotions, Old Map of Feelings, Numbness Bar.md`, embodied practice, verbatim.
- **Filename:** `audio-08-low-intensity-feelings.mp3` · **Target duration:** 24–26 min finished (three object rounds).
- **Tone/pace:** neutral and roomy. The learner is hunting 2% signals; an expressive voice drowns them. The line "They are there. You have been not registering them." is read as plain fact, not reassurance.
- **Safety note:** Module 5 is High-intensity and consent-gated. The preamble names that; the stop instructions at the end of the script are read exactly as written.
- **Preamble (spoken):** "The low-intensity feelings practice, from Module 5. Run this only inside the module, after its consent check, with your partner reachable."
- **Pause map:** `[10s]` after "Pick up the object. Hold it. Look at it."; `[10s]` after the out-loud question; `[60s]` for each "Stay with the sensation for 60 seconds"; `[60s]` for each repeat with a second or third feeling; `[15s]` for the notebook lines; the second and third object rounds are guided by one sentence each ("Pick a second object. Do it again.") followed by `[4min]` of silence per round — announce that the silence is coming.

> **Script.**
>
> Sit. Both feet on the floor. Center, ground, drop a bubble (Day 3 practice — if it has fallen out of your body, re-read M05 first).
>
> Pick up the object. Hold it. Look at it.
>
> Ask yourself, out loud: *"Right now, in my emotional body, with this object — am I feeling some anger, some sadness, some fear, some joy?"*
>
> Do not look for big feelings. Look for low-intensity ones. 2%. 5%. The almost-imperceptible kind. They are there. You have been not registering them.
>
> Name the first feeling out loud: *"I feel some [anger / sadness / fear / joy] looking at this [object]."*
>
> Locate the sensation in the body. Where exactly? Bones? Heart? Nervous system? Eyes? Be specific. Touch the place with your hand if it helps.
>
> Stay with the sensation for 60 seconds. Do not analyze. Do not story. Do not figure out why. Just register. Breathe.
>
> Notice if a second feeling surfaces underneath the first. Often the first is on top and the next is deeper. Name it. Locate it. 60 seconds. If a third surfaces, repeat.
>
> When the feelings stop arriving, set the object down. Take three breaths.
>
> In your notebook, write fast, no editing: *Object · feeling · location · what the feeling seemed to be about, if anything came clear.* Three or four lines. The point is not insight — the point is the rep.
>
> Pick a second object. Do it again. Do three objects total.

Also record, at the end of the track, the module's two stop instructions, verbatim:

> If you find yourself in an emotion — a feeling about something years ago, with a story attached, lasting longer than five minutes — stop, ground, and note it. Do not try to process it. Day 6 is for emotional healing process. Today is for **registering**.
>
> If you find yourself dissociating — floating, watching from outside, unable to feel anything anywhere — stop, ground (Section D, top of file), end the practice. Voice-message your partner that you stopped. This is not failure. Your system told you it was at limit.

If intensity passes forty percent, or you lose present time, or the original event becomes vivid in your body: stop, ground, and message your partner. Stopping is a valid completion."
- **Pause map:** honor every stated duration as real silence (Position 0: settle pauses; Position 1: `[15s]` after "Wait. Listen."; Position 2: up to `[3min]` of held silence with one quiet time-mark halfway; Position 3: `[3–5min]` silence per feeling, announce the silence; Position 4: `[10s]` between closing steps; Position 5: `[5s]` between steps). `[3s]` after every italic spoken line.

> **Script.**
>
> **Position 0 — Set foundation (5 min).**
> Sit. Both feet on the floor. Center. Drop a grounding cord into the earth. Set a bubble at arm's-length. Imagine a golden cube around the bubble — the container of this practice. Three breaths, exhale longer than inhale.
>
> Name out loud one bright principle you are sourcing from. (Examples: clarity, presence, integrity, gentleness, truth.) One word.
>
> **Position 1 — Make contact with yourself (3 min).**
> One hand on chest, one on belly. Speak to yourself by name: *"[Name], what can I do for you?"* Wait. Listen. (If the answer is "stop the practice" — stop. That is a valid completion.)
>
> Name the small stored emotion you have chosen, plainly: *"I have a stored emotion about [X]."* One sentence. No story yet.
>
> **Position 2 — Navigate to liquid state (5–8 min).**
> Close your eyes. Locate the stored emotion in your body. Where exactly? Heart? Throat? Belly? Bones? Specific.
>
> Let the sensation get *bigger*. Not the story — the sensation. If a sound wants to come (a sigh, a hum, a soft groan, even a single word), let it. Sound moves emotion faster than language.
>
> Stay until you feel a softening — a "yes, I am with this" quality. That is liquid state. If you cannot find it in 5 minutes, ground and end here. The bar may still be high today. Try again another day.
>
> **Position 3 — Unmix (8–12 min).**
> Eyes closed: of the four feelings — anger, sadness, fear, joy — **which two or three are in this mixed emotion?** Name them out loud. *"In this I have some anger and some sadness."* Whatever is true.
>
> Pick *one* of them. The one that wants to move first. Let it take more space. Anger as anger — jaw setting, arms wanting to push, bones solid. Sadness as sadness — chest softening, throat thickening, tears if they come. Fear as fear — skin prickling, breath shallow, attention sharpening.
>
> 3 to 5 minutes. **One feeling at a time.** No story. If the mind tries to explain "this is because of…" — set the explanation down and return to the sensation.
>
> If a second feeling rises behind the first, let it move. Same way. 3 to 5 minutes.
>
> If a memory of the original event surfaces, register but do not narrate it. *"I see myself at seven, in the kitchen."* That is enough. Stay with the feeling, not the storyboard.
>
> **Position 4 — Closing (5–7 min).**
> When the body has softened and the breath deepened, begin to close. Open eyes. Name three things you can see. Drink water. One hand on the chair, one foot pressed to the floor. You are here, in this room, now.
>
> If a new decision arrived (an old *"I will never need anyone"* becoming *"I can let myself be witnessed now"*) — write it down, one sentence, present tense.
>
> Out loud: *"Thank you, [Name]. We will continue when ready."* The container is closing.
>
> **Position 5 — Cleanup (3 min).**
> The golden cube dissolves. The bubble softens. The grounding cord remains. Stand. Walk three steps. Drink water. Notice the temperature of the room. You are back.
>
> In your notebook, fast, no editing: *Mixed emotion · component feelings · what moved · what didn't · any new decision · how my body feels right now.*

### Track 11 · The ego-state locator (Module 9)

- **Source (operator note):** v1 `Days/Day 09 - Ego States, Problem Ownership, Learning Spiral.md`, embodied practice, verbatim.
- **Filename:** `audio-11-ego-state-locator.mp3` · **Target duration:** 28–32 min finished.
- **Tone/pace:** matter-of-fact for the first four states. For the Demon visit (state 5), do not drop into a hushed or ominous register; read it exactly as plainly as the rest. The boundedness is carried by the words, and your delivery must not add drama the script deliberately avoids.
- **Safety note:** the Demon visit is 90 seconds and locator-only by design. **Read every bolded boundary line as written.** Do not shorten the return-to-center instructions between states.
- **Preamble (spoken):** "The ego-state locator, from Module 9. Run this only inside the module, after its consent check. This is a locator, not a journey. You will need space to stand, water, and a notebook."
- **Pause map:** `[20s]` after each state's posture instruction (finding the posture takes time); `[10s]` after each "Out loud" sentence prompt; `[10s]` for each "Return to center."; `[10s]` for "Take three breaths before the next one."; in state 5, time the visit honestly — `[15s]` after the locator sentence, `[10s]` after the noticing instruction, then move to the breaths; `[15s]` before the final notebook paragraph.

> **Script.**
>
> Stand. Both feet on the floor. Center, ground, drop a bubble (Day 3 — if it has fallen out of your body, re-read M05 first).
>
> You will visit five states. Each gets a posture and a sentence or two. Return to center between each.
>
> **1. Parent (3 min).** Take the posture of authority. Spine up, chin slightly raised, hands on hips or one finger pointing — whichever your body recognizes as the posture a parent (or teacher, boss) took when they were *being parental* at you. Out loud: one Critical Parent sentence — *"You should…", "Good people don't…", "That's not how this is done."* Use a real one. Then, same posture, one Nurturing Parent sentence — *"Let me take care of that for you,"* or whatever the over-functioning helper-voice in you actually says. Notice whose voice it actually is. Return to center.
>
> **2. Child (3 min).** Soften the posture. Drop the chin. Let your weight come down. Some learners crouch or sit on the floor. Find the posture your body takes when no one is watching and the day has been long. Out loud: one Free Child sentence — *"I want…", "That looks fun," "Let's…"* — with appetite, like a six-year-old. Then, same softer posture, one Adapted Child sentence — *"It's okay, I don't need anything," "I'm sorry," "Is that all right?"* Notice how old you feel. Return to center.
>
> **3. Adult (4 min).** Stand again. Both feet on the floor. Knees soft. Shoulders down. Weight even. Eye level. Hands easy at your sides. This is your present-time grown body, here, now, not performing anything. Out loud: *"I am here. I can think clearly about this. I am willing to take responsibility for my part. I am asking you to take responsibility for yours."* Or any sentence in that shape, in your own words. Notice that the posture is the least dramatic of the five. The voice is plain. **This is the state PM is practiced from.** Stay an extra 30 seconds. Get the felt sense of it so you can find your way back later. Return to center.
>
> **4. Gremlin (3 min).** Shift posture. The Gremlin's body is often slightly hunched, slightly turned away, conspiratorial — leaning in, like at a bar, telling someone something they shouldn't. Find the posture your body takes when you are about to enjoy something you know you shouldn't. Out loud, in a *sotto voce* tone — quieter than normal, almost muttered — one Gremlin sentence: *"They never appreciate you," "You're not actually going to do this," "You're better than them," "Just give up," "Go on, have one."* Use one your Gremlin actually says. Notice the small charge of pleasure — that's gremlin food. Notice it without indulging it. Return to center. **Take three breaths before the next one.**
>
> **5. Demon (90 seconds, bounded).** The shortest and most specific visit. You are not going to perform your Demon. You are not going to enter it. You are going to **locate it in your body and acknowledge it is here.**
>
> Stand still. Place one hand on your sternum, the other on your belly. Out loud, in your own voice, plainly, say once: *"I have a Demon. I know it is here. It has the capacity to harm. I am not it. I am the one who can know it."*
>
> Notice where in your body the awareness of the Demon lives. Some learners feel coldness, some tightness, some a strange calm, some feel nothing — that is also data.
>
> **Do not perform the Demon's voice. Do not give it lines. Do not try to "meet" it. This is a locator, not a journey.**
>
> Take three slow breaths, audible exhale. Both hands on your sternum. Out loud: *"I am here. I am in my Adult. The Demon is known. I am not it."* Return to center fully.
>
> Sit down. Drink water. Write fast, no editing: *which state was easiest to find · which was hardest · which surprised me · what posture each one took in my body.* Five or six lines. The point is the rep, not insight.

### Track 12 · The declaration (Module 10)

- **Source (operator note):** v1 `Days/Day 10 - Map of Possibility, Bright Principles, Three Powers, Integration.md`, embodied practice, verbatim.
- **Filename:** `audio-12-declaration.mp3` · **Target duration:** 18–20 min finished.
- **Tone/pace:** spacious. This track holds more silence per spoken word than any other module script; the learner is composing real sentences about their actual life between your lines. Read the "If it does not feel true — that is data" passage with particular flatness; it is permission, not consolation.
- **Preamble (spoken):** "The declaration practice, from Module 10. Find a place where you will not be interrupted. Stand when you are ready."
- **Pause map:** `[10s]` after "You are arriving in your own house."; `[30s]` after the sourcing question ("what principle is sourcing me right now?"); `[60s]` after each of the three declaration prompts (the learner needs composing time); `[10s]` after each "Say it." or equivalent; `[15s]` before the final writing instruction; close the track with `[10s]` of room tone.

> **Script.**
>
> Stand up. Both feet on the floor. Knees soft. Shoulders down. Hands loose.
>
> Three breaths. Exhale longer than inhale. Audible.
>
> **Locate yourself.** Drop your weight into your feet. Notice the ground. Notice the room. You are not collecting yourself for performance. You are arriving in your own house.
>
> **Notice the principle.** Ask quietly: *what principle is sourcing me right now?* Do not judge it. Just name it. Do not declare from inside a shadow principle. If you cannot find a bright principle to source from, breathe, drop, and wait. The declaration can wait three minutes; it cannot wait until the source is right.
>
> **First declaration — what you are.** Out loud, one sentence. Not what you do, not what you want. *What you are.* "I am a person who tells the truth." "I am the author of my life." "I am someone who can stay in the room when it gets hard." Say it. Hear it in your body. Notice whether it is true.
>
> If it does not feel true — that is data. Soften the claim until it does. *"I am someone learning to tell the truth"* is closer than *"I am a person who tells the truth"* if the latter is reaching past you. (This is the is-glue dissolver doing closing-day work: a declaration the size of your actual life holds; one welded past your lived experience collapses on contact with life.)
>
> **Second declaration — what you commit to.** One sentence. Name the work you are taking forward. The one piece. "I am running the daily box catch for the next 90 days." "I am asking one dangerous question per week." Specific. Small enough to actually do.
>
> **Third declaration — what gameworld you are now entering.** One sentence. The course was one gameworld; you are leaving it. What is the next? "I am entering the gameworld of my marriage on different terms." "I am entering the gameworld of being an adult to my own children." The next gameworld is not necessarily grand. It is the actual one.
>
> Sit down. Write the three declarations on paper. Date it. Put it where you will see it.

Also record the module's closing line for this practice, verbatim, after a pause:

> If you cannot honestly make any of the three right now — that is also data. Write what would be true today, even if small. Smallness is calibration, not failure.

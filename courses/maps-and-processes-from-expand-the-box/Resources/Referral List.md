# Referral List — Template

> **What this is.** A template the Community Manager (CM) uses to generate the per-learner referral packet at enrollment. It is not a populated list. It supplies the structure, the canonical PM-aware referral pointers, and the verbatim language that must appear in every packet.
>
> **Who maintains it.** The CM. Updated quarterly (see Section 9) and at the start of every cohort.
>
> **When it is used.** At enrollment, after the learner completes intake (Doc 04 Section A). The CM fills in region-appropriate sections from intake data, removes sections that don't apply (e.g., Section 6 only appears if Screen 4 returned yes), and delivers the result as the per-learner packet (Doc 04 Section E) — as a PDF and on the platform — before content access opens.

---

## 0. Screen → section map (which intake screen each section serves)

Doc 04 Section A.2 has five intake screens. Each one, when it returns a flag, requires specific resources in the learner's packet. This table maps every screen to the section(s) here that answer it, so the CM can see coverage at a glance and not ship a packet that is missing the resource a screen demands.

| Intake screen (Doc 04 A.2) | What it flags | Section(s) in this list | Always or conditional |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Screen 1 — Suicidality** | Suicidal ideation, plan, intent | **§2 Crisis lines** (first in packet) + **§4 Licensed clinicians** | §2 always; §4 always |
| **Screen 2 — Trauma & dissociation** | Flashbacks, lost time, dissociation | **§5 Trauma-qualified clinicians** (+ §4) | §5 for any Screen-2 learner; many CMs keep it for all |
| **Screen 3 — Active addiction / compulsion** | Alcohol, drugs, self-harm, food restricting/bingeing, gambling, sex, screens out of control | **§5A Addiction & Recovery resources** (+ §4 for dual diagnosis) | §5A whenever Screen 3 flags; safe to keep for all |
| **Screen 4 — Coercive / unsafe environment** | Safety, housing, children, income at risk if the learner changes behavior | **§6 Domestic violence & legal resources** | §6 only if Screen 4 returned yes |
| **Screen 5 — Mania / psychosis / spiritual emergency** | Mania, psychosis, hallucinations, psychiatric hospitalization | **§4 Licensed clinicians** + **§2 Crisis lines** | both always; clinician confirmation gated per Doc 04 D |

**Honest gaps to note.**
- **Screen 5 has no dedicated "psychiatric / prescriber" subsection.** It is served by the general licensed-clinician directories in §4 plus the crisis lines in §2. If a learner flags Screen 5, the CM should add the learner's own prescriber as the primary contact (per Doc 04 A.2 Screen 5, access is deferred until the prescribing clinician confirms in writing) and use §4 only as backup. A future revision may add a §4 sub-list of psychiatry/med-management directories.
- **§3 (PM-aware coaches) maps to no screen.** It is opportunity-side support, not a gate response. It is never a substitute for the clinician sections a screen requires.
- **§5A (new) is the home for Screen 3.** Before this revision the list had no addiction resource and Screen 3 had nowhere to point. That gap is now closed.

## 1. How to populate this template (CM-side notes)

This is the source; the per-learner packet is the output. To generate a packet:

1. **Read the learner's intake responses (Doc 04 Section A.1).** You need country, state/province, primary language, and answers to all five screens (1 suicidality, 2 trauma, 3 addiction/compulsion, 4 coercive environment, 5 mania/psychosis). See the Section 0 map for which section each screen drives.
2. **Save a copy** as `Referral Packet — [Learner Name] — [Cohort ID].md`. Do not edit the source.
3. **Fill in regional sections.** Keep the learner's country first in Section 2.
4. **Remove sections that do not apply.** Screen 4 no → remove Section 6. Screen 2 no → Section 5 is optional (many CMs leave it in). Screen 3 no → Section 5A is optional (the national addiction/recovery pointers are safe to keep for all learners; remove only if you are trimming the packet). Screen 5 yes → add the learner's own prescriber as the primary clinician contact in Section 4 (Doc 04 A.2 requires written prescriber confirmation before access).
5. **Confirm every named coach or clinician is currently practicing and accepting clients** before the packet ships. Stale referrals are worse than no referrals.
6. **Generate the PDF** and store on the platform. Packet must exist before content access opens (Doc 04 Section K).

The verbatim language in Sections 2 and 8 is canonical. Do not paraphrase.

## 2. CRISIS LINES (top of every learner's packet)

> This section is **always first** in every per-learner packet. Not buried, not on a back page. First. The learner's own country goes at the top of the list.

### Always-included list

- **United States.** 988 — Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (call or text). Crisis Text Line — text **HOME** to **741741**.
- **United Kingdom.** Samaritans — **116 123** (free, 24/7). SHOUT — text **85258**.
- **Canada.** **9-8-8** — Suicide Crisis Helpline (call or text, 24/7).
- **Australia.** Lifeline — **13 11 14** (24/7). Beyond Blue — **1300 22 4636**.
- **EU general.** **116 123** — Emotional Support Helpline (available in many EU countries; check local availability).
- **Germany.** Telefonseelsorge — **0800 111 0 111** or **0800 111 0 222** (free, 24/7).
- **France.** **3114** — numéro national de prévention du suicide (free, 24/7).
- **International directory.** **findahelpline.com** — point of last resort if the learner's country is not listed.

### Note to the learner (include verbatim in the packet)

> If your country is not listed above, see **findahelpline.com** or search **"[your country] suicide crisis line"** immediately. Do not delay because the list above doesn't have your number — the directories exist precisely for this.

### Verbatim safety instruction (include in every packet, immediately after the crisis-line list)

> **If you are in immediate physical danger to yourself or another person right now, contact the relevant number above OR your country's general emergency line (e.g., 911 in the US, 999 in the UK, 112 in the EU, 000 in Australia). The course is paused while you are safe.**

### CM note (do not include in the learner-facing packet)

Crisis line numbers change. Verify each at every quarterly update (Section 9). The numbers above were canonical at last review; assume nothing.

## 5. TRAUMA-QUALIFIED CLINICIANS (template)

> Include for any learner who routed through Screen 2 (Trauma & Dissociation) at intake — even if they passed without restrictions. Many CMs leave it in for all learners.

### The PM-precise distinction

PM distinguishes between two things that look similar from outside:

- **Emotional Healing Process (EHP)** — *in scope* for PM. Completing feelings that have been incomplete, often for a long time. EHP can be intense; it is held with grounding, partner support, and the consent practices in this course.
- **Trauma processing** — *not in scope* for PM. Reorganization of nervous-system patterning around overwhelming events. This is the work of a trauma-qualified clinician.

A learner with significant trauma who treats this course as a substitute for trauma care will likely get hurt. The course can be valuable for that learner — *alongside* a trauma-qualified clinician who is in the loop.

### What "trauma-qualified" means

Trained and currently practicing in one or more of:

- **EMDR** (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)
- **Somatic Experiencing** (Levine)
- **Sensorimotor Psychotherapy** (Ogden)
- **TF-CBT** (Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy)
- **IFS** (Internal Family Systems, when used for trauma)
- **Equivalent evidence-based modality** the CM can verify

A generalist therapist who lists "trauma" as one of many interests is not trauma-qualified for this purpose. Look for the modality.

### Named trauma-qualified clinicians (where verified by CM)

2. **[Clinician Name], [credential].** [Location] · [Modality] · [Languages] · [Contact]

## 5A. ADDICTION & RECOVERY RESOURCES (template — serves Screen 3)

> Include for any learner who flagged **Screen 3 (Active addiction / compulsive behavior)** at intake (Doc 04 Section A.2). Screen 3 covers more than alcohol and drugs: it names **self-harm, restricting/bingeing food, gambling, sexual behavior, and screens/scrolling.** The resources below cover that full range. The national pointers are safe to keep in every packet; the disordered-eating and self-harm pointers especially belong in any packet where Screen 3 touched food or self-harm.
>
> Doc 04 Screen 3 defers or gates a flagged learner until there is **active professional support in writing** — a 12-step sponsor plus meeting attendance, or a treating clinician. This section is how the CM points the learner at that support. It is not a substitute for the licensed-clinician section (§4); for a diagnosable substance use or eating disorder, §4 applies in parallel.

### How to use this section

- Lead with the learner's **own country's** national line/service, then list the cross-border fallbacks.
- For a learner whose Screen 3 answer was about **food**, foreground the disordered-eating pointers. For **self-harm**, foreground the self-harm + crisis-line pointers (crisis lines live in §2 and are already first in the packet).
- Mutual-aid meetings (AA, NA, SMART) are free and immediate; clinical treatment (via §4 and the locators below) runs alongside, not instead.

### United States

- **SAMHSA National Helpline — 1-800-662-HELP (4357).** Free, confidential, 24/7, 365 days, English and Spanish. Treatment referral and information for substance use *and* mental-health concerns. Also text your ZIP code to **435748 (HELP4U)**. *(Number confirmed current at last review against samhsa.gov — re-confirm each quarter.)*
- **SAMHSA treatment locator — findtreatment.gov.** Searchable directory of licensed substance-use and mental-health treatment facilities. Pair it with the helpline number above rather than quoting a separate number from the locator page.
- **SMART Recovery — smartrecovery.org.** Free, evidence-based (CBT / motivational interviewing) mutual-aid meetings, in person and online. Covers any addictive or compulsive behavior — alcohol, drugs, gambling, and others — not just substances.
- **Alcoholics Anonymous — aa.org/find-aa.** Free meetings everywhere; in-person, online, and phone.
- **Narcotics Anonymous — na.org** (meeting search at na.org/meetingsearch). Free; open to anyone.

### Disordered eating (United States)

- **National Eating Disorders Association (NEDA) — nationaleatingdisorders.org/get-help.** Free, confidential screening tool, a specialist treatment finder, and low-cost peer-support options. **Note for the CM:** NEDA closed its telephone helpline; do not put a NEDA phone number in a packet. NEDA now routes urgent need to text support and screening. Confirm against their site each quarter.
- **Crisis Text Line — text NEDA to 741741.** 24/7 text support for someone in distress about eating or body concerns. (This is the same Crisis Text Line in §2, reached with the keyword **NEDA** instead of **HOME**.)
- **NAMI HelpLine — nami.org/help.** Information and referral for mental-health concerns including eating disorders. **Not a crisis line and not 24/7** (staffed business hours, weekdays); for after-hours or crisis use §2.
- For an active eating disorder, treat it as a clinical condition: use §4 (licensed clinicians) in parallel, and §2 crisis lines if there is acute risk.

### Self-harm (United States)

- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** (call or text **988**) and **Crisis Text Line** (text **HOME** to **741741**) — both already first in the packet (§2). For self-harm without suicidal intent these are still the right immediate contacts.
- For ongoing self-harm, a licensed clinician (§4), ideally trauma-qualified (§5) when self-harm is trauma-linked, is the standing support.

### United Kingdom

- **FRANK — talktofrank.com.** Honest drug and alcohol information and confidential advice. Phone **0300 123 6600** (24/7); text **82111**. *(Confirmed current at last review against talktofrank.com — re-confirm each quarter.)*
- **We Are With You (WithYou) — wearewithyou.org.uk.** Free, confidential drug, alcohol, and mental-health support, including a web chat with trained recovery workers seven days a week, and local services across England and Scotland.
- **SMART Recovery UK — smartrecovery.org.uk.** Free evidence-based meetings, online and in person.
- **Beat (eating disorders) — beateatingdisorders.org.uk.** The UK eating-disorder charity, with helplines and online support. The CM confirms the current helpline number from Beat's site before adding it to a packet rather than quoting a number from memory.

### Your region (fallback for any country not listed above)

> If your country is not listed above, search **"[your national health service] + addiction support"** or **"[your country] drug and alcohol helpline"** — for example, the NHS in the UK, your national or state health department, or your GP/family doctor. For an immediate crisis (including self-harm or suicidal thoughts), use the crisis lines at the top of this packet (§2) or **findahelpline.com**, which lists vetted lines by country. Mutual-aid fellowships are global: **AA (aa.org)**, **NA (na.org)**, and **SMART Recovery (smartrecovery.org)** all have meeting finders that cover most countries.

### CM note (do not include in the learner-facing packet)

- **Do not invent a helpline number.** Where a number is given above it was verified against the organization's own site at last review; re-confirm each quarter (Section 9). Where it is not given (NEDA, NAMI, Beat, the directories), name the organization and its site and have the learner — or you — confirm the current number, rather than guessing digits.
- Mutual aid is **not** clinical treatment and **not** a gate substitute. Doc 04 Screen 3 requires written confirmation of active support before access (sponsor + meetings, or a treating clinician). A list of meetings is a starting point, not the confirmation itself.
- For a learner with both addiction and trauma (common), §5 and §5A apply together; for both addiction and a mood/psychotic disorder, add §4 and, for Screen 5, the prescriber.

## 7. PM TRAINER PATH AND ONGOING COMMUNITY

> For learners who completed the course and want to deepen. Not crisis-side — opportunity-side. Include from the start so the learner knows what's beyond Day 10.

### Deepening pathways

- **PM Trainer Path.** The structured next-step training for learners who want to become PM practitioners. See the **PM main site at possibilitymanagement.org** for current details. (CM: update with the exact URL slug at quarterly review if known. Do not invent a URL.)
- **Possibility Lab.** Longer, immersive deepening containers — typically multi-week intensives. Run by senior PM trainers globally. See possibilitymanagement.org.
- **Possibility Team.** Small ongoing groups (often weekly) where PM is practiced live between trainings: dangerous questions, experiments, feedback. **Open to anyone** — no invitation and no prerequisite training; a learner can join an existing team or start one. See possibilitymanagement.org. Module 11's continuation material shows a course pairing how to become one.
- **Local PM Spark groups and Sparks salons.** Regular in-person or online gatherings where PM is practiced live. Often the most useful next step for a learner who wants community and practice without committing to a full training. The CM maintains a list of currently active Sparks and salons by region as part of the quarterly refresh.

### Reading

- **Conscious Feelings — Clinton Callahan.** The foundational PM text on the emotional body and feelings work. Available through possibilitymanagement.org and major book retailers.
- Other Callahan titles (e.g., *Radiant Joy Brilliant Love*, *Directing the Power of Conscious Feelings*) are referenced in specific module readings; the CM points learners to whichever fits where they want to go next.

### CM note

Some learners look for "what's next" by the end of Day 5. Others wait until after Day 10. Include this section in every packet from the start so a learner who is ready does not have to ask.

# Worked Exemplars Pack

| | |
|---|---|
| **For** | You, the learner, from Module 4 onward, whenever you wonder "is what I'm producing any good?" |
| **What it is** | Five worked models of the course's core practice artifacts, each annotated with *why* it is strong, keyed to the named criteria the modules teach |
| **Reads alongside** | Module 4 (capture discipline) · Module 6 (unmixing) · Module 8 (the exchange shape) · [Daily Practice Spine](../Practice/Daily%20Practice%20Spine.md) · [Experiment Bank](Experiment%20Bank.md) |

The course gives you practice-quality feedback directly at Module 4 and then mostly stops telling you what good looks like. This pack fills that gap. Five artifacts, each done well by an ordinary learner on an ordinary day, with margin notes naming exactly what makes them work.

Two warnings before you read:

1. **These are models, not templates.** Copying their words defeats them. Copy their *shape*: the specificity, the modesty, the discipline of staying with what actually happened.
2. **They are deliberately unimpressive.** No breakthroughs, no tears of release, no transformed marriages. Real practice artifacts are small. If your own pages look smaller than these, you are probably doing fine. If they look much grander, re-read the margin notes.

Numbered markers like **(1)** in each model point to the margin notes underneath it.

## A. A partner exchange: strong, then the same exchange drifting

**The named criteria (Module 8):** timed roles held (speaker speaks, listener holds vacuum) · the three-part scripted reply, in order: *what I heard you say · what I noticed in myself · the question I am leaving with you* · the witness statement is a located feeling with intensity, not a performance · the question is open, with no advice folded inside it · no fixing, no advising, no capping the story with your own.

### A.1 The strong version

*Voice exchange, Module 5. Partner A recorded 6 minutes about noticing anger during a phone call with her mother. Partner B listened all the way through once, then recorded this reply:*

> "Hi. I listened to your message twice, once just to listen. **(1)**
>
> What I heard you say was that the anger showed up in your jaw while your mum was still talking, and that you noticed it and didn't do anything with it yet, and that the noticing itself was new. **(2)**
>
> What I noticed in myself, listening, was some fear, maybe 3 out of 10, in my belly, around the part where you said 'I always go quiet with her.' I notice I go quiet with my brother the same way. I'm not making this about me, just telling you the witness was a real person. **(3)**
>
> The question I'm leaving with you: what is the anger asking you to make a boundary about? You don't owe me an answer. **(4)**
>
> I'm complete. Thanks for sending it." **(5)**

**Margin notes:**

- **(1) Vacuum listening, honored in async form.** One full listen with nothing else running before any response forms. The reply names it, which tells the speaker they were actually received.
- **(2) "What I heard you say" paraphrases the part that landed, not the whole message.** Three specifics (jaw, the not-acting, the newness of noticing) and zero interpretation. Notice what is absent: no "I think what's really going on is…"
- **(3) The witness statement is a feeling, located, with intensity** (fear, belly, 3/10), exactly the Module 5 grammar. The one-line resonance ("my brother") is named *as* the listener's own material and immediately handed back. One line is the limit; two is the start of story-capping.
- **(4) The question is open and carries no advice.** Compare it to its drifted cousin: "have you thought about setting a boundary with her?" Same topic, but that one is advice wearing a question mark. "You don't owe me an answer" keeps the question a gift instead of homework.
- **(5) A clean close.** The exchange is named complete out loud. That is the completion-loop discipline (Module 8) applied to the exchange itself, and it leaves no residue hanging in the pairing.

### A.2 The same exchange, drifting

*Same recording from Partner A. A different reply: friendly, well-meant, and off the rails. The drift moments are flagged.*

> "Hey! Great message. **(D1)** Honestly, I think your mum sounds a lot like mine. When my mother does that thing where she talks over me, what works for me is I just tell her straight up, 'Mum, I need you to hear me right now.' You should try that, seriously, it changed everything for us. **(D2)** Also I wouldn't worry too much about going quiet, that's just a freeze response, it's really common, I read about it. **(D3)** Anyway you're doing amazing, you've totally got this, your mum is lucky to have a daughter who works on herself! **(D4)** Let me know how the next call goes, I want updates!" **(D5)**

**Drift notes:**

- **(D1) Verdict instead of witnessing.** "Great message" grades the speaker. Witnessing reports what happened in the witness; it never scores.
- **(D2) Story-capping plus advice-giving, the double drift.** The listener's mother arrives and takes over the exchange, and "what works for me… you should try that" converts the witness into an advisor. Both are explicitly off-limits in the partner agreement ("I will not advise, fix, or diagnose. I will witness."). Notice the speaker's material is now nowhere in the reply.
- **(D3) Fixing by diagnosis.** "That's just a freeze response" reaches for borrowed clinical vocabulary to make the discomfort go away. Module 6 names this directly: no therapy language you do not own. It also tells the speaker their noticing was nothing, which is the opposite of what happened.
- **(D4) Pep-talk filler.** "You've got this" is not information. The speaker learns nothing about how they were, which was the entire purpose of having a witness.
- **(D5) No close, and a hook.** Instead of naming the exchange complete, it opens an obligation ("I want updates"). The pairing now carries residue.

Drift is not a character flaw. Every one of these moves comes from wanting to help. The practice is catching the want and returning to the witness seat.

## B. A filled Beep! Book page

**The named criteria (Module 4 and the [Daily Practice Spine](../Practice/Daily%20Practice%20Spine.md)):** date-stamped · flat engineer's register, *what I did · what happened* · the Go!/Beep!/Shift! grammar with every Beep! getting a Shift! line · Shifts name one specific change, never "I'll be better" · captures within ten minutes or swept the next morning · no reflection prose, no self-talk · the live experiment restated in its three-part form.

> **Tue 11 Nov** **(1)**
>
> Sweep from yesterday:
> **Go!**: said the budget sentence to R. as planned. Kept it to one sentence. **(2)**
> **Beep!**: checked my phone twice during E.'s story at dinner. Didn't notice till after. *(captured this morning, not in the moment)* **(3)**
> **Shift!**: phone in the kitchen drawer during dinner tonight. **(4)**
>
> Today's experiment. What: when the standup goes over 20 min, I say "I need to drop, I'm at my limit." By when: Fri standup. Noticing: what my shoulders do, whether anyone actually minds. **(5)**
>
> 14:10, **Beep!**: said the standup sentence but laughed while saying it. Sentence landed as a joke. **Shift!**: same sentence, no laugh, feet flat. **(6)**

**Margin notes:**

- **(1) Dated.** The dates are what make the log readable in week four. An undated page is a note; a dated page is data.
- **(2) A Go! is logged too.** The book records what worked, not only what didn't. A book of only Beeps (or only Gos) is a broken instrument.
- **(3) Honest capture metadata.** The learner missed the ten-minute capture window and says so flatly, in the morning sweep, which is the safety net working as designed. No apology paragraph about being bad at capturing.
- **(4) The Shift! is one specific, observable change** (phone, drawer, dinner). Compare "be more present with E." which is a wish, not a Shift.
- **(5) The live experiment in the full three-part form:** what I will do / by when / what I will notice. One line. Specific window. Noticing names body data *and* world data.
- **(6) A same-day capture, timestamped, within ten minutes.** And note what the whole page never does: explain feelings about the entries, draw conclusions about what kind of person this proves the learner is, or write a paragraph. Engineering log, not journal.

## C. A completed experiment capture (including a failed one)

**The named criteria (Module 4 and the [Experiment Bank](Experiment%20Bank.md)):** three-part design before the rep: *what I will do / by when / what I will notice* · one experiment at a time · captured within ten minutes of the rep · the capture reports what happened, not what it means · a failed experiment is a Beep!: design data, never a verdict.

> **Experiment #3** *(designed Sunday, written before running)* **(1)**
> *What I will do:* when Dad starts on my job situation at Sunday lunch, I say one sentence: "I'd rather hear about the allotment," and change the subject once. **(2)**
> *By when:* Sunday lunch, 1–3 pm.
> *What I will notice:* what happens in my chest when I say it; what he does; whether I go quiet for the rest of lunch. **(3)**
>
> *Capture (Sunday 2:40 pm, in the car):* **(4)**
> Did it. Voice came out quieter than planned. He paused, said "fine, fine," moved on, came back to the job thing ten minutes later. I didn't repeat the sentence the second time. Chest: tight before, oddly loose right after saying it. Went about 60% quiet for the rest of lunch, not fully.
>
> **Beep!**: the plan only covered the first attempt. No move prepared for round two. **(5)**
> **Shift!**: next family lunch, same sentence, prepared to say it twice. That's the whole change. **(6)**

**Margin notes:**

- **(1) Designed before the rep, not reconstructed after.** Writing the three parts in advance is what makes it an experiment instead of a story about a lunch.
- **(2) "What I will do" is one specific behavior in one real situation.** A sentence you could film. Not "set better boundaries with Dad."
- **(3) "What I will notice" is decided in advance** and includes body data. You cannot trust noticing that gets chosen after the fact; the Box edits.
- **(4) Captured within ten minutes, in the car.** Flat report: what happened in the body, what the other person did, what the learner actually did, including the part that didn't go to plan. No analysis of what it all means about the relationship.
- **(5) The failure is written as a Beep!: a precise design gap** ("no move prepared for round two"), not a verdict ("I'm spineless, I caved like always"). Same event, two write-ups, completely different futures. The Beep version produces experiment #4; the verdict version produces the Beep Swamp.
- **(6) The Shift! changes exactly one variable.** Not a new experiment, not a personality overhaul. The rep compounds because the design improves one notch per turn.

## D. An unmixing restatement (Module 6), as a transcript

**The named criteria (Module 6, the Unmixing Restatement micro-practice):** start from the everyday word ("stressed," "frustrated," "overwhelmed") · restate using **only the four feelings** (anger, sadness, fear, joy) · each component gets a body location and an intensity (0–10) · each component gets its asked-for action: anger says make a boundary, sadness says let this go, fear says prepare, joy says more of this · small material only · naming is the whole practice: stop before processing.

*A learner, alone, notebook open, speaking out loud. Transcribed as spoken, hesitations included.*

> "Okay. The normal sentence is: I'm stressed about my sister visiting next week. **(1)**
>
> Restating. I have some… fear, and some anger, mixed together about the visit. **(2)** Hm. And a little joy, actually, which is confusing, but it's there. **(3)**
>
> The fear is in my stomach, maybe a 4. The anger is lower than I expected, jaw, about a 2. The joy is in my chest, a 2, maybe a 3 when I think about her actually being here. **(4)**
>
> What are they asking. The fear says prepare, so: the fear is about the sleeping arrangements conversation, I could just have that conversation before she arrives. The anger says make a boundary, probably about the length of the visit. I notice I don't want to do that one. Noted. **(5)** The joy says more of this, which I think means: plan the one walk we always take, instead of leaving it to chance.
>
> Reading it back: I have some fear at 4, some anger at 2, some joy at 2, about my sister's visit. That is… considerably more workable than 'stressed.' **(6)**
>
> Stopping there." **(7)**

**Margin notes:**

- **(1) Starts with the real everyday sentence, unimproved.** The practice needs the actual vocabulary you actually use, because that vocabulary is where the mixing hides.
- **(2) Only the four.** "Stressed" is not on the New Map of Feelings; it is a story about a mix. The restatement form is exactly the Module 6 sentence: *I have some [feeling] and some [feeling] mixed together about [trigger].*
- **(3) The surprise component is allowed in.** Joy inside a "stress" is common and usually the component the old wording was hiding. The learner doesn't argue with it; "it's there" is the whole verification needed.
- **(4) Location and number for each component.** And an honest reading against expectation ("lower than I expected"). The first reading is the reading; no justifying the numbers.
- **(5) Asked-for actions, with honesty about reluctance.** "I notice I don't want to do that one. Noted." No self-scolding, no forcing a commitment. Naming the reluctance *is* data; the practice doesn't require acting today.
- **(6) The payoff is precision, not relief.** Nothing got healed in this transcript. A vague weather system became three named, located, sized components. That is the entire job of this practice.
- **(7) Stops at naming.** Moving the components is EHP work with its own container and, beyond small material, a witness. Knowing where this practice ends is part of doing it well.

## E. A Close-the-loop self-check, filled honestly

**The named criteria:** the three-word scale used as defined, *not yet / starting / landed in my body* ([Learning Self-Assessment](Learning%20Self-Assessment.md)) · "landed" means findable in your body more or less on purpose, not "I can explain it" · honesty leans toward "not yet" · a "not yet" is information with a next move attached, never a verdict.

*A learner's five-minute self-check at the end of Module 5, written in the back of the Beep! Book.*

> Close the loop, Module 5.
>
> Tell a feeling from an emotion in a live moment: **starting.** I caught one clean (the irritation at the printer, present-time, gone in two minutes). I missed the one with my flatmate till the next day. **(1)**
>
> Locate a feeling at low intensity, 2–5%: **starting.** Worked with objects in the practice. Hasn't happened yet out in the day without the setup. **(2)**
>
> Notice when I've hit my Numbness Bar: **not yet.** Honestly I can find numbness afterwards, looking back, but I have never once caught it while it's happening. **(3)**
>
> Next move on the "not yet": re-run the object practice tomorrow morning before Module 6, and tell J. in our next exchange that bar-noticing is the edge I'm on. If it's still "not yet" after that, I stretch the cadence a day. The form says Module 6 stands on Module 5, so this is exactly the thing to be slow about. **(4)**
>
> Biggest mover this module: I have stopped saying "I'm fine" to myself as if it were a feeling. **(5)**

**Margin notes:**

- **(1) "Starting" backed by evidence in both directions.** One catch, one miss, both named. The scale words only mean something when they are attached to actual moments.
- **(2) Honest about the scaffolding.** Works-in-the-practice but not-yet-in-life is precisely what "starting" is for. Claiming "landed" here would be the intellectual-swallowing trap the scale exists to catch: explaining a distinction is not having it.
- **(3) The "not yet," stated flat, with the precise shape of the gap.** Not "I'm bad at this." The learner can find numbness in retrospect but not live; that one sentence tells them (and would tell a CM, in aggregate) exactly what to practice next.
- **(4) The "not yet" is treated as information: it produces a plan, not a mood.** Revisit the practice, take the edge to the partner, stretch the cadence if needed. Those are the course's own three moves for a readiness gap, chosen calmly, because a "not yet" caught *before* Module 6 is the instrument working.
- **(5) The delta is modest and real.** Not "I have transformed my relationship to my emotions." One specific default sentence, retired. That is what installed thoughtware actually looks like at week two.

## F. Community posts: a strong stake, its witnesses, a loop-close, a Beep, then a drifting stake

**The named criteria ([Community Message Templates](Community%20Message%20Templates.md) and the Staking choreography):** a stake is the Day-4 experiment format made public — *what I will do / by when / what I will notice* · the action is one specific behavior you could film, the by-when is a real window · witnessed-by is a named human hook, not advice · the loop-close reports what happened flat, with one Shift · a Beep is a failed experiment posted as design data, never a verdict · visibility is the whole mechanism, and nothing here is High material.

### F.1 The strong stake

*Posted to the Stake Board by a learner on Module 4, before running the experiment.*

> **STAKE** **(1)**
> What I will do: when the team standup runs past twenty minutes, I say one sentence: "I need to drop, I'm at my limit for this one." **(2)**
> By when: Thursday's standup. **(3)**
> What I will notice: what my shoulders do while I say it, and whether anyone actually minds. **(4)**
> Module 04, self-paced. Witnesses welcome.

Two replies followed:

> "Witnessed 🕯" **(5)**
>
> "Witnessed 🕯 — I'm on the same edge with meetings, glad to hear how it lands." **(6)**

**Margin notes:**

- **(1) The stake is the Day-4 format, unchanged.** It is not a new genre. It is the experiment artifact the course already teaches, posted before the rep instead of captured after it. That is the entire move: declaration made public.
- **(2) One filmable behavior.** A specific sentence, in a specific recurring situation. Compare the drift below: "be better about boundaries at work" is not a stake, because nobody could witness it happening.
- **(3) A real by-when.** A named day, this week. A stake with no by-when is a wish; the deadline is what makes it stake-able.
- **(4) Noticing decided in advance, body data and world data.** Shoulders (body) and whether anyone minds (world). Chosen before the rep, because noticing picked afterward is the Box editing.
- **(5) The bare witness reply is complete.** "Witnessed 🕯" is the whole convention. The staker now has a named human to report back to. No advice attached.
- **(6) One line of resonance, no advice.** "Same edge, glad to hear how it lands" names the witness as a real person and adds zero technique, zero "have you tried." Two witnesses is the cap; this stake now has its two.

### F.2 The strong loop-close

*The same learner, replying to their own stake two days later.*

> **LOOP CLOSED** **(1)**
> What happened: said the sentence. Voice came out flatter than I meant, but it came out. Standup actually ended; one person said "yeah, good call." Then it drifted back for two minutes and I didn't re-say it. **(2)**
> What I noticed: shoulders up around my ears before, dropped right after the sentence. Nobody minded. The minding was all mine. **(3)**
> Shift: next standup, same sentence, and I stay on mute-off until it's actually closed. **(4)**

**Margin notes:**

- **(1) The close is a reply to the stake, not a new post.** The loop closes where it opened, so the witnesses see the report-back in one thread. That is the completion-loop discipline applied to the commons.
- **(2) Flat report, including what didn't go to plan.** The drift-back and the not-re-saying are in there, unvarnished. No spin, no "but overall I crushed it."
- **(3) Body and world, separated and specific.** Shoulders located and tracked across the moment; the realization ("the minding was all mine") stated as a noticing, not a life-lesson.
- **(4) One Shift, one variable.** Same sentence, one added move. Not a personality overhaul. The rep compounds because the design improves one notch.

### F.3 The strong Beep

*A different learner, Module 4, posting a failed experiment proudly.*

> **BEEP!** (this one didn't land, posting it as data) **(1)**
> The experiment: say the boundary sentence to my dad at Sunday lunch. **(2)**
> What happened: never said it. The moment came, my mouth opened, and I changed the subject instead. **(3)**
> The Beep: I had no plan for the half-second of dread right before speaking. The experiment just assumed I'd say it. **(4)**
> Shift: next time, hand flat on the table as the physical cue to speak. One trigger, prepared in advance. **(5)**

**Margin notes:**

- **(1) Posted as data, not confession.** The header itself frames the failure correctly: a Beep is design information. The room answers a Beep with "🕯" or "good data," never with advice. Failed experiments are Beeps, and Beeps are exactly what the board is for.
- **(2) The original stake, restated in one line** so the Beep is readable on its own.
- **(3) What actually happened, flat.** No story about being weak, no history of every other time. Just the event.
- **(4) The Beep is a precise design gap,** not a verdict. "No plan for the dread" is a fixable specification. Compare the verdict version ("I always cave, I'm spineless"), which produces nothing but the Beep Swamp.
- **(5) The Shift changes one variable** and adds a concrete physical trigger. That is a real next experiment, not a resolution to "try harder."

### F.4 A drifting stake

*Posted to the Stake Board, well-meant and off the shape. The drift moments are flagged.*

> **STAKE** I'm finally committing to doing the deep work and really showing up for myself this season. **(D1)** I'm going to work on being more present and setting better boundaries in all my relationships, **(D2)** and honestly process some heavy stuff that came up about my mother that I've been carrying for years. **(D3)** This is a big one for me, I can feel it's going to be transformative. Sending love to everyone on this journey, we've got this!! 🙏✨ **(D4)**

**Drift notes:**

- **(D1) A declaration of intent, not a stake.** "Committing to showing up for myself this season" has no action and no by-when. It is a mood with a deadline of "someday." The fix is a single filmable behavior and a real date.
- **(D2) Vague and plural.** "More present," "better boundaries," "all my relationships" — nothing here could be witnessed happening. A stake is one behavior, one situation, once. The Box loves the plural because the plural can never be checked.
- **(D3) High material in the commons.** "Process some heavy stuff about my mother" is exactly the work that is live, witnessed, and in a pod — never declared as a public stake and never processed over text. A Space Holder redirects this gently to the Witness Board and the pod (the redirect script is in the [Telegram Community Operator Guide](Telegram%20Community%20Operator%20Guide.md) §8). This is the line the whole model protects.
- **(D4) Performance, not practice.** "Transformative," "sending love," "we've got this," the emoji garland — the post is written for applause, which is the wrong attractor. Visibility is the mechanism so you actually run the rep, not so the room cheers the announcement. The fix is smaller, plainer, and by Thursday.

Every drift here comes from sincerity, the same way the partner-exchange drift in section A does. The practice is the same too: catch the want-to-be-seen-as-doing-the-work, and come back to one small thing you will actually do, by when, and what you will notice.

## How to use this pack on your own work

Once per module, hold one artifact of yours next to the matching exemplar and ask three questions: *Is mine specific where theirs is specific? Is mine modest where theirs is modest? Did mine stop where the practice ends?* Three yeses, carry on. Any no: the margin note next to the gap names the criterion to practice. That check is feedback you can give yourself, which is the point; the course will not always be there to do it for you.

    If you going to be a car mechanic, there is a broken car and you need to fix it, how are you going to do that? It is an exact metaphor at the beginning to be a Lab Trainer. In order to fix the car, you need to know how fully functional cars work, the multitude of ways that a car can break down, a variety of ways to repair the car back to full functionality, plus whatever tools and parts are needed for making the repairs. The same is true for people.
     

    What kind of tests you can apply to find what is going on?

    When a car overheats, what are the tests to find what cause overeating?

    1. There is a leak in the water cooling system, there is no water in the radiator.

    2. There is a head gasket leak, the oil from the engine is going in the water of the engine. There is two liquid for the engine. If the oil goes in the water.

    3. Either the oil goes in the water, and you open the radiator cap and stick your finger in there if the water is not too hot. If the water has spots of oil in it, you have oil leaking into the water.

    If the water is clear, you pull out the oil dip stick, when the engine is hot. You drip some of the oil on the hot exhaust manifold and if the oil boils it has water in it. But if it just smokes then it is pure oil.

    4. There is a thermal valve that permits the cool water to go into the engine or blocks from going into the engine. The thermal valve could be stuck or broken and not letting cool water to go into the engine when it is needed. Take out the thermal valve in the radiator cap, and the water will go all the time in the engine. It will not overheat. Put in a new thermal valve. It costs 4.99$ at the car part store.
